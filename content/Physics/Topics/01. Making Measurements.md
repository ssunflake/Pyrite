## Measuring Length

### Rulers
- Measure to nearest **1 mm**
- Avoid **parallax error** — read at eye level directly above the scale

### Vernier Calipers
- Measure to nearest **0.1 mm**
- Used for widths of objects, internal/external diameters
- Read main scale + vernier scale

### Micrometer Screw Gauge
- Measure to nearest **0.01 mm**
- Used for wire diameters, small thicknesses
- Check for **zero error** before use

---

## Measuring Time

- **Stopwatch** — measures time intervals
- For pendulum experiments, time **multiple oscillations** and divide to reduce random error
- **Period (T)** = time for one complete oscillation

---

## Measuring Volume

- **Regular shapes:** use formula (e.g. $V = l \times w \times h$ for a cuboid)
- **Irregular solids:** use displacement method
  - Submerge in measuring cylinder
  - Volume of solid = rise in water level

## Errors and Accuracy

### Types of Error
| Error | Description | How to reduce |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Random error** | Unpredictable variation in readings | Take multiple readings and average |
| **Systematic error** | Consistent offset in one direction | Calibrate instrument; check for zero error |
| **Zero error** | Instrument reads non-zero when it should read zero | Subtract the zero error from all readings |

### Accuracy vs Precision
- **Accurate** — close to the true value
- **Precise** — repeated readings close to each other (but not necessarily true value)
- You want readings to be both **accurate AND precise**

### Significant Figures
- Express answers to the same number of significant figures as the least precise measurement
- e.g. $3.2 \times 4.56 = 14.592 \approx 15$ (2 s.f.)

## Scalars and Vectors

| Scalar | Vector |
|--------|--------|
| Has magnitude only | Has magnitude AND direction |
| Speed, distance, mass, time, energy, temperature | Velocity, displacement, force, acceleration, momentum |

### Adding Vectors
- **Same direction:** add magnitudes
- **Opposite direction:** subtract magnitudes
- **At an angle:** use scale drawing or Pythagoras' theorem

For two perpendicular vectors:
$$R = \sqrt{A^2 + B^2}$$

---

## Key Skills
- Always record the **unit** with every measurement
- Use an appropriate number of **significant figures**
- Identify and correct for **zero errors**
- Reduce random error by **repeating measurements**
