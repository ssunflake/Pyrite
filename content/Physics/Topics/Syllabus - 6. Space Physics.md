## 6.1 Earth & the Solar System

### The Solar System

| Object | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Sun | Star at the centre; mostly hydrogen & helium |
| Planets | 8 planets orbiting the Sun |
| Moons | Natural satellites orbiting planets |
| Asteroids | Rocky bodies, mostly in asteroid belt |
| Comets | Icy bodies with elongated orbits |
| Dwarf planets | e.g. Pluto, Eris |

### The Eight Planets (in order)
1. Mercury
2. Venus
3. Earth
4. Mars
5. *(Asteroid Belt)*
6. Jupiter
7. Saturn
8. Uranus
9. Neptune

> **Mnemonic:** My Very Excellent Mother Just Served Us Nachos

### Orbits
- Planets orbit the Sun in **elliptical** paths (nearly circular)
- Moons orbit planets
- Gravity provides the **centripetal force** for orbits
- Closer planets → shorter orbital period, faster orbital speed

### Gravity & Orbits
$$F = \frac{Gm_1m_2}{r^2}$$

- Gravity decreases with distance squared (**inverse square law**)
- For circular orbit: gravitational force = centripetal force

### Satellites
| Type | Orbit | Use |
|------|-------|-----|
| **Geostationary** | 36,000 km above equator, 24h period | TV, communications |
| **Low Earth Orbit (LEO)** | 200–2000 km, ~90 min period | Weather, GPS, ISS |

**Geostationary orbit:** always stays above same point on Earth's surface

## 6.2 Stars & the Universe

### The Sun as a Star
- Our Sun is a **medium-sized, main sequence** star
- Age: ~4.6 billion years
- Core temperature: ~15 million °C
- Energy source: **nuclear fusion** (hydrogen → helium)

### The Life Cycle of Stars

#### Stars of similar mass to the Sun:
```
Nebula → Protostar → Main sequence star → Red giant → 
White dwarf → Black dwarf (eventually)
```

#### Massive stars:
```
Nebula → Protostar → Main sequence star → Red supergiant → 
Supernova → Neutron star OR Black hole
```

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Nebula** | Cloud of gas and dust |
| **Protostar** | Gravity pulls nebula together; heats up |
| **Main sequence** | Stable stage; fusion of H → He balances gravity |
| **Red giant/supergiant** | H runs out; outer layers expand; He fusion begins |
| **Supernova** | Massive star explodes violently |
| **White dwarf** | Remnant of medium star; Earth-sized, very hot |
| **Neutron star** | Ultra-dense remnant; mostly neutrons |
| **Black hole** | Gravity so strong not even light can escape |

### The Universe

**Scale of the Universe:**
- Moon → Earth → Solar System → Galaxy (Milky Way) → Universe

| Object | Scale |
|--------|-------|
| Earth | ~12,700 km diameter |
| Solar System | ~9 billion km across |
| Milky Way galaxy | ~100,000 light-years across |
| Observable Universe | ~93 billion light-years across |

### Light Year
- Distance light travels in one year
- 1 light-year ≈ 9.46 × 10¹⁵ m
- Used to measure astronomical distances

### The Big Bang Theory

> The universe began ~13.8 billion years ago from an extremely hot, dense point and has been **expanding** ever since.

**Evidence for the Big Bang:**

| Evidence | Explanation |
|----------|-------------|
| **Red-shift** | Light from distant galaxies is shifted to longer wavelengths → galaxies moving away |
| **Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)** | Faint radiation detected from all directions → remnant heat from Big Bang |
| **Hubble's Law** | More distant galaxies recede faster — universe is expanding |

### Red-Shift
- When a source of light moves **away** from us, wavelengths appear **longer** (shifted toward red)
- Called the **Doppler effect** for light
- More distant galaxies have greater red-shift → expanding universe

$$z = \frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda} \approx \frac{v}{c}$$

### Hubble's Law
$$v = H_0 d$$

- $v$ = recession speed of galaxy
- $H_0$ = Hubble constant (~70 km/s/Mpc)
- $d$ = distance to galaxy

> The further away a galaxy, the faster it is moving away from us.

### Dark Matter & Dark Energy
- **Dark matter:** Invisible matter that explains why galaxies rotate faster than expected
- **Dark energy:** Unknown force causing the expansion of the universe to **accelerate**
- Together, they make up ~95% of the universe
