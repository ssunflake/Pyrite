## 1.1 Length & Time

### Key Units
| Quantity | SI Unit | Symbol |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Length | metre | m |
| Time | second | s |
| Mass | kilogram | kg |

### Measuring Length
- Use a **ruler** for straight lengths (mm precision)
- Use **vernier calipers** for small lengths (0.1mm precision)
- Use a **micrometer** for very small lengths (0.01mm precision)

### Measuring Time
- Use a **stopwatch** for short intervals
- Use a **fiducial mark** when timing pendulum oscillations
- **Period (T)** = time for one complete oscillation

### Errors
- **Random error** — unpredictable variation, reduced by averaging multiple readings
- **Systematic error** — consistent offset (e.g. zero error), fixed by calibration

## 1.2 Motion

### Key Definitions
- **Distance** — total path length (scalar)
- **Displacement** — straight-line distance from start to end (vector)
- **Speed** — rate of change of distance (scalar)
- **Velocity** — rate of change of displacement (vector)
- **Acceleration** — rate of change of velocity

### Equations of Motion
$$v = \frac{d}{t}$$
$$a = \frac{v - u}{t}$$
$$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$
$$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$

Where:
- $u$ = initial velocity (m/s)
- $v$ = final velocity (m/s)
- $a$ = acceleration (m/s²)
- $s$ = displacement (m)
- $t$ = time (s)

### Distance-Time Graphs
| Shape of Graph | Meaning |
|----------------|---------|
| Horizontal line | Stationary |
| Straight diagonal | Constant speed |
| Curved (steepening) | Accelerating |
| Curved (flattening) | Decelerating |

> 💡 **Gradient of d-t graph = speed**

### Velocity-Time Graphs
| Shape of Graph | Meaning |
|----------------|---------|
| Horizontal line | Constant velocity |
| Straight diagonal up | Constant acceleration |
| Straight diagonal down | Constant deceleration |

> 💡 **Gradient of v-t graph = acceleration**
> 💡 **Area under v-t graph = displacement**

### Free Fall
- Objects in free fall accelerate at **g = 10 m/s²** (9.8 m/s² more precisely)
- Air resistance eventually causes **terminal velocity** when drag = weight

## 1.3 Mass & Weight

| Quantity | Definition | Unit |
|----------|-----------|------|
| Mass | Amount of matter in an object | kg |
| Weight | Gravitational force on an object | N |

$$W = mg$$

Where $g = 10$ N/kg on Earth's surface.

- Mass is constant everywhere
- Weight changes with gravitational field strength
- Measured with a **balance** (mass) or **spring balance/newton meter** (weight)

## 1.4 Density

$$\rho = \frac{m}{V}$$

- $\rho$ = density (kg/m³)
- $m$ = mass (kg)
- $V$ = volume (m³)

### Measuring Density
- **Regular solids:** measure dimensions, calculate volume, use formula
- **Irregular solids:** use displacement method (eureka can or measuring cylinder)
- **Liquids:** measure mass of known volume using measuring cylinder

| Material | Approx. Density |
|----------|----------------|
| Water | 1000 kg/m³ |
| Air | 1.2 kg/m³ |
| Aluminium | 2700 kg/m³ |
| Iron/Steel | 7800 kg/m³ |

> 💡 Objects float if their density < density of the fluid

## 1.5 Forces

### Types of Forces
- **Gravitational** — attraction between masses
- **Normal contact** — perpendicular to surface
- **Friction** — opposes motion
- **Tension** — through stretched objects
- **Air resistance / drag** — fluid resistance

### Newton's Laws
1. **1st Law:** An object stays at rest or constant velocity unless a resultant force acts on it
2. **2nd Law:** $F = ma$ (resultant force = mass × acceleration)
3. **3rd Law:** Every action has an equal and opposite reaction

$$F = ma$$

### Resultant Forces
- Forces are **vectors** — direction matters
- Resultant force = vector sum of all forces
- If resultant = 0 → object is in **equilibrium**

### Friction
- **Static friction** — prevents stationary objects from moving
- **Dynamic friction** — opposes motion of moving objects
- Friction always acts **opposite to motion**

### Hooke's Law
$$F = ke$$

- $F$ = force applied (N)
- $k$ = spring constant (N/m)
- $e$ = extension (m)
- Valid only up to the **limit of proportionality**

### Turning Effect (Moments)
$$\text{Moment} = F \times d$$

- $F$ = force (N)
- $d$ = perpendicular distance from pivot (m)
- Unit: Nm

**Principle of Moments (equilibrium):**
$$\sum \text{clockwise moments} = \sum \text{anticlockwise moments}$$

### Centre of Gravity
- Point where the entire weight appears to act
- Object is stable if vertical line through CoG falls within the base

## 1.6 Momentum

$$p = mv$$

- $p$ = momentum (kg m/s)
- $m$ = mass (kg)
- $v$ = velocity (m/s)

### Conservation of Momentum
> In a closed system, **total momentum before = total momentum after** any collision or explosion

$$m_1u_1 + m_2u_2 = m_1v_1 + m_2v_2$$

### Impulse
$$\text{Impulse} = F \times t = \Delta p$$

- Larger time → smaller force for same change in momentum
- e.g. airbags, crumple zones, catching a ball

## 1.7 Energy, Work & Power

### Forms of Energy
- Kinetic, Gravitational potential, Chemical, Thermal, Nuclear, Electrical, Light, Sound

### Conservation of Energy
> Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred between forms.

### Work
$$W = Fd\cos\theta$$

For horizontal forces:
$$W = Fd$$

- $W$ = work done (J)
- $F$ = force (N)
- $d$ = distance moved in direction of force (m)

### Kinetic Energy
$$E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

### Gravitational Potential Energy
$$E_p = mgh$$

### Power
$$P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{E}{t}$$

- $P$ = power (W)
- $W$ = work done (J)
- $t$ = time (s)

### Efficiency
$$\text{Efficiency} = \frac{\text{useful energy output}}{\text{total energy input}} \times 100\%$$

> Efficiency is always < 100% due to energy losses (usually as heat/sound)

---

## 1.8 Pressure

$$P = \frac{F}{A}$$

- $P$ = pressure (Pa or N/m²)
- $F$ = force (N)
- $A$ = area (m²)

### Pressure in Fluids
$$P = h\rho g$$

- $h$ = depth (m)
- $\rho$ = density of fluid (kg/m³)
- $g$ = gravitational field strength (N/kg)

> Pressure increases with depth and acts in all directions equally.

### Atmospheric Pressure
- ~101,000 Pa at sea level
- Decreases with altitude
- Measured with a **barometer**
