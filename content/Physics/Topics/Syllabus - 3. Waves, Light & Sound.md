## 3.1 General Wave Properties

### What is a Wave?
A wave is a **transfer of energy** without the transfer of matter.

### Types of Waves

| Type | Particle movement | Examples |
|------|------------------|---------|
| **Transverse** | Perpendicular to wave direction | Light, water waves, EM waves |
| **Longitudinal** | Parallel to wave direction | Sound, seismic P-waves |

### Key Wave Terms

| Term | Definition | Unit |
|------|-----------|------|
| Amplitude (A) | Max displacement from equilibrium | m |
| Wavelength (λ) | Distance of one complete wave | m |
| Frequency (f) | Number of waves per second | Hz |
| Period (T) | Time for one complete wave | s |
| Wave speed (v) | Distance travelled per second | m/s |

### Wave Equations
$$v = f\lambda$$
$$T = \frac{1}{f}$$

### Wave Diagrams
- **Crest** = highest point
- **Trough** = lowest point
- Wavelength = distance from crest to crest (or trough to trough)

### Reflection, Refraction & Diffraction

| Phenomenon | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Reflection** | Wave bounces off a surface |
| **Refraction** | Wave changes speed/direction at a boundary |
| **Diffraction** | Wave spreads around edges/through gaps |

> Maximum diffraction occurs when gap size ≈ wavelength

## 3.2 Light & Reflection

### Reflection of Light
- **Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection** (measured from normal)
- Normal = line perpendicular to the surface at the point of incidence

### Plane Mirrors — Image Properties
- Same size as object
- Same distance behind mirror as object is in front
- **Virtual** (cannot be projected onto a screen)
- **Laterally inverted**

### Curved Mirrors
| Type | Effect | Use |
|------|--------|-----|
| Concave | Converges light | Make-up mirrors, torches, telescopes |
| Convex | Diverges light (wider field) | Car rear-view mirrors, security mirrors |

## 3.3 Refraction & Lenses

### Refraction
- Light bends when passing from one medium to another
- Caused by a **change in speed**

| Transition | Bending direction |
|-----------|------------------|
| Less dense → More dense (e.g. air → glass) | Bends **toward** normal |
| More dense → Less dense (e.g. glass → air) | Bends **away** from normal |

### Snell's Law
$$n = \frac{\sin i}{\sin r} = \frac{v_1}{v_2}$$

- $n$ = refractive index
- $i$ = angle of incidence
- $r$ = angle of refraction

### Total Internal Reflection (TIR)
- Occurs when light travels from **dense to less dense** medium
- Angle of incidence > **critical angle**

$$\sin C = \frac{1}{n}$$

**Applications:** Optical fibres, periscopes with prisms, diamond sparkle

### Lenses

| Type | Effect | Symbol |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Converging (convex)** | Refracts light to a focal point | Thicker in middle |
| **Diverging (concave)** | Spreads light outward | Thinner in middle |

**Key terms:**
- **Principal focus (F)** — where parallel rays converge after the lens
- **Focal length (f)** — distance from lens to principal focus

**Converging lens image rules:**
| Object position | Image type |
|----------------|-----------|
| Beyond 2F | Real, inverted, smaller |
| At 2F | Real, inverted, same size |
| Between F and 2F | Real, inverted, larger |
| At F | No image (parallel rays) |
| Inside F | Virtual, upright, larger |

**Uses of lenses:**
- Camera (converging, real image)
- Magnifying glass (converging, virtual image)
- Spectacles (converging for long sight, diverging for short sight)

## 3.4 Electromagnetic Spectrum

All EM waves:
- Travel at **3 × 10⁸ m/s** in a vacuum (speed of light)
- Are **transverse** waves
- Transfer energy
- Do NOT need a medium

### The EM Spectrum (low f → high f)

| Type | Wavelength | Frequency | Uses |
|------|-----------|-----------|------|
| Radio waves | ~1m–1km | Lowest | TV, radio |
| Microwaves | ~1mm–1m | ↑ | Cooking, satellites, WiFi |
| Infrared | ~700nm–1mm | ↑ | Heating, remote controls, thermal imaging |
| Visible light | ~400–700nm | ↑ | Sight, photography |
| Ultraviolet | ~10–400nm | ↑ | Sterilisation, sunbeds |
| X-rays | ~0.01–10nm | ↑ | Medical imaging, security scanners |
| Gamma rays | <0.01nm | Highest | Cancer treatment, sterilising equipment |

> As frequency increases → wavelength decreases (since v = fλ and v is constant)

**Dangers:**
- UV → sunburn, skin cancer
- X-rays → cell damage, cancer
- Gamma → most ionising, most dangerous

## 3.5 Sound

### Properties of Sound
- **Longitudinal** wave
- Requires a **medium** to travel (cannot travel through vacuum)
- Travels as **compressions and rarefactions**

### Speed of Sound
| Medium | Approximate speed |
|--------|------------------|
| Air (20°C) | 340 m/s |
| Water | 1500 m/s |
| Steel | 5000 m/s |

> Sound travels **faster** in denser/stiffer materials (solids > liquids > gases)

### Frequency & Pitch
- Higher frequency → higher pitch
- Human hearing range: **20 Hz – 20,000 Hz**
- **Ultrasound:** > 20,000 Hz (above human hearing)

### Amplitude & Loudness
- Higher amplitude → louder sound
- Measured in **decibels (dB)**

### Echo & Reflection of Sound
- Sound reflects off hard surfaces → **echo**
- Used in **SONAR** (Sound Navigation and Ranging)

$$d = \frac{v \times t}{2}$$

### Ultrasound Applications
- Medical scanning (safe, non-ionising)
- Sonar (depth measurement, submarine detection)
- Industrial flaw detection
- Cleaning delicate equipment
