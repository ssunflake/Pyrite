## 2.1 Kinetic Molecular Model of Matter

### The Three States of Matter

| Property | Solid | Liquid | Gas |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Shape | Fixed | Takes container shape | Fills container |
| Volume | Fixed | Fixed | Fills container |
| Particle arrangement | Regular, close | Random, close | Random, far apart |
| Particle movement | Vibrate about fixed positions | Slide past each other | Move freely & rapidly |
| Forces between particles | Strong | Moderate | Very weak |
| Compressibility | Incompressible | Incompressible | Highly compressible |

### Changes of State

```
Solid ←—— freezing ——→ Solid
Solid ←—— melting ———→ Liquid
Liquid ←—— evaporation/boiling ——→ Gas
Gas ←—— condensation ——→ Liquid
Solid ←—— sublimation ——→ Gas
```

- During a change of state, **temperature stays constant**
- Energy is used to break/form bonds, not increase temperature

### Brownian Motion
- Random motion of particles visible in smoke/pollen in water
- **Evidence** for the kinetic molecular model
- Caused by unequal bombardment by invisible molecules

## 2.2 Thermal Properties & Temperature

### Temperature Scales
- **Celsius (°C)** — 0°C = melting point of ice, 100°C = boiling point of water
- **Kelvin (K)** — absolute scale
$$T(K) = T(°C) + 273$$

- **Absolute zero** = 0 K = −273°C — particles have minimum energy, no motion

### Thermometers
| Type | Thermometric Property |
|------|----------------------|
| Liquid-in-glass | Expansion of liquid |
| Thermocouple | Voltage difference |
| Resistance thermometer | Electrical resistance change |

### Specific Heat Capacity
$$Q = mc\Delta T$$

- $Q$ = heat energy (J)
- $m$ = mass (kg)
- $c$ = specific heat capacity (J/kg°C)
- $\Delta T$ = temperature change (°C or K)

| Material | SHC (J/kg°C) |
|----------|-------------|
| Water | 4200 |
| Aluminium | 900 |
| Copper | 390 |
| Iron | 500 |

> Water has a very high SHC — good for cooling systems and climate regulation.

### Specific Latent Heat
$$Q = mL$$

- $Q$ = heat energy (J)
- $m$ = mass (kg)
- $L$ = specific latent heat (J/kg)

| Type | Change of State |
|------|----------------|
| Latent heat of **fusion** | Solid ↔ Liquid |
| Latent heat of **vaporisation** | Liquid ↔ Gas |

> During melting/boiling, temperature stays **constant** even though energy is being added.

### Evaporation vs Boiling

| Evaporation | Boiling |
|-------------|---------|
| Occurs at any temperature | Occurs at boiling point only |
| Occurs at surface only | Occurs throughout liquid |
| Causes cooling | Requires continuous heat supply |

**Factors affecting rate of evaporation:**
- Temperature (higher → faster)
- Surface area (larger → faster)
- Humidity (lower → faster)
- Air movement/wind (more → faster)

## 2.3 Thermal Processes

### Conduction
- Transfer of thermal energy through a material **without the material moving**
- Mainly in **solids**
- Mechanism: vibrating particles pass energy to neighbours; free electrons in metals also carry energy

| Good conductors | Poor conductors (Insulators) |
|----------------|------------------------------|
| Metals (copper, aluminium) | Wood, plastic, glass, air |

> Metals are good conductors because of **free electrons**.

### Convection
- Transfer of thermal energy by the **movement of a fluid** (liquid or gas)
- Only in **fluids** (liquids and gases)
- Mechanism: hot fluid becomes less dense → rises → cold fluid sinks → circular convection current

**Examples:**
- Heating water in a beaker
- Sea breezes (land/sea heat differently)
- Radiators (despite the name, use convection)

### Radiation
- Transfer of thermal energy as **electromagnetic waves** (infrared radiation)
- Does **NOT** need a medium — works through a vacuum
- All objects emit and absorb radiation

**Factors affecting radiation:**
| Factor | Better emitter/absorber |
|--------|------------------------|
| Colour | Dark/matt surfaces |
| Texture | Rough surfaces |
| Temperature | Higher temperature → more radiation |

| Surface | Emission | Absorption |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Black/matt | Best | Best |
| White/shiny | Worst | Worst |

**Applications:**
- Vacuum flask (silvered walls reduce radiation, vacuum prevents conduction/convection)
- Solar panels (black surface absorbs radiation)
- Survival blankets (shiny to reflect radiation)

### Comparing the Three Methods

| | Conduction | Convection | Radiation |
|--|-----------|------------|-----------|
| Medium needed? | Yes (solid) | Yes (fluid) | No |
| Particles move? | No | Yes | No |
| Best in | Solids | Fluids | Vacuum |
