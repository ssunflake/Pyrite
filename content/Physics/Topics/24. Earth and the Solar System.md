## The Solar System

| Object | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Sun** | Central star; provides heat and light; 99.8% of solar system mass |
| **Planets** | 8 large bodies orbiting the Sun |
| **Moons** | Natural satellites orbiting planets |
| **Asteroids** | Rocky bodies; mostly in the asteroid belt (between Mars and Jupiter) |
| **Comets** | Icy bodies with highly elliptical orbits; develop a tail near Sun |
| **Dwarf planets** | e.g. Pluto, Eris — smaller than planets, orbit Sun |
| **Meteors/Meteorites** | Rock fragments that burn up in atmosphere (meteor = shooting star); if they reach ground = meteorite |

## Orbits

### Planetary Orbits
- Planets orbit the Sun in **elliptical** paths (nearly circular)
- Closer to Sun → shorter orbital period → faster orbital speed
- Gravity provides the **centripetal force** for orbits

### Orbital Period and Distance (Kepler's Third Law)
$$T^2 \propto r^3$$

- $T$ = orbital period
- $r$ = mean orbital radius (distance from Sun)

| Planet | Orbital period | Distance from Sun |
|--------|---------------|------------------|
| Mercury | 88 days | 0.39 AU |
| Earth | 365 days | 1 AU |
| Jupiter | 12 years | 5.2 AU |
| Neptune | 165 years | 30 AU |

(1 AU = 1 Astronomical Unit = average Earth–Sun distance ≈ 150 million km)

### Gravitational Force and Orbits

$$F = \frac{Gm_1m_2}{r^2}$$

- Gravity decreases with distance squared (**inverse square law**)
- For circular orbit: gravitational force = centripetal force

## The Moon

- Earth's only natural satellite
- Orbital period: ~27.3 days
- No atmosphere → extreme temperatures, no weather
- Surface covered with craters (impacts)
- Reflected sunlight makes it visible (not self-luminous)

### Tides
- Caused by the gravitational pull of the Moon (and Sun) on Earth's oceans
- High tides when ocean faces toward or away from Moon
- Two high tides and two low tides every ~24 hours

## Satellites

### Natural Satellites
- Moons orbiting planets

### Artificial Satellites

| Type | Orbit Height | Period | Uses |
|------|-------------|--------|------|
| **Low Earth Orbit (LEO)** | 200–2000 km | ~90 minutes | Weather, GPS, ISS, Earth observation |
| **Geostationary orbit** | 35,786 km | 24 hours | TV, telecommunications, weather |

### Geostationary Satellites
- Orbit above the **equator**
- Period = 24 hours → always above same point on Earth
- Very useful for communication (dish antenna always points same direction)
- Cannot observe polar regions well

### GPS (Global Positioning System)
- Network of ~30 satellites in MEO (medium Earth orbit)
- Receiver calculates position from signals from multiple satellites
- Accurate to within metres

## The Earth

### Structure of Earth
| Layer | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Crust** | Thin outer shell (5–70 km thick); solid rock |
| **Mantle** | Thick layer of semi-solid rock; convection currents drive plate tectonics |
| **Outer core** | Liquid iron and nickel; generates Earth's magnetic field |
| **Inner core** | Solid iron and nickel; extreme pressure and temperature |

### Earth's Magnetic Field
- Generated by convection of liquid iron in the outer core
- Protects Earth from harmful solar wind (charged particles)
- Used for navigation (compasses)
