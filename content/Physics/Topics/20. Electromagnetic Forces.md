## The Motor Effect

> When a **current-carrying conductor** is placed in a **magnetic field**, it experiences a **force**.

This is called the **motor effect** (or the force on a current-carrying conductor).

### Conditions for Maximum Force
- The conductor must be **perpendicular** to the magnetic field
- If conductor is parallel to field → no force

---

## Fleming's Left-Hand Rule (Motor Effect)

Used to find the direction of the force on a current-carrying conductor:

- **F**irst finger → **F**ield direction (North to South)
- Se**C**ond finger → **C**urrent direction (conventional, + to −)
- Thu**M**b → **M**otion / Force (direction of movement)

> 💡 Left Hand = Motor (uses electrical energy to produce motion)

### The Formula

$$F = BIl$$

- $F$ = force on conductor (N)
- $B$ = magnetic flux density (T, tesla)
- $I$ = current (A)
- $l$ = length of conductor in the field (m)

**Factors increasing the force:**
- Stronger magnet (larger B)
- Larger current (larger I)
- Longer wire in the field (larger l)

---

## Force on a Moving Charge

A moving charged particle in a magnetic field also experiences a force:

$$F = Bqv$$

- $q$ = charge (C)
- $v$ = velocity (m/s)

---

## The DC Electric Motor

A **DC motor** converts **electrical energy → kinetic (mechanical) energy**.

### Construction
- A rectangular **coil** of wire (armature)
- Placed between the poles of a permanent magnet
- Connected to power supply via a **split-ring commutator** and brushes

### How it Works
1. Current flows through the coil
2. Motor effect → force on each side of coil
3. Forces on opposite sides are in opposite directions → coil rotates
4. **Split-ring commutator** reverses current direction every half turn → keeps rotation in same direction
5. **Carbon brushes** maintain electrical contact as commutator rotates

### Increasing Motor Speed
- Increase current
- Use stronger magnets
- More turns of wire in the coil
- Use an iron core in the coil (concentrates magnetic field)

---

## The Loudspeaker

Uses the motor effect to convert **electrical signals → sound**.

1. AC signal fed to coil attached to speaker cone
2. Coil in magnetic field → alternating force on coil
3. Coil vibrates back and forth at same frequency as signal
4. Vibrating cone creates sound waves in air

---

## The Moving-Coil Ammeter (Galvanometer)

Uses the motor effect to measure current:
- Current in coil → force → coil rotates against a spring
- Angle of rotation proportional to current
- Pointer deflects across a scale

---

## Force Between Two Parallel Wires

- Two parallel wires carrying current exert forces on each other
- **Same direction currents:** wires **attract**
- **Opposite direction currents:** wires **repel**

---

## Cathode Ray Tube / Electron Beam Deflection

- Charged particles (electrons) moving in a magnetic field are deflected by a force (motor effect)
- Used in oscilloscopes, old TV screens (CRTs)
- **Electric field** can also deflect electrons (inkjet printer)
