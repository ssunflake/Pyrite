## Properties of Light

- Light is a **transverse, electromagnetic wave**
- Travels at **3 × 10⁸ m/s** in a vacuum (the fastest anything can travel)
- Does **not** need a medium — travels through vacuum
- Can be reflected, refracted, diffracted, and absorbed

---

## Reflection of Light

### Laws of Reflection
1. The **angle of incidence** equals the **angle of reflection**
2. The incident ray, reflected ray, and **normal** all lie in the same plane

> ⚠️ Angles are always measured from the **normal** (perpendicular to the surface), NOT from the surface itself.

### Types of Reflection
| Type | Surface | Result |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Specular (regular)** | Smooth, flat (mirror) | Clear, sharp image |
| **Diffuse** | Rough surface | Scattered light, no clear image |

### Plane Mirror — Image Properties
- Same **size** as object
- Same **distance** behind mirror as object is in front
- **Virtual** (cannot be projected on screen — rays don't actually pass through)
- **Upright**
- **Laterally inverted** (left-right reversed)

---

## Refraction of Light

> **Refraction** is the bending of light when it passes from one medium to another, caused by a change in **speed**.

### Refraction Rules
| Transition | Speed change | Bending direction |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Less dense → more dense (e.g. air → glass) | Slows down | Bends **towards** normal |
| More dense → less dense (e.g. glass → air) | Speeds up | Bends **away** from normal |

> If light hits a boundary at **90°** (along the normal) → no bending occurs.

### Refractive Index

$$n = \frac{\sin i}{\sin r}$$

Also:
$$n = \frac{v_1}{v_2} = \frac{c}{v_{medium}}$$

- $n$ = refractive index (no unit, always ≥ 1)
- $i$ = angle of incidence (in less dense medium)
- $r$ = angle of refraction (in more dense medium)
- Higher $n$ → light bends more when entering that medium

| Medium | Refractive Index |
|--------|-----------------|
| Vacuum/Air | 1.0 |
| Water | 1.33 |
| Glass | ~1.5 |
| Diamond | 2.42 |

## Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

> When light travels from a **more dense to less dense** medium, and the angle of incidence exceeds the **critical angle**, the light is **totally internally reflected** — none passes through.

### Critical Angle

$$\sin C = \frac{1}{n}$$

- $C$ = critical angle
- $n$ = refractive index of the denser medium

| What happens | Angle of incidence |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Refraction (some reflection) | < Critical angle |
| Light travels along boundary | = Critical angle |
| Total internal reflection | > Critical angle |

### Conditions for TIR
1. Light must be travelling from **more dense to less dense** medium
2. Angle of incidence must be **greater than** the critical angle

### Applications of TIR

| Application | Explanation |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Optical fibres** | Light bounces along inside fibre by TIR; used in communications and endoscopes |
| **Prism periscopes** | Right-angled prisms replace mirrors; TIR gives 100% reflection |
| **Bicycle reflectors** | Prisms reflect light back to driver |
| **Diamond sparkle** | High n (2.42) → small critical angle → lots of TIR inside |

## Lenses

### Converging (Convex) Lens
- Thicker in the **middle**
- Refracts parallel rays to a **focal point**
- Can form real or virtual images

### Diverging (Concave) Lens
- Thicker at the **edges**
- Diverges parallel rays (rays appear to come from a virtual focal point)
- Always forms a **virtual, upright, smaller** image

### Key Terms
- **Principal axis:** line through the centre of the lens
- **Optical centre (C):** centre of the lens
- **Principal focus (F):** where parallel rays converge after passing through lens
- **Focal length (f):** distance from lens centre to principal focus

### Converging Lens — Ray Diagrams

Three standard rays:
1. Ray parallel to axis → passes through **F** after lens
2. Ray through optical centre → continues **straight**
3. Ray through **F** before lens → travels parallel to axis after lens

### Converging Lens — Image Summary

| Object distance | Image type | Image position | Image size | Use |
|----------------|-----------|---------------|-----------|-----|
| Beyond 2F | Real, inverted | Between F and 2F | Smaller | Camera |
| At 2F | Real, inverted | At 2F | Same size | Photocopier |
| Between F and 2F | Real, inverted | Beyond 2F | Larger | Projector |
| At F | No image | ∞ | — | Searchlight, torch |
| Inside F (< F) | Virtual, upright | Same side as object | Larger | Magnifying glass |

### The Lens Formula (Extension)
$$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v}$$

- $f$ = focal length
- $u$ = object distance
- $v$ = image distance

## Dispersion of Light

- White light is a mixture of all colours (wavelengths) of visible light
- When white light passes through a **prism**, different colours refract by different amounts → **spectrum**
- **Red** refracts least (longest wavelength)
- **Violet** refracts most (shortest wavelength)

### Order of Spectrum
**R O Y G B I V** (Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, Violet)
- Red: longest wavelength, lowest frequency
- Violet: shortest wavelength, highest frequency
