## The Kinetic Model

The **kinetic model** states that all matter is made up of tiny particles (atoms/molecules) that are in constant motion. The properties of solids, liquids and gases can be explained by the arrangement and movement of these particles.

---

## The Three States of Matter

### Solids
- Particles are **closely packed** in a regular, ordered arrangement (lattice)
- Particles **vibrate** about fixed positions — they do not move freely
- **Strong forces** between particles hold them in place
- **Fixed shape and volume**
- Nearly incompressible

### Liquids
- Particles are **close together** but arranged randomly
- Particles can **slide and flow past each other**
- **Moderate forces** between particles
- **Fixed volume, no fixed shape** — takes the shape of container
- Nearly incompressible

### Gases
- Particles are **far apart**, moving rapidly in random directions
- Particles move freely, colliding with each other and container walls
- **Very weak forces** between particles (essentially none)
- **No fixed shape or volume** — fills any container
- Highly compressible

### Comparison Table

| Property | Solid | Liquid | Gas |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Particle arrangement | Regular, close | Random, close | Random, far apart |
| Particle movement | Vibration only | Slide past each other | Rapid random motion |
| Shape | Fixed | Takes container shape | Fills container |
| Volume | Fixed | Fixed | Fills container |
| Compressibility | Very low | Very low | High |
| Density | High | Medium-High | Low |
| Forces between particles | Strong | Moderate | Very weak |

## Changes of State

### Heating and Cooling Curve

```
Temperature
↑
|          boiling/condensation
|     ___________________________
|    /                           \
| __/   melting/freezing          \__
|/                                    \
|_______________________________________→ Heat added
```

- **Flat sections** = change of state (temperature stays constant)
- **Rising sections** = temperature increasing (energy increases particle KE)

### Change of State Processes

| Process | Change | Energy |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Melting | Solid → Liquid | Energy absorbed |
| Freezing | Liquid → Solid | Energy released |
| Evaporation | Liquid → Gas (at any temperature) | Energy absorbed |
| Boiling | Liquid → Gas (at boiling point) | Energy absorbed |
| Condensation | Gas → Liquid | Energy released |
| Sublimation | Solid → Gas (directly) | Energy absorbed |

> During a change of state, energy is used to break/form bonds — **not** to increase temperature.

## Brownian Motion

> The **random, erratic** movement of microscopic particles suspended in a fluid (liquid or gas), caused by unequal collisions with surrounding molecules.

### Experiment
- Smoke particles viewed under a microscope with a light source
- Smoke particles appear as bright specks moving randomly

### Significance
- Provides evidence for the **kinetic model of matter**
- Shows that gas molecules are moving randomly and are invisible
- Larger smoke particles bombarded unequally from all sides → random motion

## Gas Pressure and the Kinetic Model

- Gas molecules are in constant **random motion**
- They collide with the walls of their container → exert a **force** on the walls
- **Pressure** = force per unit area from these collisions

### Effect of Temperature on Pressure
- Higher temperature → molecules move faster → more frequent and harder collisions → **higher pressure** (at constant volume)

### Effect of Volume on Pressure (Boyle's Law)
> At constant temperature, pressure × volume = constant.

$$PV = \text{constant}$$
$$P_1V_1 = P_2V_2$$

- Compress gas (reduce V) → molecules hit walls more often → **higher pressure**
- Expand gas (increase V) → molecules hit walls less often → **lower pressure**

### Pressure Law (Gay-Lussac's Law)
> At constant volume, pressure is proportional to absolute temperature.

$$\frac{P_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2}{T_2}$$

- Temperature must be in **Kelvin**
$$T(K) = T(°C) + 273$$

### Combined Gas Law
$$\frac{P_1V_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2V_2}{T_2}$$

---

## Absolute Zero

- **Absolute zero** = 0 K = −273°C
- The temperature at which particles have the **minimum possible energy**
- Gas molecules would have zero kinetic energy and exert zero pressure
- Cannot be reached in practice (only approached)

---

## Evaporation vs Boiling

| Feature | Evaporation | Boiling |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Temperature | Any temperature | Only at boiling point |
| Location | Surface of liquid | Throughout liquid |
| Rate | Slow and gradual | Rapid, visible bubbles |
| Cooling effect | Yes (takes energy from surroundings) | External heat needed |

### Why evaporation causes cooling:
- Fastest (highest energy) molecules escape from the surface
- Average KE of remaining molecules decreases
- Temperature of liquid drops

**Factors increasing evaporation rate:**
- Higher temperature
- Larger surface area
- Lower humidity
- Draught / air movement
