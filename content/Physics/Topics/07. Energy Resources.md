## Non-Renewable Energy Resources

> Resources that **cannot be replenished** once used up (on human timescales).

### Fossil Fuels (coal, oil, natural gas)
| Fuel | How it produces electricity |
|------|-----------------------------|
| **Coal** | Burned → heats water → steam → turbine → generator |
| **Oil** | Same as coal, also used directly as fuel |
| **Natural gas** | Burns more cleanly than coal; also used in turbines |

**Advantages:**
- Reliable — produces electricity on demand
- Existing infrastructure
- High energy density

**Disadvantages:**
- Produce CO₂ → greenhouse effect → climate change
- Produce SO₂ → acid rain
- Finite supply — will run out
- Mining/drilling damages environment

### Nuclear Power
- **Uranium-235** undergoes **fission** in a reactor → heat → steam → turbine → generator
- Does not produce CO₂
- Produces **radioactive waste** that must be stored safely for thousands of years
- Risk of accidents (Chernobyl, Fukushima)
- High initial building cost

## Renewable Energy Resources

> Resources that can be replenished naturally and will not run out.

### Solar Energy
- **Photovoltaic cells** → convert sunlight directly to electricity
- **Solar thermal** → heat water using sunlight
- **Advantages:** free fuel, no emissions, low maintenance
- **Disadvantages:** depends on weather/daylight, expensive panels, large areas needed

### Wind Energy
- Wind turns turbine blades → generator → electricity
- **Advantages:** no fuel cost, no emissions
- **Disadvantages:** unreliable (wind not constant), noisy, visual impact on landscape, kills birds

### Hydroelectric Power (HEP)
- Falling water from reservoir drives turbines
- **Advantages:** reliable, no emissions during generation, can respond quickly to demand
- **Disadvantages:** flooding of valleys, destruction of habitats, expensive dams

### Tidal Energy
- Tides turn turbines as water flows in and out
- **Advantages:** predictable, no fuel cost
- **Disadvantages:** few suitable sites, impact on coastal ecosystems, expensive

### Geothermal Energy
- Heat from Earth's interior heats water → steam → turbine
- **Advantages:** reliable, low emissions
- **Disadvantages:** only viable in certain locations (volcanic areas)

### Biomass / Biofuels
- Burning plant material or animal waste
- Can be **carbon neutral** if replanted (CO₂ absorbed during growth = CO₂ released during burning)
- Examples: wood, biogas, ethanol from crops
- **Disadvantages:** land use, potential deforestation, not always truly carbon neutral

### Wave Energy
- Waves move floating devices → generates electricity
- **Advantages:** no fuel cost, no emissions
- **Disadvantages:** unreliable, technology still developing, maintenance at sea is difficult

## Energy and the Environment

### Greenhouse Effect
- Burning fossil fuels releases CO₂ and CH₄
- These greenhouse gases trap heat in the atmosphere
- Leads to **global warming** → climate change

### Consequences of Climate Change
- Rising sea levels (melting ice caps)
- More extreme weather events
- Loss of habitats and species

### Acid Rain
- Burning coal releases SO₂ and NOₓ
- These dissolve in rainwater → sulfuric/nitric acid
- Damages forests, lakes, buildings

### Nuclear Waste
- Low-level waste: protective clothing → buried
- High-level waste: must be stored safely for thousands of years

## The National Grid

- Electricity is generated at **power stations**
- **Step-up transformers** increase voltage to ~400,000 V for transmission
- High voltage = low current = less energy lost as heat in cables
- **Step-down transformers** reduce voltage for homes (230 V) and industry

$$P = I^2 R$$

> Lower current in transmission lines → less power wasted as heat.
