## What is a Wave?

A **wave** is a transfer of **energy** from one place to another without the transfer of matter.

- The particles of the medium vibrate but do not travel with the wave
- Waves carry energy, information, and momentum

---

## Types of Waves

### Transverse Waves
- Particles vibrate **perpendicular** (at 90°) to the direction of wave travel
- Can travel through solids and surface of liquids
- **Examples:** light, all EM waves, water surface waves, waves on a rope/string, seismic S-waves

### Longitudinal Waves
- Particles vibrate **parallel** (along) the direction of wave travel
- Travel as compressions and rarefactions
- **Examples:** sound, ultrasound, seismic P-waves

| Feature | Transverse | Longitudinal |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Particle vibration | Perpendicular to travel | Parallel to travel |
| Can travel in vacuum? | Yes (EM waves) | No (needs medium) |
| Shows crests and troughs? | Yes | No (compressions/rarefactions) |
| Examples | Light, water waves | Sound, ultrasound |

---

## Key Wave Quantities

| Quantity | Symbol | Unit | Definition |
|----------|--------|------|-----------|
| Amplitude | A | m | Maximum displacement from rest position |
| Wavelength | λ | m | Distance between two successive identical points (e.g. crest to crest) |
| Frequency | f | Hz | Number of complete waves per second |
| Period | T | s | Time for one complete oscillation |
| Wave speed | v | m/s | Distance travelled per second |

---

## Wave Equations

$$v = f\lambda$$

$$T = \frac{1}{f}$$

- $v$ = wave speed (m/s)
- $f$ = frequency (Hz)
- $\lambda$ = wavelength (m)
- $T$ = period (s)

### Example
A wave has frequency 200 Hz and wavelength 1.7 m. Find the wave speed.
$$v = f\lambda = 200 \times 1.7 = 340 \text{ m/s}$$

## Wave Diagrams

### Transverse Wave
```
Amplitude →↕
           /\      /\      /\
          /  \    /  \    /  \
---------/----\--/----\--/----\--------→ direction of travel
              \/      \/
|←— λ ——→|
```

- **Crest** = highest point
- **Trough** = lowest point
- **Amplitude** = height from rest position to crest/trough
- **Wavelength** = crest to crest (or trough to trough)

## Reflection of Waves

- Waves bounce off a surface
- **Angle of incidence = angle of reflection** (measured from normal)
- Wavelength and frequency are **unchanged**
- Speed may change (if medium changes)

**Evidence from ripple tank:**
- Straight waves reflected from a flat barrier reflect at equal angles
- Curved barrier can focus waves to a point

---

## Refraction of Waves

- Waves change direction when they move from one medium to another (change of speed)
- Wavelength changes; **frequency stays the same**
- If speed increases → wavelength increases → bends away from normal
- If speed decreases → wavelength decreases → bends towards normal

$$v = f\lambda \Rightarrow \text{if } f \text{ is constant and } v \text{ decreases, then } \lambda \text{ decreases}$$

**In ripple tank:**
- Waves moving from deep to shallow water **slow down and refract** (bend towards normal in shallow region)
- If waves hit shallow area at 90° → slow down, no bending, wavelength decreases

---

## Diffraction of Waves

> **Diffraction** is the spreading out of waves as they pass through a gap or around an obstacle.

- Most noticeable when gap size ≈ wavelength
- If gap >> wavelength: little diffraction (waves pass straight through)
- If gap ≈ wavelength: maximum diffraction (waves spread widely)
- If gap << wavelength: very little energy gets through

### Diffraction Through a Gap
- Narrow gap → greater diffraction
- Wide gap → less diffraction, more like straight rays

### Applications
- Radio waves diffract around hills (long wavelength matches hill size)
- Light diffracts very little through a doorway (wavelength much smaller than gap)
- Sound diffracts around corners (wavelength comparable to doorway size)

---

## Interference (Brief Overview)

- When two waves meet, they can **add** (constructive) or **cancel** (destructive)
- **Constructive interference:** crests meet → bigger amplitude
- **Destructive interference:** crest meets trough → cancel out

---

## Ripple Tank Experiments

A **ripple tank** is used to demonstrate all wave properties:

| Observation | Wave property shown |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Waves reflected from flat barrier | Reflection |
| Waves bending at shallow region | Refraction |
| Waves spreading through a gap | Diffraction |
| Two sources producing pattern | Interference |
