## What is Radioactivity?

> **Radioactivity** is the **spontaneous** and **random** emission of radiation from unstable atomic nuclei.

- **Spontaneous:** not triggered by external conditions (temperature, pressure, chemical state have no effect)
- **Random:** cannot predict exactly when a particular nucleus will decay
- The nucleus changes to become more stable

---

## The Three Types of Radiation

| Property | Alpha (α) | Beta (β) | Gamma (γ) |
|----------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Nature** | Helium nucleus ($^4_2\text{He}$) | Fast-moving electron ($^0_{-1}e$) | Electromagnetic wave (photon) |
| **Charge** | +2 | −1 | 0 |
| **Mass** | 4 u | ~0 | 0 |
| **Speed** | ~5% of c | Up to ~90% of c | c (3×10⁸ m/s) |
| **Range in air** | ~5 cm | ~1 m | Kilometres |
| **Penetrating power** | Very low | Medium | Very high |
| **Stopped by** | Paper or skin | 3–5 mm aluminium | Thick lead or concrete |
| **Ionising ability** | **Very high** | Medium | **Very low** |

---

## Deflection in Electric and Magnetic Fields

| Radiation | Electric field | Magnetic field |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Alpha | Deflected toward negative plate | Deflected |
| Beta | Deflected toward positive plate (opposite to alpha) | Deflected (opposite direction to alpha) |
| Gamma | Not deflected | Not deflected |

- Alpha and beta deflect in opposite directions (opposite charges)
- Gamma not deflected (no charge)
- Alpha deflects less than beta (more massive)

## Nuclear Decay Equations

### Alpha Decay
$$^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2} Y + ^4_2\alpha$$

- Mass number decreases by **4**
- Atomic number decreases by **2**

**Example:**
$$^{226}_{88}\text{Ra} \rightarrow ^{222}_{86}\text{Rn} + ^4_2\alpha$$

### Beta Decay
$$^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A}_{Z+1} Y + ^0_{-1}\beta$$

- Mass number **unchanged**
- Atomic number increases by **1**
- A neutron converts to a proton + emitted electron

**Example:**
$$^{14}_6\text{C} \rightarrow ^{14}_7\text{N} + ^0_{-1}\beta$$

### Gamma Emission
- Nucleus releases energy as a gamma photon
- **No change** in mass number or atomic number
- Often follows alpha or beta decay

> In all decay equations: **total mass number is conserved** and **total charge (atomic number) is conserved**.

## Background Radiation

> **Background radiation** is the low-level radiation that is always present in the environment.

### Sources

| Natural Sources | Artificial Sources |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Radon gas (from rocks/soil) — largest source | Medical X-rays and procedures |
| Rocks and soil (granite, etc.) | Nuclear power stations |
| Cosmic rays from space | Nuclear weapons testing fallout |
| Food and drink (e.g. potassium-40) | Industrial sources |
| Building materials | |
| Living organisms | |

> Background radiation must be **subtracted** from measured count rates to get the true count rate from a source.

$$\text{corrected count rate} = \text{measured count rate} - \text{background count rate}$$

## Half-Life

> The **half-life** is the time taken for **half the radioactive nuclei** in a sample to decay (or for the **activity to halve**).

- Half-life is **constant** for a given isotope
- **Independent** of temperature, pressure, or chemical state
- Different isotopes have very different half-lives (milliseconds to billions of years)

### Half-Life Calculations

| Number of half-lives elapsed | Fraction of nuclei remaining | Fraction that have decayed |
|------------------------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------------|
| 0 | 1 | 0 |
| 1 | 1/2 | 1/2 |
| 2 | 1/4 | 3/4 |
| 3 | 1/8 | 7/8 |
| 4 | 1/16 | 15/16 |
| n | $(1/2)^n$ | $1 - (1/2)^n$ |

**Formula:**
$$N = N_0 \times \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n$$

where $n = \dfrac{t}{t_{1/2}}$

### Activity
- **Activity** = number of decays per second
- Unit: **Becquerel (Bq)** (1 Bq = 1 decay per second)
- Activity halves every half-life (just like number of nuclei)

## Uses of Radioactivity

| Use | Type | Reason |
|-----|------|--------|
| **Smoke detectors** | Alpha (Americium-241) | Ionises air between plates → current flows → alarm if smoke blocks ions |
| **Medical tracers** | Gamma (short half-life, e.g. Tc-99m) | Penetrates body; short half-life = low dose |
| **Cancer treatment (radiotherapy)** | Gamma | Kills cancer cells (focused beam) |
| **Thickness gauges (paper/metal)** | Beta | Amount absorbed depends on thickness |
| **Sterilising food and equipment** | Gamma | Kills bacteria without heating |
| **Carbon dating** | Beta (C-14, t½ = 5730 years) | Living things absorb C-14; stops at death → date from ratio |
| **Uranium dating (geology)** | Alpha (U-238, t½ = 4.5 billion years) | For very old rocks |

## Dangers of Radiation

### Health Effects
- **Ionising radiation** removes electrons from atoms → forms ions
- Can break DNA → **mutations** → cancer
- High doses → radiation sickness, tissue damage, death
- Effects depend on: type of radiation, dose, duration, body part exposed

### Ionising Ability and Penetration
- Alpha: most ionising but least penetrating (dangerous if inhaled/ingested)
- Beta: moderate ionisation and penetration
- Gamma: least ionising but most penetrating (most dangerous externally)

### Safety Precautions

| Precaution | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Increase distance from source | Intensity ∝ 1/d² (inverse square law) |
| Minimise exposure time | Less time = less dose |
| Use shielding (lead, concrete) | Absorbs radiation |
| Handle with tongs, not bare hands | Avoids skin contamination |
| Store in lead-lined containers | Absorbs radiation when stored |
| Wear protective clothing and film badge | Monitor and reduce exposure |

## Nuclear Fission

> **Fission** is the splitting of a heavy nucleus into two smaller nuclei when struck by a neutron, releasing a large amount of energy and more neutrons.

$$^{235}_{92}\text{U} + ^1_0\text{n} \rightarrow \text{fission products} + 2\text{–}3 \, ^1_0\text{n} + \text{energy}$$

### Chain Reaction
- Neutrons released from fission trigger more fissions
- **Controlled chain reaction** → nuclear reactor (power generation)
- **Uncontrolled chain reaction** → nuclear bomb

### Nuclear Reactor (brief)
- Fuel rods: enriched uranium
- Control rods: absorb excess neutrons (boron or cadmium) → control rate
- Moderator: slows neutrons to ideal speed (water or graphite)
- Coolant: carries heat away to generate steam → turbine → electricity

---

## Nuclear Fusion

> **Fusion** is the joining of two light nuclei to form a heavier nucleus, releasing a large amount of energy.

$$^2_1\text{H} + ^3_1\text{H} \rightarrow ^4_2\text{He} + ^1_0\text{n} + \text{energy}$$

- Requires **extremely high temperatures** (~10⁷ K) to overcome electrostatic repulsion
- This is how the **Sun and stars** generate energy
- Not yet practical for power generation on Earth (being researched — ITER project)

| | Fission | Fusion |
|-|---------|--------|
| Process | Heavy nucleus splits | Light nuclei join |
| Energy released | Large | Even larger |
| Fuel | Uranium | Hydrogen isotopes |
| Radioactive waste | Yes (significant) | Minimal |
| Current use | Nuclear power stations | Stars (not yet on Earth) |
