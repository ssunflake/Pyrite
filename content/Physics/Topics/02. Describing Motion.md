## Distance and Displacement

| Quantity | Type | Definition |
|----------|------|-----------|
| **Distance** | Scalar | Total path length travelled |
| **Displacement** | Vector | Straight-line distance from start to finish, with direction |

---

## Speed and Velocity

| Quantity | Type | Formula |
|----------|------|---------|
| **Speed** | Scalar | $v = \dfrac{d}{t}$ |
| **Velocity** | Vector | $v = \dfrac{s}{t}$ (displacement / time) |

- Units: m/s or km/h
- **Average speed** = total distance ÷ total time
- **Instantaneous speed** = speed at a specific moment

### Typical Speeds
| Object | Approximate speed |
|--------|-----------------|
| Walking | 1.5 m/s |
| Cycling | 6 m/s |
| Car on motorway | 30 m/s |
| Sound in air | 340 m/s |
| Light | 3 × 10⁸ m/s |

---

## Acceleration

$$a = \frac{v - u}{t}$$

- $a$ = acceleration (m/s²)
- $v$ = final velocity (m/s)
- $u$ = initial velocity (m/s)
- $t$ = time (s)

- **Positive acceleration** = speeding up (in direction of motion)
- **Negative acceleration (deceleration)** = slowing down
- Units: m/s²

---

## Distance–Time (d–t) Graphs

| Graph shape | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| Horizontal line | Stationary (at rest) |
| Straight line (positive gradient) | Constant speed |
| Curve (increasing gradient) | Accelerating |
| Curve (decreasing gradient) | Decelerating |

> 💡 **Gradient of d–t graph = speed**

$$\text{speed} = \frac{\Delta d}{\Delta t} = \text{gradient}$$

---

## Velocity–Time (v–t) Graphs

| Graph shape | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| Horizontal line | Constant velocity (zero acceleration) |
| Straight line sloping up | Constant acceleration |
| Straight line sloping down | Constant deceleration |
| Curve | Changing acceleration |

> 💡 **Gradient of v–t graph = acceleration**
> 💡 **Area under v–t graph = displacement**

$$a = \text{gradient} = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}$$
$$s = \text{area under graph}$$

### Area Under v–t Graph
- Rectangle: $A = \text{base} \times \text{height}$
- Triangle: $A = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$
- For complex shapes, split into rectangles + triangles

## Equations of Motion (SUVAT)

Used when acceleration is **constant**:

$$v = u + at$$
$$s = \frac{(u + v)}{2} \times t$$
$$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$
$$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$

| Symbol | Quantity | Unit |
|--------|---------|------|
| s | displacement | m |
| u | initial velocity | m/s |
| v | final velocity | m/s |
| a | acceleration | m/s² |
| t | time | s |

## Free Fall and Terminal Velocity

### Free Fall
- All objects fall with the same acceleration due to gravity: **g = 9.8 m/s² ≈ 10 m/s²**
- In the absence of air resistance, all objects accelerate equally regardless of mass

### Terminal Velocity
When an object falls through a fluid (air or liquid):

1. Initially accelerates — weight > drag
2. As speed increases, drag increases
3. Eventually **drag = weight** → no resultant force → **terminal velocity**

| Stage | Forces | Motion |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Just released | Weight >> drag | Accelerating |
| Falling faster | Weight > drag | Still accelerating, but less |
| Terminal velocity | Weight = drag | Constant velocity |

> Skydivers reach terminal velocity of ~55 m/s (120 mph) before opening parachute.
> After parachute opens, terminal velocity drops to ~5–6 m/s.

## Measuring Motion

### Ticker Timer
- Makes dots on tape at regular intervals (50 Hz = 50 dots/second)
- Gap between dots = distance in 0.02 s
- Wider gaps = faster motion

### Light Gates
- Beam of light broken by object passing through
- Accurately measures time for object to pass
- Connected to a data logger or computer

### Motion Sensors (Ultrasound)
- Sends pulses of ultrasound, measures time for echo
- Plots real-time d–t or v–t graphs on a computer
