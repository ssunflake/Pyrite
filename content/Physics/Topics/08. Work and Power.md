## Work Done

$$W = Fd$$

- $W$ = work done (J, joules)
- $F$ = force (N)
- $d$ = distance moved **in the direction of the force** (m)

> Work is only done when a force causes displacement **in the direction of the force**.

### Important Points
- If force is perpendicular to motion → **no work done** (e.g. centripetal force)
- If object doesn't move → **no work done** (e.g. holding a heavy bag still)
- 1 joule = work done when 1 N force moves through 1 m

### Work at an Angle
If the force is at angle θ to the direction of motion:
$$W = Fd\cos\theta$$

| Angle | Work done |
|-------|----------|
| 0° (same direction) | $W = Fd$ (maximum) |
| 90° (perpendicular) | $W = 0$ |
| 180° (opposite) | $W = -Fd$ (negative work) |

## Power

$$P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{E}{t}$$

- $P$ = power (W, watts)
- $W$ = work done / energy transferred (J)
- $t$ = time taken (s)

> **Power** is the rate of doing work or transferring energy.

- 1 watt = 1 joule per second
- 1 kilowatt (kW) = 1000 W
- 1 megawatt (MW) = 10⁶ W

### Alternative Power Formula
Since $W = Fd$ and $v = d/t$:

$$P = Fv$$

- Useful when a constant force moves at constant velocity
- e.g. a car travelling at constant speed against friction

### Typical Power Values
| Device | Approximate Power |
|--------|-----------------|
| LED bulb | 10 W |
| Laptop | 50 W |
| Human (walking) | 100 W |
| Kettle | 2000–3000 W |
| Car engine | 50,000–150,000 W |
| Jet engine | ~100 MW |
| Large power station | ~1 GW |

## Energy and Power Together

$$E = Pt$$

- If you know power and time → calculate energy used
- Useful for electricity bills (kilowatt-hours)

### Kilowatt-Hour (kWh)
- Unit used by electricity companies for energy
$$1 \text{ kWh} = 1000 \text{ W} \times 3600 \text{ s} = 3,600,000 \text{ J} = 3.6 \times 10^6 \text{ J}$$

**Cost of electricity:**
$$\text{Cost} = \text{power (kW)} \times \text{time (h)} \times \text{price per kWh}$$

## Efficiency and Power

$$\text{Efficiency} = \frac{P_{useful}}{P_{total}} \times 100\%$$

- Same as energy efficiency but using power values

### Example
A motor uses 500 W of electrical power and outputs 350 W of mechanical power.
$$\text{Efficiency} = \frac{350}{500} \times 100\% = 70\%$$

## Worked Examples

### Example 1 — Work Done
A box is pushed 5 m along the floor with a force of 40 N.
$$W = Fd = 40 \times 5 = 200 \text{ J}$$

### Example 2 — Power
A student of mass 60 kg climbs stairs of height 3 m in 6 s.
$$W = mgh = 60 \times 10 \times 3 = 1800 \text{ J}$$
$$P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{1800}{6} = 300 \text{ W}$$

### Example 3 — Power and Velocity
A car engine provides a driving force of 2000 N at a constant speed of 20 m/s.
$$P = Fv = 2000 \times 20 = 40,000 \text{ W} = 40 \text{ kW}$$
