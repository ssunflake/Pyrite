## Density

$$\rho = \frac{m}{V}$$

- $\rho$ = density (kg/m³)
- $m$ = mass (kg)
- $V$ = volume (m³)

| Material | Density (kg/m³) |
|----------|----------------|
| Air | 1.2 |
| Water | 1000 |
| Aluminium | 2700 |
| Iron/Steel | 7800 |
| Gold | 19300 |
| Ice | 920 |

> Objects **float** if their density < density of the fluid they are in.

### Measuring Density
- **Regular solid:** measure dimensions with ruler/calipers, calculate volume, use formula
- **Irregular solid:** use displacement method in a measuring cylinder or eureka can
- **Liquid:** measure mass of a known volume using measuring cylinder and balance

## Pressure

$$P = \frac{F}{A}$$

- $P$ = pressure (Pa = N/m²)
- $F$ = force (N)
- $A$ = area (m²)

**Examples:**
- Sharp knife: small area → high pressure → cuts easily
- Snowshoes: large area → low pressure → don't sink into snow
- Nails: pointed tip → very high pressure

---

## Pressure in Fluids

$$P = h \rho g$$

- $h$ = depth below surface (m)
- $\rho$ = density of fluid (kg/m³)
- $g$ = gravitational field strength (N/kg)

**Properties of pressure in a fluid:**
- Increases with depth
- Acts equally in all directions at the same depth
- Does not depend on the shape of the container (Pascal's principle)

### Atmospheric Pressure
- Air has weight → exerts pressure on surfaces
- At sea level: ~101,000 Pa (101 kPa)
- Decreases with altitude (less air above)
- Measured with a **barometer** or **manometer**

### Upthrust (Archimedes' Principle)
> When an object is fully or partially submerged, the upward **upthrust** equals the weight of fluid displaced.

$$\text{Upthrust} = \rho_{fluid} \times V_{displaced} \times g$$

- Object floats when: **upthrust = weight**
- Object sinks when: **weight > upthrust**

## Hooke's Law

> The extension of a spring is **directly proportional** to the applied force, provided the **limit of proportionality** is not exceeded.

$$F = ke$$

- $F$ = force / load (N)
- $k$ = spring constant (N/m) — stiffness of spring
- $e$ = extension = stretched length − original length (m)

### Limit of Proportionality
- Up to this point: F ∝ e (straight line on graph)
- Beyond this: graph curves → spring does not obey Hooke's Law
- **Elastic limit**: beyond this point the spring is permanently deformed

### Force–Extension Graph
- **Linear section** (straight line through origin): Hooke's Law obeyed
- **Curved section**: limit of proportionality exceeded
- **Permanent set**: spring doesn't return to original length after load removed

### Elastic and Plastic Deformation

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Elastic deformation** | Object returns to original shape when force removed |
| **Plastic (inelastic) deformation** | Object is permanently deformed |

## Stretching, Compressing, and Bending

- Forces can **stretch**, **compress**, or **bend** materials
- **Tensile force** — pulls ends of object apart (stretching)
- **Compressive force** — pushes ends together (compression)
- **Shear force** — acts parallel to surface, causes layers to slide

### Springs in Series and Parallel

| Arrangement | Effective spring constant |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| Series (end to end) | $\dfrac{1}{k_{eff}} = \dfrac{1}{k_1} + \dfrac{1}{k_2}$ (softer) |
| Parallel (side by side) | $k_{eff} = k_1 + k_2$ (stiffer) |
