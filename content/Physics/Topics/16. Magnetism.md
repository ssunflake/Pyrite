## Magnetic Poles

- Every magnet has a **North (N)** and **South (S)** pole
- **Like poles repel; unlike poles attract**
- You can never isolate a single pole — breaking a magnet always gives two smaller magnets

---

## Magnetic Fields

> A **magnetic field** is a region in space where a magnetic force is experienced.

- Represented by **magnetic field lines** (lines of force)
- Field lines go from **North to South** (outside the magnet)
- Arrows on field lines show direction of force on a **north** pole
- **Closer lines = stronger field**
- Field lines **never cross**

### Drawing Field Lines

**Bar magnet:**
- Lines come out of North, loop around, and go into South
- Field is strongest at the poles (lines closest together)
- Field lines are curved

**Two attracting poles (N–S):**
- Field lines connect between the poles

**Two repelling poles (N–N):**
- Field lines push away from each other; a neutral point exists between them

## Magnetic Materials

| Type | Behaviour | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Ferromagnetic** | Strongly attracted to magnets; can be magnetised | Iron, steel, nickel, cobalt |
| **Non-magnetic** | Not attracted to magnets | Copper, aluminium, wood, plastic |

> Note: Magnetic materials are **attracted** to both poles of a magnet.
> Testing with one pole of a magnet can only confirm if something is **magnetic**, not if it's a magnet.

### Hard and Soft Magnetic Materials

| Property | Soft magnetic material | Hard magnetic material |
|----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Example | Iron | Steel |
| Magnetised easily? | Yes | No (harder to magnetise) |
| Loses magnetism easily? | Yes | No (retains magnetism) |
| Use | Electromagnet cores, transformers | Permanent magnets |

## Making and Destroying Magnets

### Magnetising
| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Stroking** | Stroke with a permanent magnet in one direction repeatedly |
| **Electrical** | Place inside a solenoid (coil) carrying DC current |

### Demagnetising
| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Hammering** | Physical shock disrupts domain alignment |
| **Heating** | Thermal energy disrupts domain alignment |
| **AC current** | Alternating current in a solenoid scrambles domains |

### Magnetic Domains
- Magnetic materials are made of tiny **domains** (regions where atoms act like mini magnets)
- In an **unmagnetised** material: domains point in random directions → cancel out
- In a **magnetised** material: domains aligned in same direction → net magnetic effect

## Electromagnetism

### Magnetic Field Around a Straight Wire
- A current-carrying wire produces a **circular magnetic field** around it
- **Right-hand grip rule:** thumb points in direction of current → fingers curl in direction of field lines

### Magnetic Field of a Solenoid (Coil)
- A solenoid (coil of wire carrying current) produces a field similar to a **bar magnet**
- One end is North, the other South
- Inside: uniform field (parallel lines)
- **Right-hand rule for solenoid:** curl fingers in direction of current → thumb points to North pole

### Factors Affecting Electromagnet Strength
- **Increasing current** → stronger field
- **More turns** of wire → stronger field
- **Iron core** (soft iron) → much stronger field (iron amplifies field)
- Closer to wire → stronger field

---

## Electromagnets

An **electromagnet** is a temporary magnet made from a coil of wire with a soft iron core, which produces a magnetic field only when current flows.

### Advantages over Permanent Magnets
- Can be switched on and off
- Strength can be varied by changing current
- Polarity can be reversed by reversing current

### Applications of Electromagnets

| Application | How it works |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Electric bell** | Electromagnet attracts striker repeatedly |
| **Relay switch** | Small current activates electromagnet which switches larger circuit |
| **Circuit breaker** | Electromagnet trips switch when current too high |
| **Maglev trains** | Electromagnets levitate and propel train |
| **Scrap metal crane** | Lifts magnetic metals; switched off to release |
| **MRI scanner** | Very powerful electromagnets image body |

## Earth's Magnetic Field

- Earth behaves like a giant bar magnet
- Geographic North Pole ≈ Magnetic South Pole (a compass North pole points towards it)
- Used for navigation (compass)
- **Magnetic declination** = angle between true north and magnetic north (varies by location)
- Earth's field is generated by convection currents in molten iron in Earth's outer core
