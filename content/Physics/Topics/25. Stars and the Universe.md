| Milky Way galaxy | ~100,000 light-years across |
| Local Group of galaxies | ~10 million light-years |
| Observable Universe | ~93 billion light-years across |

### The Light-Year
$$1 \text{ light-year} = 9.46 \times 10^{15} \text{ m}$$

- Distance light travels in one year
- Used to measure astronomical distances (stars, galaxies)

## The Sun and Stars

### The Sun
- A **medium-sized, main sequence star**
- Age: ~4.6 billion years
- Diameter: ~1.4 million km
- Core temperature: ~15 million°C
- Energy source: **nuclear fusion** (hydrogen → helium)
- Will become a red giant in ~5 billion years

### What is a Star?
- A large ball of hot gas (plasma) held together by gravity
- Energy produced by **nuclear fusion** in the core
- Visible because they are **self-luminous** (emit their own light)

---

## The Life Cycle of Stars

### Stars of Similar Mass to the Sun

```
Nebula → Protostar → Main Sequence Star → Red Giant → 
White Dwarf → (eventually) Black Dwarf
```

### Massive Stars (much larger than Sun)

```
Nebula → Protostar → Main Sequence Star → Red Supergiant →
Supernova → Neutron Star  OR  Black Hole
```

---

## Stages in Stellar Evolution

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Nebula** | Giant cloud of hydrogen gas and dust; gravity pulls it together |
| **Protostar** | Contracting cloud heats up; nuclear fusion not yet started |
| **Main sequence star** | Nuclear fusion of H → He; radiation pressure balances gravity; **stable** for most of star's life |
| **Red giant** | H in core runs out; core contracts and heats; outer layers expand and cool; He fusion begins |
| **Red supergiant** | Same as red giant but for massive stars; heavier elements fused |
| **Planetary nebula** | Outer layers of red giant expelled into space |
| **White dwarf** | Dense remnant of medium star; no fusion; cools over billions of years |
| **Black dwarf** | Cooled-down white dwarf (theoretical — universe not old enough yet) |
| **Supernova** | Massive star explodes violently; brief outburst brighter than entire galaxy |
| **Neutron star** | Ultra-dense remnant after supernova; mostly neutrons; spinning rapidly |
| **Pulsar** | Rotating neutron star emitting regular pulses of radiation |
| **Black hole** | Remnant of most massive stars; gravity so strong nothing can escape, not even light |

## The Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) Diagram

- Plots **temperature** (x-axis, hot left to cool right) vs **luminosity** (y-axis)
- **Main sequence:** diagonal band where most stars spend most of their lives
- **Giants and supergiants:** upper right (cool, bright)
- **White dwarfs:** lower left (hot, dim)
- The Sun is a middle-of-the-road main sequence star

## Galaxies

> A **galaxy** is a vast collection of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter held together by gravity.

| Type | Shape | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| Spiral | Flat disk with spiral arms | Milky Way, Andromeda |
| Elliptical | Elliptical blob | No star formation |
| Irregular | No regular shape | Magellanic Clouds |

- Our galaxy is the **Milky Way**
- Contains ~200–400 billion stars
- The Sun is in one of the spiral arms

## The Universe and Its Origin

### The Big Bang Theory

> The Universe began ~**13.8 billion years ago** from an extremely hot, dense point (singularity) and has been **expanding** ever since.

**Evidence for the Big Bang:**

| Evidence | Explanation |
|----------|-------------|
| **Red-shift of galaxies** | Light from distant galaxies is shifted to longer (red) wavelengths → galaxies moving away from us |
| **Hubble's Law** | More distant galaxies have greater red-shift → expanding universe |
| **Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation** | Faint microwave radiation detected uniformly from all directions → remnant heat from the Big Bang |

---

## Red-Shift

> When a source of light moves **away** from an observer, the observed wavelength is **longer** (shifted towards red end of spectrum). This is the **Doppler effect** applied to light.

$$z = \frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda} \approx \frac{v}{c}$$

- $z$ = redshift
- $\Delta\lambda$ = change in wavelength
- $\lambda$ = original wavelength
- $v$ = recession speed
- $c$ = speed of light

### Hubble's Law

$$v = H_0 d$$

- $v$ = recession speed of galaxy (km/s)
- $H_0$ = Hubble constant (~70 km/s/Mpc)
- $d$ = distance to galaxy (Mpc = megaparsec)

> The further away a galaxy, the faster it recedes — **universe is expanding**.

## Dark Matter and Dark Energy

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Dark matter** | Invisible matter that doesn't emit or absorb light; inferred from its gravitational effects on galaxies; ~27% of Universe |
| **Dark energy** | Unknown force causing the expansion of the Universe to **accelerate**; ~68% of Universe |
| **Ordinary matter** | Everything we can see (atoms, stars, planets); only ~5% of Universe |

---

## The Fate of the Universe

Depends on the total mass/energy content:
- **Big Freeze:** expansion continues forever → stars burn out → cold, dark Universe
- **Big Crunch:** gravity eventually halts expansion → Universe collapses
- Current evidence suggests expansion is **accelerating** → Big Freeze most likely
