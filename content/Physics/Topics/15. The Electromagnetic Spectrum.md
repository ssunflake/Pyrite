## What are Electromagnetic Waves?

All electromagnetic (EM) waves:
- Are **transverse** waves
- Travel at the **speed of light** in a vacuum: $c = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s
- Do **not** need a medium (travel through vacuum)
- Transfer energy
- Can be reflected, refracted, diffracted, and absorbed

---

## The Electromagnetic Spectrum

Ordered from **longest wavelength / lowest frequency** to **shortest wavelength / highest frequency**:

| Wave Type | Wavelength | Frequency | Energy per photon |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|------------------|
| Radio waves | 10 cm – 10 km | ~10⁴ – 10⁹ Hz | Lowest |
| Microwaves | 1 mm – 10 cm | ~10⁹ – 10¹² Hz | ↑ |
| Infrared (IR) | 700 nm – 1 mm | ~10¹¹ – 10¹⁴ Hz | ↑ |
| Visible light | 400 – 700 nm | ~4×10¹⁴ – 7×10¹⁴ Hz | ↑ |
| Ultraviolet (UV) | 10 – 400 nm | ~10¹⁵ – 10¹⁶ Hz | ↑ |
| X-rays | 0.01 – 10 nm | ~10¹⁶ – 10¹⁹ Hz | ↑ |
| Gamma rays | < 0.01 nm | > 10¹⁹ Hz | Highest |

> **v = fλ** at constant speed: as frequency increases, wavelength decreases.

---

## Visible Light

- Only part of the EM spectrum detectable by the human eye
- Wavelength range: approximately **400 nm (violet) to 700 nm (red)**
- **ROYGBIV:** Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, Violet
  - Red: longest wavelength, lowest frequency
  - Violet: shortest wavelength, highest frequency

---

## Radio Waves

- **Wavelength:** centimetres to kilometres
- **Source:** oscillating electrons in antennas, stars
- **Uses:**
  - TV and radio broadcasting
  - Mobile phones (longer wavelengths)
  - Radar (shorter radio waves)
  - Astronomy (radio telescopes)
- **Hazards:** None at typical exposure levels
- Diffract easily around hills and buildings (long wavelength)

---

## Microwaves

- **Wavelength:** 1 mm to ~30 cm
- **Source:** microwave ovens, satellites
- **Uses:**
  - Microwave cooking (water molecules absorb microwaves → heat food)
  - Satellite communication (pass through atmosphere)
  - Mobile phones
  - Radar (detecting aircraft, speed cameras)
- **Hazards:** Can heat body tissue at high intensities

---

## Infrared (IR) Radiation

- **Wavelength:** ~700 nm to 1 mm
- All objects **emit** infrared radiation; hotter objects emit more
- **Uses:**
  - Thermal imaging cameras
  - TV/device remote controls
  - Heating (infrared heaters)
  - Optical fibres (telecommunications)
  - Night vision equipment
  - Medical physiotherapy
- **Hazards:** Burns skin; can damage eyes

---

## Visible Light

- **Uses:**
  - Vision
  - Photography
  - Optical fibres (internet)
  - Lasers (surgery, cutting, barcode scanning)
- **Hazards:** Bright light can damage eyes; lasers are dangerous

---

## Ultraviolet (UV) Radiation

- **Wavelength:** 10 nm to 400 nm
- **Source:** Sun (main source), UV lamps
- **Uses:**
  - Security marking (invisible ink visible under UV)
  - Sterilising water and medical equipment
  - Detecting forged bank notes
  - Vitamin D production in skin (small amounts)
  - Sunbeds (tanning)
- **Hazards:**
  - Skin cancer
  - Premature skin ageing
  - Eye damage (cataracts, blindness)
  - Sunburn

---

## X-rays

- **Wavelength:** 0.01 nm to 10 nm
- **Source:** X-ray tubes (electrons decelerated rapidly)
- **Uses:**
  - Medical imaging (bones, teeth, tumours)
  - Airport security scanners
  - Industrial inspection of welds/structures
  - Treating cancer (radiotherapy)
- **Hazards:**
  - Ionising radiation → can damage/mutate cells → cancer
  - Medical staff wear lead aprons; patients have minimum necessary exposure

---

## Gamma Rays

- **Wavelength:** < 0.01 nm
- **Source:** Radioactive nuclei decaying (nuclear emissions)
- **Uses:**
  - Treating cancer (targeted gamma rays kill tumour cells)
  - Sterilising food, surgical instruments, medical equipment
  - Medical tracers (gamma-emitting isotopes)
  - Industrial thickness gauges
- **Hazards:**
  - Most ionising and most penetrating of all EM radiation
  - Can cause cancer, radiation sickness, cell damage
  - Requires thick lead or concrete shielding

---

## Ionising vs Non-Ionising Radiation

| Radiation | Ionising? | Risk Level |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Radio, Microwave | No | Low/None |
| Infrared, Visible | No | Low (unless intense) |
| Ultraviolet | Partially | Medium |
| X-rays | Yes | High |
| Gamma rays | Yes (most ionising) | Very high |

> **Ionising radiation** has enough energy to remove electrons from atoms → creates ions → can damage DNA → cancer or cell death.
