## 3.1 Non-metallic Substances and Covalent Bonding

> A **covalent bond** is the electrostatic attraction between two nuclei and the **shared pair of electrons** between them.

### Why Covalent Bonding Occurs
- Non-metals need to **gain electrons** to achieve a full outer shell
- They cannot transfer electrons (neither can easily give them up)
- Instead, atoms **share** electrons вЂ” both atoms count the shared pair as their own

### Types of Covalent Bonds

| Bond | Shared pairs | Examples |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Single bond | 1 pair (2 electrons) | HвЂ“H, CвЂ“H, OвЂ“H |
| Double bond | 2 pairs (4 electrons) | O=O, C=O, C=C |
| Triple bond | 3 pairs (6 electrons) | Nв‰ЎN, Cв‰ЎC |

## 3.3 Giant Structures

### Giant Ionic Lattice
- Ions arranged in a regular 3D lattice held by strong electrostatic attractions
- Ratio of ions determined by charge balance (e.g. NaCl: 1:1; MgClв‚‚: 1:2)
- Very high melting points

### Giant Covalent Structures

| Structure | Bonding | Properties | Uses |
|-----------|---------|-----------|------|
| **Diamond** | Each C bonded to 4 others tetrahedral | Very hard, high m.p., does not conduct | Cutting tools, jewellery |
| **Graphite** | Each C bonded to 3 others in layers; delocalised electrons | Soft/slippery (layers slide), conducts electricity | Lubricant, electrodes, pencils |
| **Silicon dioxide (SiOв‚‚)** | Each Si bonded to 4 O; each O to 2 Si | Very hard, high m.p., does not conduct | Glass, sand |
| **Graphene** | Single layer of graphite; delocalised electrons | Extremely strong, very light, conducts | Electronics, composites |

> **Diamond vs graphite:** Both are giant covalent structures of carbon. Diamond doesn't conduct (all electrons in bonds); graphite conducts (delocalised electrons between layers). Graphite is slippery because layers can slide.

### Metallic Bonding and Structure (Giant Metallic)

> **Metallic bonding** is the electrostatic attraction between a lattice of positive metal ions and a sea of delocalised (free) electrons.

| Property | Behaviour | Reason |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Electrical conductivity | **Good conductor** | Delocalised electrons carry charge |
| Thermal conductivity | **Good conductor** | Electrons and vibrating ions transfer energy |
| Malleability/ductility | **Can be shaped** | Layers of ions can slide without breaking the bond |
| High melting point | Generally high | Many strong metalвЂ“electron interactions |
| Lustre | Shiny | Delocalised electrons reflect light |
