## 5.1 The Mole and Avogadro's Constant

### The Mole

> One **mole** is the amount of substance that contains **6.02 × 10²³** particles (atoms, molecules, ions or formula units).

$$N_A = 6.02 \times 10^{23} \text{ mol}^{-1} \quad \text{(Avogadro's constant)}$$

### The Mole Triangle

$$\boxed{n = \frac{m}{M_r}} \quad \boxed{m = n \times M_r} \quad \boxed{M_r = \frac{m}{n}}$$

Where:
- $n$ = number of moles (mol)
- $m$ = mass (g)
- $M_r$ = relative molecular (or formula) mass (g/mol)

**Example:** How many moles are in 36 g of water (H₂O, Mᵣ = 18)?
$$n = \frac{36}{18} = 2 \text{ mol}$$

**Example:** What mass is 0.5 mol of CaCO₃ (Mᵣ = 100)?
$$m = 0.5 \times 100 = 50 \text{ g}$$

### Number of Particles

$$\text{Number of particles} = n \times N_A = n \times 6.02 \times 10^{23}$$

**Example:** How many molecules in 2 mol of CO₂?
$$2 \times 6.02 \times 10^{23} = 1.204 \times 10^{24} \text{ molecules}$$

### Molar Volume of Gases

At **room temperature and pressure (r.t.p.)** — 25°C, 1 atm:
$$V_m = 24 \text{ dm}^3/\text{mol} \quad (24{,}000 \text{ cm}^3/\text{mol})$$

At **standard temperature and pressure (s.t.p.)** — 0°C, 1 atm:
$$V_m = 22.4 \text{ dm}^3/\text{mol}$$

$$\boxed{n = \frac{V}{24}} \quad \boxed{V = n \times 24} \quad \text{(at r.t.p., V in dm}^3\text{)}$$

**Example:** Volume of 0.25 mol of gas at r.t.p.?
$$V = 0.25 \times 24 = 6 \text{ dm}^3$$

## 5.2 The Mole and Chemical Equations

### Stoichiometry (Mole Ratios)

The **coefficients** in a balanced equation give the **mole ratios** of reactants and products.

$$2\text{Mg} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{MgO}$$
→ 2 mol Mg reacts with 1 mol O₂ to produce 2 mol MgO

### Calculations from Equations — Method

1. Write balanced equation
2. Identify moles of **given** substance (using n = m/Mᵣ)
3. Use **mole ratio** from equation to find moles of **wanted** substance
4. Convert moles to mass (or volume) of wanted substance

**Example:** What mass of MgO is produced from 6 g of Mg?
$$2\text{Mg} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{MgO}$$
- n(Mg) = 6/24 = 0.25 mol
- Ratio Mg:MgO = 2:2 = 1:1
- n(MgO) = 0.25 mol
- m(MgO) = 0.25 × 40 = **10 g**

### Limiting Reagent

> The **limiting reagent** is the reactant that is completely used up first; it determines the maximum amount of product formed.

**Method:**
1. Find moles of each reactant
2. Divide by stoichiometric coefficient
3. **Smaller** result → limiting reagent

**Example:** 3 g H₂ + 32 g O₂ → H₂O  
$$2\text{H}_2 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{H}_2\text{O}$$
- n(H₂) = 3/2 = 1.5 mol → 1.5/2 = **0.75**
- n(O₂) = 32/32 = 1.0 mol → 1.0/1 = **1.0**
- Smaller ratio = H₂ → **H₂ is limiting reagent**

### Percentage Yield

$$\% \text{ yield} = \frac{\text{actual yield}}{\text{theoretical yield}} \times 100\%$$

Reasons yield < 100%: reversible reactions, product lost during transfer, side reactions, incomplete reaction.

### Percentage Purity

$$\% \text{ purity} = \frac{\text{mass of pure substance}}{\text{mass of impure sample}} \times 100\%$$

## 5.3 Moles and Solution Chemistry

### Concentration

$$\boxed{c = \frac{n}{V}} \quad \boxed{n = c \times V} \quad \boxed{V = \frac{n}{c}}$$

Where:
- $c$ = concentration (mol/dm³ or mol dm⁻³)
- $n$ = moles (mol)
- $V$ = volume in **dm³** (= litres; 1 dm³ = 1000 cm³ = 1 L)

> ⚠️ Always convert cm³ to dm³ before using: divide by 1000.

**Example:** What is the concentration of 0.1 mol NaOH in 250 cm³?
$$V = 250/1000 = 0.25 \text{ dm}^3$$
$$c = 0.1/0.25 = 0.4 \text{ mol/dm}^3$$

### Titration Calculations

Used to find unknown concentration from a known one.

**Method:**
1. Write balanced equation to get mole ratio
2. Calculate moles of **known** solution: n = c × V
3. Use mole ratio to find moles of **unknown**
4. Calculate concentration of unknown: c = n/V

**Example:** 25.0 cm³ of NaOH was neutralised by 20.0 cm³ of 0.10 mol/dm³ HCl.

$$\text{NaOH} + \text{HCl} \rightarrow \text{NaCl} + \text{H}_2\text{O}$$

- n(HCl) = 0.10 × (20.0/1000) = 0.002 mol
- Ratio 1:1 → n(NaOH) = 0.002 mol
- c(NaOH) = 0.002 / (25.0/1000) = **0.08 mol/dm³**

### Mass Concentration (g/dm³)

$$\text{mass concentration} = c \times M_r \quad \text{(g/dm}^3\text{)}$$

### The Full Mole Map

| Given | Formula | Find |
|-------|---------|------|
| Mass (g) | n = m ÷ Mᵣ | Moles |
| Volume of gas (dm³, r.t.p.) | n = V ÷ 24 | Moles |
| Volume of solution (dm³) | n = c × V | Moles |
| Number of particles | n = N ÷ Nₐ | Moles |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Mole | Amount of substance containing 6.02 × 10²³ particles |
| Avogadro's constant (Nₐ) | 6.02 × 10²³ mol⁻¹ |
| Molar mass | Mass of one mole of substance (numerically equal to Mᵣ in g/mol) |
| Stoichiometry | Quantitative relationship between reactants and products |
| Limiting reagent | Reactant that is completely consumed first; limits product yield |
| Concentration | Amount of solute per unit volume of solution (mol/dm³) |
