## 21.1 Experimental Design

### Key Concepts in Experimental Method

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Independent variable** | The variable you deliberately change |
| **Dependent variable** | The variable you measure/observe as a result |
| **Control variable** | All other variables kept constant to ensure a fair test |
| **Accuracy** | How close a result is to the true value |
| **Precision** | How close repeated results are to each other |
| **Reliability** | Consistent results when experiment repeated |
| **Validity** | Experiment actually tests what it claims to test |

### Safety in the Laboratory
- **Flammable** materials: keep away from flames; use fume cupboard
- **Corrosive** substances: wear gloves, goggles, lab coat
- **Toxic** gases: use fume cupboard (e.g. Cl₂, HCl, H₂S, SO₂, NH₃)
- **Concentrated acids/alkalis**: handle with care; neutralise spills
- Eye protection should be worn at all times

### Common Apparatus and Measurements

| Measuring equipment | Used for | Precision |
|--------------------|---------|-----------|
| Measuring cylinder | Volume of liquid | ±0.5 cm³ |
| Burette | Precise volume in titration | ±0.05 cm³ |
| Pipette (volumetric) | Fixed precise volume | ±0.1 cm³ |
| Balance | Mass | ±0.01 g |
| Thermometer | Temperature | ±0.5°C |
| Gas syringe | Volume of gas | ±1 cm³ |

## 21.2 Separation and Purification

The appropriate technique depends on the **nature and properties** of the mixture components:

### 1. Filtration

**Separates:** Insoluble solid from a liquid (suspension)

**Method:** Pour mixture through filter paper in a funnel; solid (residue) stays on paper; liquid (filtrate) passes through.

**Example:** CaCO₃(s) from water; sand from muddy water

---

### 2. Evaporation to Dryness

**Separates:** Dissolved solid from solution (when solid is stable when heated)

**Method:** Heat solution in evaporating dish until all solvent evaporates; solid remains.

**When to use:** When exact temperature doesn't matter (salt is not heat-sensitive)

**Example:** NaCl from NaCl(aq)

---

### 3. Crystallisation

**Separates:** Pure crystals of a dissolved solid from solution

**Method:**
1. Evaporate until solution is **saturated** (test: a crystal forms when glass rod is withdrawn)
2. Allow to cool slowly → large crystals form
3. Filter off crystals
4. Wash with minimum cold distilled water (remove impurities)
5. Dry by patting between filter paper or in oven at low temperature

**Advantage over evaporation:** Produces larger, purer crystals.

**Example:** CuSO₄ crystals from CuSO₄(aq)

---

### 4. Simple Distillation

**Separates:** Solvent (liquid) from a solution; or two liquids with **very different** boiling points (> ~25°C apart)

**Method:** Heat solution; solvent vapour rises, passes through condenser (cooled by water), liquid collected in flask.

**Example:** Obtaining pure water from salt water; separating water from ethanol (approximately)

**Apparatus:** Flask, Liebig condenser (water-cooled), receiving flask, thermometer at side-arm

---

### 5. Fractional Distillation

**Separates:** Miscible liquids with **similar boiling points** (< ~25°C apart)

**Method:** Like simple distillation but with a **fractionating column** packed with glass beads or rings.
- Multiple condensation/evaporation cycles in the column
- More volatile liquid (lower b.p.) reaches top of column first
- Collected in fractions

**Examples:**
- Separating ethanol/water (b.p. 78°C/100°C)
- Separating petroleum fractions in oil refinery

---

### 6. Paper Chromatography

**Separates:** Components of a dissolved mixture based on solubility in solvent and attraction to paper

Covered in detail in 21.3.

---

### 7. Solvent Extraction (Miscibility)

**Separates:** Immiscible liquids — e.g. oil from water using a separating funnel.

**Method:** Allow layers to separate in separating funnel; run off the denser layer from the tap; pour the top layer from the top.

---

### Purity Testing Methods

| Method | What it tests | How |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| Melting point | Purity of solid | Sharp melting point = pure; range/depression = impure |
| Boiling point | Purity of liquid | Constant boiling = pure |
| Chromatography | Single substance or mixture | Single spot = pure; multiple spots = mixture |

## 21.3 Chromatography

> **Chromatography** separates components of a mixture based on their different affinities for a **stationary phase** and a **mobile phase** (solvent).

### Principle

- **Stationary phase:** paper (cellulose fibres which adsorb water) or silica gel/alumina on TLC plate
- **Mobile phase:** solvent (ethanol, water, hexane, etc.) that moves through the stationary phase
- Components with **higher affinity for solvent** move further; those with **higher affinity for paper** move less

### Paper Chromatography — Procedure

1. Draw pencil baseline on chromatography paper (use pencil — not pen, it would dissolve)
2. Spot the mixture and known reference substances on the baseline
3. Place paper in solvent (below baseline — must not touch baseline spots)
4. Allow solvent to rise (capillary action) until near top
5. Mark solvent front with pencil
6. Remove paper, allow to dry
7. Identify spots: some visible; others require a **locating agent** (e.g. ninhydrin for amino acids, UV light for some substances)

### Rf Value

$$R_f = \frac{\text{distance moved by spot}}{\text{distance moved by solvent front}}$$

- Rf is always **between 0 and 1**
- Rf is characteristic of a compound **in a given solvent** — can be used to identify compounds
- Same Rf as a known standard → same compound

**Interpreting chromatograms:**
- **One spot** from a pure substance → pure compound
- **Multiple spots** → mixture
- Spot at same position as known standard → same compound present

### Thin-Layer Chromatography (TLC)
- Stationary phase = thin layer of silica gel or alumina on glass/aluminium plate
- More reproducible Rf values than paper chromatography
- Requires UV lamp or iodine vapour to visualise non-coloured spots

### Column Chromatography
- Used to separate and **collect** large amounts of separated components
- Column packed with silica gel; solvent flows down
- Fractions collected at bottom

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Filtration | Separation of insoluble solid from liquid using filter paper |
| Distillation | Separation of liquids by evaporation and condensation |
| Crystallisation | Formation of pure crystals from a saturated solution by cooling |
| Chromatography | Separation of mixture components by differential movement through stationary and mobile phases |
| Rf value | Distance moved by compound ÷ distance moved by solvent front |
| Stationary phase | Phase that does not move in chromatography (paper, silica) |
| Mobile phase | Solvent that moves through stationary phase in chromatography |
