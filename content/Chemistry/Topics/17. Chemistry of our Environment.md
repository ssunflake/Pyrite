## 17.1 Air Quality

### Composition of Clean Dry Air

| Gas | Approximate % by volume |
|-----|------------------------|
| Nitrogen (N₂) | 78% |
| Oxygen (O₂) | 21% |
| Argon (Ar) | ~0.9% |
| Carbon dioxide (CO₂) | ~0.04% |
| Other noble gases | Traces |

### Air Pollutants — Sources and Effects

| Pollutant | Formula | Main source | Effects | Removal/solution |
|-----------|---------|------------|---------|-----------------|
| **Carbon monoxide** | CO | Incomplete combustion (car engines, gas heaters) | Toxic — binds to haemoglobin; reduces O₂ transport; fatal at high concentrations | Catalytic converters; ensure complete combustion |
| **Sulfur dioxide** | SO₂ | Burning fossil fuels (containing S impurities); volcanic eruptions | Acid rain; respiratory irritant; damages buildings | Remove S from fuels; flue gas desulfurisation; use catalytic converter |
| **Nitrogen oxides** (NOₓ) | NO, NO₂ | High-temperature combustion in engines (N₂ + O₂ → 2NO) | Acid rain; smog; respiratory irritant | Catalytic converters |
| **Unburned hydrocarbons** | CₓHᵧ | Incomplete combustion | Photochemical smog; carcinogenic (some) | Catalytic converters |
| **Particulates** (soot/dust) | C (carbon) | Diesel engines, burning | Respiratory disease; climate effects; soils buildings | Filters; catalytic converters |
| **Lead compounds** | Pb | Formerly in leaded petrol | Neurotoxin; developmental problems in children | Removal of lead from petrol (now done in most countries) |
| **CFCs** | Various | Aerosols, refrigerants (old) | Ozone depletion | Replaced by HFCs |

### Acid Rain

**Formation:**
$$\text{S}(s) + \text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow \text{SO}_2(g)$$
$$2\text{SO}_2(g) + \text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow 2\text{SO}_3(g)$$
$$\text{SO}_3(g) + \text{H}_2\text{O}(l) \rightarrow \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4(aq)$$

For nitrogen oxides:
$$\text{N}_2(g) + \text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow 2\text{NO}(g) \quad \text{(in engine)}$$
$$4\text{NO}(g) + 3\text{O}_2(g) + 2\text{H}_2\text{O}(l) \rightarrow 4\text{HNO}_3(aq)$$

**Effects of acid rain:**
- Kills fish and aquatic life (acidifies lakes and rivers)
- Kills trees (damages leaf surfaces; leaches nutrients from soil)
- Corrodes buildings and statues (especially limestone and marble: CaCO₃ + H₂SO₄ → CaSO₄ + H₂O + CO₂)
- Damages metals

### Catalytic Converter
- Honeycomb of Pt/Rh/Pd catalyst
- Converts three main pollutants:
$$2\text{CO}(g) + 2\text{NO}(g) \rightarrow 2\text{CO}_2(g) + \text{N}_2(g)$$
$$\text{Unburned hydrocarbons} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{CO}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O}$$

## 17.2 Carbon Dioxide, Methane and Climate Change

### The Greenhouse Effect

**Natural greenhouse gases:** CO₂, CH₄, H₂O vapour, N₂O

**Mechanism:**
1. Solar radiation (short wavelength) passes through atmosphere → absorbed by Earth's surface
2. Earth re-emits longer wavelength infrared radiation
3. Greenhouse gases absorb IR → re-emit in all directions → atmosphere warms
4. This natural effect is **essential** for life (~33°C warmer than without it)

### Enhanced Greenhouse Effect

**Human sources of CO₂:**
- Burning fossil fuels: C + O₂ → CO₂; CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O
- Deforestation: fewer trees to absorb CO₂
- Cement production: CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂

**Human sources of CH₄:**
- Livestock (enteric fermentation), rice paddies, landfill, natural gas leaks

**Consequences of global warming:**
- Sea level rise (thermal expansion + ice melting)
- More extreme weather events
- Shifts in ecosystems, agricultural zones
- Ocean acidification: CO₂ + H₂O ⇌ H₂CO₃

### Reducing Carbon Emissions
- Renewable energy (solar, wind, tidal, hydro)
- Nuclear energy
- Carbon capture and storage (CCS)
- Energy efficiency improvements
- Electric vehicles
- Reforestation

## 17.3 Water

### The Water Cycle

Water constantly cycles: evaporation → condensation → precipitation → runoff/infiltration → repeat

### Sources of Water
- Rivers, lakes, reservoirs (fresh water)
- Groundwater (aquifers)
- Desalination of sea water (expensive — distillation or reverse osmosis)

### Water Purification (Treatment of Drinking Water)

**Main steps:**

| Step | Process | Purpose |
|------|---------|---------|
| **1. Sedimentation** | Water left in large tanks; large solid particles sink | Remove large suspended particles |
| **2. Filtration** | Water passed through layers of sand and gravel | Remove smaller suspended particles |
| **3. Chlorination** | Cl₂ gas or ClO₂ dissolved in water | Kill bacteria and microorganisms |

> ⚠️ **Sedimentation may also involve addition of aluminium sulfate (Al₂(SO₄)₃) as a coagulant** — causes fine particles to clump (flocculate) and settle.

**Why chlorination?**
- Cl₂ + H₂O → HCl + HClO (hypochlorous acid — kills bacteria)
- Very effective at killing pathogens
- Concerns: reaction with organic matter → trihalomethanes (carcinogens) at high levels
- Controlled to minimise risk; benefit far outweighs risk

### Distilled Water
- Water purified by distillation: pure but expensive
- Used in labs, not for drinking at scale
- Removes all dissolved substances including useful minerals

### Testing Water Purity
- Pure water: **sharp melting point of 0°C** and **boiling point of 100°C** at 1 atm
- Dissolved substances: depress freezing point; elevate boiling point

### Hard and Soft Water

| Type | Dissolved ions | Lathers with soap? | Scale in kettles? |
|------|--------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Soft water** | Low Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺ | Easily | Rarely |
| **Temporarily hard** | HCO₃⁻ present (Ca(HCO₃)₂) | Poorly | Yes (boiling removes hardness) |
| **Permanently hard** | SO₄²⁻ present (CaSO₄, MgSO₄) | Poorly | Boiling does NOT remove |

**Removing hardness:**
- Boiling (temporary hardness only): Ca(HCO₃)₂ → CaCO₃↓ + H₂O + CO₂
- Adding Na₂CO₃ (washing soda): Ca²⁺ + CO₃²⁻ → CaCO₃↓
- Ion exchange columns: replace Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ with Na⁺ or H⁺

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Acid rain | Precipitation with pH < 5.6 due to dissolved SO₂ and NOₓ |
| Greenhouse effect | Warming of Earth's surface due to greenhouse gases absorbing and re-emitting IR radiation |
| Hard water | Water containing dissolved Ca²⁺ and/or Mg²⁺ ions |
| Chlorination | Addition of chlorine to water to kill bacteria |
| Coagulation/flocculation | Addition of alum to cause fine particles to clump and settle |
