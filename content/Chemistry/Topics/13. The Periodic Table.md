## 13.1 Classifying the Elements

> The **Periodic Table** arranges elements in order of increasing **atomic number**, in rows (periods) and columns (groups) such that elements with similar properties appear in the same group.

### Structure of the Periodic Table

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Period** | Horizontal row — elements in the same period have the same number of electron shells |
| **Group** | Vertical column — elements in the same group have the same number of outer electrons → similar properties |
| **Metals** | Left and centre of the table |
| **Non-metals** | Top right of the table |
| **Metalloids (semimetals)** | Along the dividing "staircase" line (Si, Ge, As, Sb, Te) |

### Groups and Their Names

| Group | Name | Key elements |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Group 1 | Alkali metals | Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs |
| Group 2 | Alkaline earth metals | Be, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba |
| Groups 3–12 | Transition metals | Fe, Cu, Zn, Ni, Cr, Mn, Ag, Au, Pt |
| Group 17 (7) | Halogens | F, Cl, Br, I, At |
| Group 18 (0) | Noble gases | He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe |

### Transition Metals — Special Properties
- High density, high melting points
- Form **coloured compounds** (e.g. CuSO₄ = blue, Fe₂O₃ = red-brown, K₂Cr₂O₇ = orange)
- Form ions with **variable oxidation states** (e.g. Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺, Cu⁺/Cu²⁺, Mn²⁺/Mn⁷⁺)
- Good **catalysts** (Fe in Haber, V₂O₅ in Contact, Pt in catalytic converters, MnO₂ in H₂O₂ decomposition)

## 13.2 Trends in Groups

### Group 1 — Alkali Metals (Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs)

**Electronic configuration:** All have 1 outer electron → readily lose it → very reactive metals

| Property | Trend down Group 1 |
|----------|-------------------|
| Reactivity | Increases (outer electron further from nucleus, less shielded — easier to lose) |
| Melting/boiling point | Decreases |
| Density | Generally increases (except Li < Na > K oddity) |
| Atomic radius | Increases |

**Reactions with water:**
$$2\text{Na}(s) + 2\text{H}_2\text{O}(l) \rightarrow 2\text{NaOH}(aq) + \text{H}_2(g)$$
- Li: reacts steadily, floats
- Na: reacts vigorously, melts into ball, moves around
- K: reacts violently, H₂ ignites with lilac flame
- Rb, Cs: explosive

**Reactions with oxygen:**
$$4\text{Na}(s) + \text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow 2\text{Na}_2\text{O}(s)$$
(Stored under oil to prevent reactions with air and water)

**Reactions with chlorine:**
$$2\text{Na}(s) + \text{Cl}_2(g) \rightarrow 2\text{NaCl}(s)$$

### Group 17 — Halogens (F, Cl, Br, I)

**Electronic configuration:** All have 7 outer electrons → readily gain 1 → form X⁻ ions

| Property | Trend down Group 17 |
|----------|---------------------|
| Reactivity | Decreases (harder to attract extra electron further from nucleus) |
| Melting/boiling point | Increases (stronger van der Waals forces) |
| Colour | Paler → darker (F₂ = pale yellow, Cl₂ = green, Br₂ = brown, I₂ = grey/purple) |
| State at r.t.p. | F₂, Cl₂ = gas; Br₂ = liquid; I₂ = solid |

**Halogen displacement reactions:**
A more reactive halogen displaces a less reactive one from its salt solution.

| Reactants | Observation | Equation |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Cl₂(aq) + KBr(aq) | Solution turns orange (Br₂ forms) | Cl₂ + 2KBr → 2KCl + Br₂ |
| Cl₂(aq) + KI(aq) | Solution turns brown/black (I₂ forms) | Cl₂ + 2KI → 2KCl + I₂ |
| Br₂(aq) + KI(aq) | Solution turns brown (I₂ forms) | Br₂ + 2KI → 2KBr + I₂ |
| Br₂(aq) + KCl(aq) | No reaction | — |
| I₂(aq) + KBr(aq) | No reaction | — |

**Uses of halogens:**
- Cl₂: water treatment (kills bacteria), making PVC and bleach
- Br₂: pesticides, photographic film
- I₂: antiseptic (iodine solution), testing for starch

### Group 18 — Noble Gases (He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe)

- Full outer electron shell → extremely stable → very unreactive
- Exist as monatomic gases
- Colourless, odourless
- Uses: He (balloons, airships, MRI cooling), Ne (advertising signs), Ar (inert atmosphere for welding, light bulbs), Kr/Xe (lighting)

## 13.3 Trends Across a Period

### Period 3 (Na → Ar)

| Element | Na | Mg | Al | Si | P | S | Cl | Ar |
|---------|----|----|----|----|---|---|----|----|
| Type | Metal | Metal | Metal | Metalloid | Non-metal | Non-metal | Non-metal | Noble gas |
| Outer e⁻ | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 |
| Oxide type | Basic (ionic) | Basic | Amphoteric | Acidic | Acidic | Acidic | Acidic | — |

| Property | Trend across Period 3 (left → right) |
|----------|-------------------------------------|
| Atomic radius | Decreases (more protons pull electrons closer) |
| Ionisation energy | Generally increases (more protons, same shells) |
| Metallic character | Decreases |
| Non-metallic character | Increases |
| Electronegativity | Increases |

**Oxides across Period 3:**

| Oxide | Type | Reaction with water |
|-------|------|-------------------|
| Na₂O | Basic ionic | Na₂O + H₂O → 2NaOH (alkaline) |
| MgO | Basic ionic | MgO + H₂O → Mg(OH)₂ (slightly alkaline) |
| Al₂O₃ | Amphoteric | Reacts with both acids and bases |
| SiO₂ | Acidic (giant covalent) | Insoluble in water; reacts with bases |
| P₄O₁₀ | Acidic | P₄O₁₀ + 6H₂O → 4H₃PO₄ (acidic) |
| SO₃ | Acidic | SO₃ + H₂O → H₂SO₄ (strongly acidic) |
| Cl₂O₇ | Acidic | Cl₂O₇ + H₂O → 2HClO₄ (strongly acidic) |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Period | Horizontal row in the Periodic Table; same number of electron shells |
| Group | Vertical column; same number of outer electrons; similar chemical properties |
| Alkali metals | Group 1 elements; very reactive metals that lose 1 electron easily |
| Halogens | Group 17 elements; reactive non-metals that gain 1 electron |
| Noble gases | Group 18; full outer shells; very unreactive |
| Transition metals | d-block elements; form coloured compounds, variable oxidation states, good catalysts |
