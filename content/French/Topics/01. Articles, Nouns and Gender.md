## 1.1 Grammatical Gender

Every French noun is either **masculine** or **feminine** — there is no neuter gender. While there are no perfect rules, certain **noun endings** provide strong clues.

### Common Masculine Endings

| Ending | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| -age | le voyage, le garage, le fromage, le langage, le courage |
| -ment | le gouvernement, le moment, le bâtiment, le médicament |
| -eur | le chanteur, le professeur, le moteur, le bonheur |
| -eau | le gâteau, le chapeau, le tableau, le bateau, le bureau |
| -isme | le tourisme, le journalisme, le romantisme, le racisme |
| -et | le buffet, le poulet, le billet, le robinet, le jouet |
| -ier | le fermier, le cahier, le quartier, le panier |
| -oir | le miroir, le couloir, le pouvoir, le devoir |
| -ing | le camping, le parking, le shopping, le footing |

### Common Feminine Endings

| Ending | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| -tion | la nation, la solution, la question, la révolution, la situation |
| -sion | la décision, la télévision, la compréhension, la passion |
| -ance | la chance, la danse, la tolérance, la distance, la naissance |
| -ence | la patience, la violence, la conférence, la différence |
| -ette | la chaussette, la baguette, la bicyclette, la fourchette |
| -ie | la pharmacie, la boulangerie, la magie, la géographie |
| -ure | la nature, la culture, la lecture, la nourriture, la voiture |
| -esse | la jeunesse, la largesse, la politesse, la tristesse |
| -euse | la chanteuse, la vendeuse, la chercheuse |
| -ité | la qualité, la liberté, la société, la réalité, la beauté |

> [!note] These are patterns, not absolute rules. Exceptions exist: *le problème* ends in -e but is masculine; *la main* (hand) follows no pattern and is feminine. When in doubt, always check a dictionary — and write the article alongside every new noun.

## 1.2 Definite Articles (The)

The definite article changes depending on the gender, number, and initial sound of the noun.

| | Masculine | Feminine | Plural |
|-|-----------|----------|--------|
| Before consonant | **le** | **la** | **les** |
| Before vowel or silent h | **l'** | **l'** | **les** |

**Examples:**
- *le livre* (the book) — masculine singular, begins with consonant
- *la table* (the table) — feminine singular, begins with consonant
- *l'école* (the school) — feminine singular, begins with vowel → l'
- *l'homme* (the man) — masculine singular, begins with **silent** h → l'
- *le hibou* (the owl) — masculine singular, begins with **aspirate** h → le (no elision)
- *les livres / les tables* (the books / the tables) — plural

### Contractions with de and à

When *de* or *à* is followed by *le* or *les*, they contract. *La* and *l'* do NOT contract.

| Combination | Result | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|---------|
| de + le | **du** | of the / from the | *Je viens **du** marché.* |
| de + les | **des** | of the / from the | *Elle parle **des** professeurs.* |
| de + la | de la | (no contraction) | *Je viens **de la** boulangerie.* |
| de + l' | de l' | (no contraction) | *Je viens **de l'**école.* |
| à + le | **au** | to the / at the | *Je vais **au** supermarché.* |
| à + les | **aux** | to the / at the | *Elle parle **aux** enfants.* |
| à + la | à la | (no contraction) | *Je vais **à la** piscine.* |
| à + l' | à l' | (no contraction) | *Je vais **à l'**aéroport.* |

> 💡 **Exam tip:** Contractions are automatic — you CANNOT write *Je vais à le cinéma*. It must be *au cinéma*. This applies in all contexts, including after verbs of motion, location, and possession (*du pain* not *de le pain*).

## 1.3 Indefinite Articles (A / An / Some)

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|-|-----------|----------|
| Singular | **un** | **une** |
| Plural | **des** | **des** |

**Examples:**
- *un garçon* — a boy
- *une fille* — a girl
- *des amis* — some friends / friends

> [!note] After a **negative**, *un/une/des* becomes **de** (or **d'** before a vowel):
> - *J'ai un chien.* → *Je n'ai **pas de** chien.* (I don't have a dog.)
> - *Il mange des pommes.* → *Il **ne mange pas de** pommes.* (He doesn't eat apples.)

---

## 1.4 Partitive Articles (Some — Uncountable)

The partitive article is used for uncountable quantities — things you can't easily count individually.

| | Masculine | Feminine | Before vowel / silent h |
|-|-----------|----------|------------------------|
| Partitive | **du** | **de la** | **de l'** |

**Examples:**
- *Je mange **du** pain.* — I eat (some) bread. (masculine)
- *Elle boit **de la** limonade.* — She drinks (some) lemonade. (feminine)
- *Il boit **de l'**eau.* — He drinks (some) water. (before vowel)
- *Tu veux **des** légumes ?* — Do you want (some) vegetables? (plural)

### When to Use Just 'de / d''

Replace partitive/indefinite articles with simply **de/d'**:

1. **After a negative:** *Je ne mange **pas de** viande.* (I don't eat meat.)
2. **After expressions of quantity:**

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| beaucoup de | a lot of |
| peu de | a little / few |
| assez de | enough of |
| trop de | too much / many |
| un kilo de | a kilo of |
| une bouteille de | a bottle of |
| un verre de | a glass of |
| un morceau de | a piece of |
| une tranche de | a slice of |
| plusieurs | several (no *de* needed) |

**Examples:**
- *J'ai **beaucoup de** devoirs.* — I have a lot of homework.
- *Elle boit **peu d'**eau.* — She drinks little water.
- *Il y a **trop de** bruit.* — There is too much noise.

> 💡 **Exam tip:** A very common error is writing *beaucoup **des*** or *assez **des***. After quantity expressions, it is always **de/d'** — never *du/de la/des*.

## 1.5 Making Nouns Plural

### General Rule
Add **-s** to the singular form (the -s is usually not pronounced):
- *le chat* → *les chats*
- *la maison* → *les maisons*
- *le professeur* → *les professeurs*

### Exceptions and Special Patterns

| Rule | Singular ending | Plural ending | Example |
|------|----------------|---------------|---------|
| Most nouns | any | + -s | *la ville* → *les villes* |
| Already ends in -s, -x, -z | -s / -x / -z | no change | *le bras* → *les bras*; *la voix* → *les voix* |
| -al → -aux | -al | -aux | *le journal* → *les journaux* |
| -eau → -eaux | -eau | -eaux | *le gâteau* → *les gâteaux* |
| -eu → -eux | -eu | -eux | *le jeu* → *les jeux* |
| -ou (7 exceptions) → -oux | -ou | -oux | *le genou* → *les genoux* |

**The 7 -ou → -oux exceptions** (learn by heart):
*bijou, caillou, chou, genou, hibou, joujou, pou* (jewel, pebble, cabbage, knee, owl, toy, louse)

**Exceptions to -al → -aux:**
- *le bal* → *les bals* (ball/dance)
- *le festival* → *les festivals*
- *le carnaval* → *les carnavals*

**Completely irregular plurals:**
- *l'œil* → *les yeux* (eye → eyes)
- *monsieur* → *messieurs* (Mr → Messrs)
- *madame* → *mesdames*
- *mademoiselle* → *mesdemoiselles*

## 1.6 Feminine Forms of Nouns

Many nouns referring to people have separate masculine and feminine forms.

### General Rule
Add **-e** to the masculine to get the feminine (if the masculine does not already end in -e):
- *un ami* → *une amie*
- *un étudiant* → *une étudiante*
- *un Français* → *une Française*

### Patterns and Irregular Forms

| Masculine ending | Feminine ending | Masculine | Feminine | English |
|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|----------|---------|
| -er | -ère | le boulanger | la boulangère | baker |
| -eur (action doer) | -euse | le chanteur | la chanteuse | singer |
| -teur | -trice | le directeur | la directrice | director |
| -teur | -trice | l'acteur | l'actrice | actor |
| -ien | -ienne | le musicien | la musicienne | musician |
| -ien | -ienne | le lycéen | la lycéenne | secondary school student |
| -on | -onne | le patron | la patronne | boss |
| -el | -elle | le colonel | la colonelle | colonel |
| -et | -ette | le cadet | la cadette | junior |
| -x | -se | l'époux | l'épouse | spouse |
| same form | same form | le/la journaliste | le/la journaliste | journalist |
| same form | same form | le/la secrétaire | le/la secrétaire | secretary |
| irregular | | le mari | la femme | husband / wife |
| irregular | | le père | la mère | father / mother |
| irregular | | le frère | la sœur | brother / sister |
| irregular | | le fils | la fille | son / daughter |
| irregular | | le roi | la reine | king / queen |

> [!note] *Le professeur* can refer to a female teacher; the official feminine form *la professeure* is now standard in France. *Le médecin* is increasingly being replaced by *la médecin/médecine* for women, but *le médecin* still appears in texts.

## 1.7 Collective and Compound Nouns (Brief Overview)

Some French nouns are used collectively (always singular or always plural in certain contexts):
- *la foule* (the crowd), *le public* (the audience), *la police* (the police)

**Compound nouns** often include a hyphen. Pluralisation follows their grammatical structure:
- *la grand-mère* → *les grands-mères* (both elements agree)
- *le chef-d'œuvre* → *les chefs-d'œuvre* (only the first element)
- *un gratte-ciel* → *des gratte-ciel* (verb + noun: only noun part pluralises — sometimes unchanged)

> 💡 **Exam tip:** At IGCSE level, the most important thing is to consistently use the **correct article** (*le, la, l', les*) with every noun. The article is often your clearest signal of a word's gender, and will trigger correct adjective agreement in your writing.

## Key Definitions

| Term | Définition |
|------|-----------|
| Genre (gender) | The grammatical classification of a noun as masculine or feminine |
| Article défini | Definite article: *le, la, l', les* (= the) |
| Article indéfini | Indefinite article: *un, une, des* (= a / an / some) |
| Article partitif | Partitive article: *du, de la, de l'* (= some / any — uncountable) |
| Contraction | Merging of two words: *de + le = du*; *à + le = au*; *à + les = aux*; *de + les = des* |
| Pluriel | Plural form of a noun (add -s in most cases; exceptions: -aux, -eaux, -eux) |
| Féminin | Feminine form of a noun (add -e in most cases; many irregular patterns) |
| Nom commun | Common noun — a person, place, thing, or idea |
| Nom propre | Proper noun — a specific name (capitalised: Paris, Marie) |
| Élision | Dropping of a final vowel (le/la → l') before a vowel or silent h |
