## 3.1 Adjective Agreement

In French, **adjectives must agree** in gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural) with the noun they describe.

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|-|-----------|----------|
| Singular | petit | petite |
| Plural | petits | petites |

### General Rules

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Add **-e** for feminine (if not already ending in -e) | *grand* → *grande* |
| Add **-s** for masculine plural | *petit* → *petits* |
| Add **-es** for feminine plural | *petite* → *petites* |
| If masculine ends in **-e**, feminine = same form | *timide* → *timide* |
| If masculine ends in **-s** or **-x**, plural = same form | *gris* → *gris* |

**Examples:**
- *un grand garçon* / *une grande fille* — a tall boy / girl
- *un livre intéressant* / *une histoire intéressante* — an interesting book / story
- *un ami fidèle* / *une amie fidèle* — a faithful friend (no change — already ends in -e)

## 3.3 Completely Irregular Adjectives

### Beau, Nouveau, Vieux — Three Forms

These three adjectives have a special **third form** used before masculine nouns beginning with a vowel or silent *h*:

| Masc. (consonant) | Masc. (vowel/h) | Feminine | Masc. Plural | Fem. Plural |
|-------------------|-----------------|----------|-------------|------------|
| beau | **bel** | belle | beaux | belles |
| nouveau | **nouvel** | nouvelle | nouveaux | nouvelles |
| vieux | **vieil** | vieille | vieux | vieilles |

**Examples:**
- *un **beau** jardin* / *un **bel** homme* / *une **belle** maison*
- *un **nouveau** film* / *un **nouvel** appartement* / *une **nouvelle** voiture*
- *un **vieux** château* / *un **vieil** ami* / *une **vieille** tradition*

## 3.4 Adjective Position

Most French adjectives go **after** the noun. A specific group of very common adjectives go **before** the noun.

### BAGS Adjectives (Before the Noun)

| Category | Adjectives |
|----------|-----------|
| **B**eauty | beau (beautiful), joli (pretty), mignon (cute), laid (ugly) |
| **A**ge | jeune (young), vieux (old), nouveau (new), ancien (former/old) |
| **G**oodness | bon (good), mauvais (bad), meilleur (better/best), gentil (kind) |
| **S**ize | grand (big/tall), petit (small), gros (fat/big), long (long), court (short), haut (high), bas (low) |

**Examples of BAGS adjectives before the noun:**
- *une **belle** maison* — a beautiful house
- *un **vieux** monsieur* — an old gentleman
- *un **bon** repas* — a good meal
- *une **petite** voiture* — a small car

**Examples of other adjectives after the noun:**
- *un film **intéressant*** — an interesting film
- *un pull **rouge*** — a red jumper
- *une femme **française*** — a French woman
- *une table **ronde*** — a round table

## 3.5 Adjectives That Change Meaning with Position

Some adjectives have **different meanings** depending on their position:

| Adjective | Before noun | After noun |
|-----------|------------|-----------|
| **ancien** | former (*mon **ancien** prof* = my former teacher) | ancient/old (*un prof **ancien***) |
| **cher** | dear/beloved (*ma **chère** amie* = my dear friend) | expensive (*un livre **cher***) |
| **même** | same (*le **même** problème*) | very/itself (*le jour **même***) |
| **pauvre** | poor/pitiful (*ce **pauvre** garçon!* = that poor boy!) | not wealthy (*une famille **pauvre***) |
| **propre** | own (*ma **propre** chambre* = my own room) | clean (*une chambre **propre***) |
| **seul** | only/single (*la **seule** solution*) | alone (*un homme **seul***) |
| **certain** | some (*un **certain** temps*) | sure (*c'est **certain***) |
| **grand** | great (*un **grand** homme* = a great man) | tall/big (*un homme **grand***) |
| **dernier** | last (in a series: *la **dernière** fois* = the last time) | most recent (*la semaine **dernière*** = last week) |

> 💡 **Exam tip:** The position of *dernier* is particularly important. *La dernière semaine* = the final week (of a series). *La semaine dernière* = last week (most recent). This distinction comes up in reading comprehension.

## 3.6 Comparatives

### Comparing Adjectives

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **plus** + adj + **que** | more ... than | *Il est **plus grand que** moi.* — He is taller than me. |
| **moins** + adj + **que** | less ... than | *Elle est **moins timide que** lui.* — She is less shy than him. |
| **aussi** + adj + **que** | as ... as | *Je suis **aussi intelligent que** toi.* — I am as intelligent as you. |

### Irregular Comparatives

| Adjective | Comparative | English |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| bon(ne) | **meilleur(e)** | better |
| mauvais(e) | **pire** (or *plus mauvais*) | worse |
| petit(e) (physical size) | **plus petit(e)** | smaller |
| petit(e) (importance) | **moindre** | lesser |

> [!note] *Meilleur* must agree with the noun it describes: *une **meilleure** idée* (a better idea), *de **meilleurs** résultats* (better results).

## 3.7 Superlatives

### Forming Superlatives

| Structure | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **le / la / les plus** + adj | the most ... |
| **le / la / les moins** + adj | the least ... |

The article must agree with the noun:
- *le film **le plus** intéressant* — the most interesting film
- *la fille **la plus** intelligente* — the most intelligent girl
- *les résultats **les meilleurs*** — the best results

### Superlative Position

- If the adjective normally goes **after** the noun: article + noun + article + plus/moins + adj
  - *l'élève **la plus** travailleuse* (the hardest-working student)
- If the adjective normally goes **before** the noun: le/la/les + plus/moins + adj + noun
  - *le **plus beau** tableau* (the most beautiful painting)

After a superlative, use **de** (NOT *dans*) to mean "in":
- *le plus grand pays **du** monde* — the biggest country in the world
- *la meilleure élève **de** la classe* — the best student in the class

## 3.8 Adverb Formation

Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. Most French adverbs are formed from adjectives.

### Regular Formation: Feminine adjective + -ment

| Masculine adj | Feminine adj | Adverb | English |
|--------------|-------------|--------|---------|
| lent | lente | **lentement** | slowly |
| heureux | heureuse | **heureusement** | happily / fortunately |
| doux | douce | **doucement** | softly / gently |
| complet | complète | **complètement** | completely |
| actif | active | **activement** | actively |
| naturel | naturelle | **naturellement** | naturally |
| sérieux | sérieuse | **sérieusement** | seriously |

### Adjectives Already Ending in a Vowel

Take the masculine form and add -ment directly:
- *vrai* → **vraiment** (truly)
- *absolu* → **absolument** (absolutely)
- *poli* → **poliment** (politely)

### Adjectives Ending in -ant or -ent

Change -ant → **-amment** and -ent → **-emment** (both pronounced the same: *-amment*):

| Adjective | Adverb | English |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| courant | **couramment** | fluently |
| évident | **évidemment** | obviously / evidently |
| prudent | **prudemment** | carefully / cautiously |
| récent | **récemment** | recently |
| suffisant | **suffisamment** | sufficiently |
| fréquent | **fréquemment** | frequently |

### Irregular / Unpredictable Adverbs

| Related adjective | Adverb | English |
|------------------|--------|---------|
| bon (good) | **bien** | well |
| mauvais (bad) | **mal** | badly |
| (rapide / vite) | **vite** | fast / quickly |
| — | **beaucoup** | a lot |
| peu | **peu** | a little |
| — | **très** | very |
| — | **trop** | too much |
| — | **assez** | quite / enough |

## 3.9 Adverb Position

### Modifying a Verb

**Short, common adverbs** (*bien, mal, vite, souvent, toujours, déjà, encore, beaucoup, peu, trop*) go **between the auxiliary and past participle** in compound tenses:
- *J'ai **beaucoup** travaillé.* (I worked a lot.)
- *Nous avons **bien** dormi.* (We slept well.)
- *Il a **déjà** fini.* (He has already finished.)

**Longer adverbs** (-ment adverbs) usually go **after the past participle**:
- *Il a parlé **lentement**.* (He spoke slowly.)
- *Elle a répondu **poliment**.* (She answered politely.)

In **simple tenses**, adverbs usually follow the verb directly:
- *Il parle **bien**.* (He speaks well.)
- *Elle travaille **souvent** le soir.* (She often works in the evening.)

### Modifying an Adjective or Another Adverb

Place the adverb **directly before** the adjective/adverb it modifies:
- ***très** intéressant* (very interesting)
- ***assez** rapidement* (quite quickly)
- ***vraiment** beau* (really beautiful)

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Adjectif | Adjective — a word that describes a noun; must agree in gender and number |
| Accord | Agreement — adjective matching the noun in gender and number |
| BAGS | Mnemonic for adjectives placed BEFORE the noun: Beauty, Age, Goodness, Size |
| Comparatif | Comparative form: *plus/moins/aussi* + adj + *que* |
| Superlatif | Superlative form: *le/la/les plus/moins* + adj (+ *de* for "in") |
| Adverbe | Adverb — modifies a verb, adjective, or adverb; typically ends in -ment |
| Meilleur | Better / best — irregular comparative / superlative of *bon* |
| Pire | Worse / worst — irregular comparative / superlative of *mauvais* |
| Bien | Well — irregular adverb from *bon* |
| Mal | Badly — irregular adverb from *mauvais* |
