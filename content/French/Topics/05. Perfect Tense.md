## 5.1 When to Use Le Passé Composé

The passé composé is used for:
1. **Completed actions** at a specific point in the past
2. **A sequence of events** (first he did X, then Y, then Z)
3. **Actions with a defined start or end**
4. **Sudden changes** of state or reactions
5. **Single occurrences** (as opposed to repeated habits)

### Time Expressions That Signal Passé Composé

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| hier | yesterday |
| avant-hier | the day before yesterday |
| ce matin | this morning |
| la semaine dernière | last week |
| l'année dernière | last year |
| il y a + time | ago (*il y a deux jours* = two days ago) |
| un jour / un soir | one day / one evening |
| soudain / tout à coup | suddenly |
| d'abord ... puis ... enfin | first ... then ... finally |
| une fois / deux fois | once / twice |

## 5.2 Formation of the Passé Composé

**Formula:** Subject + **auxiliary** (avoir or être, present tense) + **past participle**

### Forming the Past Participle from Regular Verbs

| Infinitive type | Rule | Infinitive | Past Participle |
|----------------|------|-----------|----------------|
| -ER verbs | remove -er, add -**é** | parler | parl**é** |
| -IR verbs | remove -ir, add -**i** | finir | fin**i** |
| -RE verbs | remove -re, add -**u** | vendre | vend**u** |

**Examples with avoir:**

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| *J'ai **parlé**.* | I spoke / I have spoken. |
| *Tu as **fini**.* | You finished. |
| *Il a **vendu** sa voiture.* | He sold his car. |
| *Nous avons **mangé**.* | We ate / have eaten. |

## 5.4 Verbs That Take ÊTRE — DR & MRS VANDERTRAMPP

Most verbs use **avoir**. A specific set use **être** — memorise them with the mnemonic:

| Letter | Verb | Past Participle | English |
|--------|------|----------------|---------|
| **D** | Devenir | devenu(e) | to become |
| **R** | Revenir | revenu(e) | to come back |
| **M** | Monter | monté(e) | to go up |
| **R** | Rentrer | rentré(e) | to return home |
| **S** | Sortir | sorti(e) | to go out |
| **V** | Venir | venu(e) | to come |
| **A** | Aller | allé(e) | to go |
| **N** | Naître | né(e) | to be born |
| **D** | Descendre | descendu(e) | to go down |
| **E** | Entrer | entré(e) | to enter |
| **R** | Rester | resté(e) | to stay |
| **T** | Tomber | tombé(e) | to fall |
| **R** | Retourner | retourné(e) | to return |
| **A** | Arriver | arrivé(e) | to arrive |
| **M** | Mourir | mort(e) | to die |
| **P** | Partir | parti(e) | to leave |
| **P** | Passer | passé(e) | to pass (by) |

> [!note] Several of these verbs (*monter, descendre, sortir, rentrer, passer, retourner*) can also take **avoir** when they have a **direct object**, which changes their meaning:
> - *Elle est **montée**.* — She went up. (no object, être)
> - *Elle **a monté** les valises.* — She brought the suitcases up. (direct object, avoir)

## 5.5 Agreement of Past Participle with Être Verbs

When a verb is conjugated with **être**, the past participle agrees with the **subject** in gender and number — like an adjective.

| Subject | Agreement | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Masculine singular | no change | *Il est parti.* |
| Feminine singular | add **-e** | *Elle est **partie**.* |
| Masculine plural | add **-s** | *Ils sont **partis**.* |
| Feminine plural | add **-es** | *Elles sont **parties**.* |

**More examples:**
- *Marie est **née** en 2005.* — Marie was born in 2005.
- *Les garçons sont **arrivés** en retard.* — The boys arrived late.
- *Mes sœurs sont **restées** à la maison.* — My sisters stayed at home.
- *Nous sommes **allés** au cinéma.* — We went to the cinema. (mixed group → masculine plural)

---

## 5.6 Agreement of Past Participle with Avoir (Preceding Direct Object)

With **avoir** verbs, the past participle normally does **NOT** agree with the subject. However, when the **direct object comes BEFORE the verb** (via a pronoun or relative clause), the past participle agrees with that preceding direct object.

| Situation | Rule | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Direct object after verb | No agreement | *J'ai acheté **la robe**.* |
| Direct object pronoun before verb | Agreement | *Je **l'**ai achet**ée**.* |
| Relative clause with *que* | Agreement | *La robe **que** j'ai achet**ée**...* |

**Examples:**
- *Où sont les clés ? Je **les** ai vu**es** sur la table.* (les = feminine plural → vues)
- *La lettre **qu'**elle a écrit**e** était longue.* (que = la lettre, feminine singular → écrite)
- *Les garçons **que** j'ai rencontré**s**...* (que = les garçons, masculine plural → rencontrés)

> 💡 **Exam tip:** In writing, this agreement must be correct for full marks. Check: is there a *que* or pronoun (le/la/les/me/te/nous/vous) before the past participle? If so, make the past participle agree.

## 5.7 Pronominal Verbs in the Passé Composé

Pronominal (reflexive) verbs ALWAYS use **être** as auxiliary.

**se lever — full passé composé:**

| Pronoun | Form |
|---------|------|
| je | **me suis** levé(e) |
| tu | **t'es** levé(e) |
| il / on | **s'est** levé |
| elle | **s'est** levée |
| nous | **nous sommes** levé(e)s |
| vous | **vous êtes** levé(e)(s) |
| ils | **se sont** levés |
| elles | **se sont** levées |

> [!note] If the reflexive pronoun is a **COI** (indirect object), there is NO agreement:
> - *Elles se sont **parlé**.* (They talked to each other — *se parler à* — se = indirect object, no agreement)
> - *Elle s'est **lavée**.* (She washed herself — se = direct object → agreement)
> - *Elle s'est **lavé** les mains.* (She washed her hands — les mains is the direct object, not se → no agreement)

## 5.8 Negative in the Passé Composé

The **ne ... pas** wraps around the **auxiliary** (not the whole verb phrase):

| Type | Example | English |
|------|---------|---------|
| ne ... pas | *Je **n'**ai **pas** mangé.* | I didn't eat. |
| ne ... jamais | *Il **n'**a **jamais** dit ça.* | He never said that. |
| ne ... rien | *Elle **n'**a **rien** fait.* | She did nothing. |
| ne ... plus | *Nous **ne** sommes **plus** sortis.* | We didn't go out anymore. |
| ne ... personne | *Je **n'**ai vu **personne**.* | I saw nobody. (*personne* goes AFTER the past participle) |
| ne ... que | *Il **n'**a mangé **qu'**une pomme.* | He only ate one apple. |

> [!note] *Personne* and *nulle part* go **after** the past participle, unlike most negatives which go between the auxiliary and past participle.

## 5.9 Questions in the Passé Composé

| Method | Example | English |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Intonation (informal) | *Tu as mangé ?* | Did you eat? |
| Est-ce que | *Est-ce que tu as mangé ?* | Did you eat? |
| Inversion | *As-tu mangé ?* | Did you eat? |
| Question word + est-ce que | *Qu'est-ce que tu as mangé ?* | What did you eat? |
| Question word + inversion | *Qu'as-tu mangé ?* | What did you eat? |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Passé composé | Perfect tense — for completed past actions; formed with auxiliary + past participle |
| Participe passé | Past participle — formed from the infinitive (-é, -i, -u + many irregular forms) |
| Auxiliaire | Auxiliary verb (*avoir* or *être*) used to form the passé composé |
| DR & MRS VANDERTRAMPP | Mnemonic for the verbs that take *être* in the passé composé |
| Accord du participe passé | Agreement of the past participle: with subject (être verbs); with preceding COD (avoir verbs) |
| Verbe pronominal | Reflexive verb — always takes *être* in compound tenses |
| COD précédant | Preceding direct object — triggers past participle agreement with avoir verbs |
