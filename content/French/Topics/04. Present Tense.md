## 4.1 When to Use Le Présent

The present tense in French covers several English constructions:

| French use | English equivalent | Example |
|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| Current action | I eat / I am eating / I do eat | *Je mange.* |
| Habitual action | I go to school every day | *Je vais au collège tous les jours.* |
| General truth | The Earth orbits the Sun | *La Terre tourne autour du Soleil.* |
| With *depuis* (ongoing from past) | I have been learning French for 3 years | *J'apprends le français depuis trois ans.* |
| Near future | He's arriving in five minutes | *Il arrive dans cinq minutes.* |
| Historic present (narrative) | Makes past events vivid | *En 1789, le peuple se soulève.* |

> 💡 **Exam tip:** French has only **one** present tense form. *Je mange* can mean "I eat", "I am eating", or "I do eat" depending on context.

## 4.2 Regular -ER Verbs

The largest group. Remove **-er** to get the stem, then add endings.

**Model verb: parler (to speak) — stem: parl-**

| Pronoun | Ending | Form |
|---------|--------|------|
| je | -**e** | parle |
| tu | -**es** | parles |
| il / elle / on | -**e** | parle |
| nous | -**ons** | parlons |
| vous | -**ez** | parlez |
| ils / elles | -**ent** | parlent |

**Other common -ER verbs:**

| Verb | English | Verb | English |
|------|---------|------|---------|
| aimer | to like / love | trouver | to find |
| habiter | to live (in) | regarder | to watch |
| écouter | to listen (to) | arriver | to arrive |
| travailler | to work | rentrer | to return home |
| jouer | to play | donner | to give |
| manger | to eat | porter | to carry / wear |
| chanter | to sing | marcher | to walk |
| danser | to dance | voyager | to travel |
| visiter | to visit | parler | to speak |
| penser | to think | chercher | to look for |

## 4.3 Regular -IR Verbs

Remove **-ir** to get the singular stem; the plural adds **-iss-** to the stem.

**Model verb: finir (to finish)**

| Pronoun | Form |
|---------|------|
| je | fin**is** |
| tu | fin**is** |
| il / elle / on | fin**it** |
| nous | fin**issons** |
| vous | fin**issez** |
| ils / elles | fin**issent** |

**Other common *finir*-type -IR verbs:**

| Verb | English |
|------|---------|
| choisir | to choose |
| remplir | to fill |
| réussir | to succeed / pass |
| obéir | to obey |
| réfléchir | to reflect / think carefully |

| rougir | to blush |

## 4.4 Regular -RE Verbs

Remove **-re** to get the stem. The *il/elle* form has **no extra ending**.

**Model verb: vendre (to sell) — stem: vend-**

| Pronoun | Ending | Form |
|---------|--------|------|
| je | -**s** | vends |
| tu | -**s** | vends |
| il / elle / on | (nothing) | vend |
| nous | -**ons** | vendons |
| vous | -**ez** | vendez |
| ils / elles | -**ent** | vendent |

**Other common -RE verbs:**

| Verb | English |
|------|---------|
| attendre | to wait (for) |
| répondre | to reply / answer |
| perdre | to lose |
| entendre | to hear |
| descendre | to go down |
| rendre | to give back / make (+ adj) |
| défendre | to defend / forbid |
| répandre | to spread |

## 4.5 Spelling-Change -ER Verbs

Some -ER verbs change spelling in certain persons to preserve pronunciation.

### -eler / -eter Verbs (Consonant Doubling)

| Verb | je / tu / il / ils | nous / vous |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| appeler (to call) | **j'appelle** | nous appelons |
| jeter (to throw) | **je jette** | nous jetons |

### -e_er Verbs (è Accent in Stressed Syllables)

| Verb | je / tu / il / ils | nous / vous |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| acheter (to buy) | **j'achète** | nous achetons |
| se lever (to get up) | **je me lève** | nous nous levons |
| amener (to bring/take) | **j'amène** | nous amenons |

### -é_er Verbs (Accent Changes under Stress)

| Verb | je / tu / il / ils | nous / vous |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| espérer (to hope) | **j'espère** | nous espérons |
| préférer (to prefer) | **je préfère** | nous préférons |
| répéter (to repeat) | **je répète** | nous répétons |
| céder (to give up / yield) | **je cède** | nous cédons |

### -ger Verbs (g → ge before -ons)

To preserve the soft *[ʒ]* sound before the *-o* of *-ons*:
- *manger* → **nous mangeons** (not *mangons*)
- *voyager* → **nous voyageons**
- *nager* → **nous nageons**

### -cer Verbs (c → ç before -ons)

To preserve the soft *[s]* sound before the *-o* of *-ons*:
- *commencer* → **nous commençons** (not *commençons* - already right)
- *lancer* → **nous lançons**
- *avancer* → **nous avançons**

## 4.7 Reflexive Verbs in the Present

Full conjugation of **se lever** (to get up):

| Pronoun | Form |
|---------|------|
| je | **me** lève |
| tu | **te** lèves |
| il / elle / on | **se** lève |
| nous | **nous** levons |
| vous | **vous** levez |
| ils / elles | **se** lèvent |

**Negative:** *Je **ne me** lève **pas**.* (I don't get up.)

**Question with *est-ce que*:** *Est-ce que tu **te** lèves tôt ?* (Do you get up early?)

**Question with inversion:** ***Te** lèves-tu tôt ?*

## 4.8 Uses of 'on'

| Use | Example | Translation |
|-----|---------|-------------|
| Informal *nous* | *On va au café.* | We're going to the café. |
| General "you/one" | *On ne sait jamais.* | You never know. |
| Passive / impersonal | *On parle français ici.* | French is spoken here. |
| Reported speech | *On m'a dit que...* | I was told that... |

> [!note] *On* always uses the **il/elle** verb form. However, past participles and adjectives may agree with the logical subject when the gender is clear: *On est **arrivées**.* (if "on" refers to a group of women)

## 4.9 Present with Depuis

**Depuis + present tense** = action that started in the past and is **still going on**:

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| *J'apprends le français **depuis** trois ans.* | I have been learning French for three years (and still am). |
| *Il habite ici **depuis** 2010.* | He has been living here since 2010. |
| *Elle attend **depuis** une heure.* | She has been waiting for an hour. |

Contrast with **pendant + past tense** (completed duration):
- *J'ai appris l'espagnol **pendant** deux ans.* — I learned Spanish for two years (and stopped).

### Other Ongoing-Action Constructions

| French | English | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Il y a + period + que + présent | for (ongoing) | *Il y a deux heures que j'attends.* |
| Ça fait + period + que + présent | for (ongoing) | *Ça fait un mois qu'elle travaille ici.* |
| Voilà + period + que + présent | for (ongoing — slightly formal) | *Voilà dix ans qu'on se connaît.* |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Le présent | Present tense — used for current actions, habits, and general truths |
| Verbe régulier | Regular verb — follows a predictable -ER, -IR, or -RE conjugation pattern |
| Verbe irrégulier | Irregular verb — does not follow the standard pattern; must be memorised |
| Radical (stem) | The base of a verb after removing the infinitive ending (-er, -ir, -re) |
| Verbe pronominal | Reflexive / pronominal verb — includes a reflexive pronoun (se lever, s'appeler) |
| Depuis | Since / for — used with the present tense for actions still in progress |
| On | Pronoun = "one / we / they" — always takes the il/elle form of the verb |
| Verbe en -er | Largest group of French verbs; model: *parler* |
| Verbe en -ir | Second group; model: *finir* (adds -iss- in plural forms) |
| Verbe en -re | Third group; model: *vendre* (no ending on il/elle) |
