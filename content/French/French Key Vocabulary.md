|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 0 | zéro | 17 | dix-sept |
| 1 | un/une | 18 | dix-huit |
| 2 | deux | 19 | dix-neuf |
| 3 | trois | 20 | vingt |
| 4 | quatre | 21 | vingt et un |
| 5 | cinq | 22 | vingt-deux |
| 6 | six | 30 | trente |
| 7 | sept | 40 | quarante |
| 8 | huit | 50 | cinquante |
| 9 | neuf | 60 | soixante |
| 10 | dix | 70 | soixante-dix |
| 11 | onze | 71 | soixante et onze |

| 14 | quatorze | 90 | quatre-vingt-dix |
| 15 | quinze | 91 | quatre-vingt-onze |

> [!note] 70 = soixante-dix (60+10), 80 = quatre-vingts (4×20), 90 = quatre-vingt-dix (4×20+10). *Quatre-vingts* loses its -s when followed by another number: quatre-vingt-un. Belgian/Swiss French uses *septante, huitante/octante, nonante* — you may see these in reading texts.

## 2. Days, Months and Seasons

### Days of the Week

| French | English |

| mardi | Tuesday |
| mercredi | Wednesday |
| jeudi | Thursday |
| vendredi | Friday |
| samedi | Saturday |

> [!note] Days are NOT capitalised in French. *Le lundi* = every Monday (habitual); *lundi* = this Monday/next Monday (specific).

## 9. Negative Structures

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| ne ... pas | not | *Je ne parle pas.* — I don't speak. |
| ne ... plus | no longer / not anymore | *Je ne fume plus.* — I no longer smoke. |
| ne ... jamais | never | *Je ne mens jamais.* — I never lie. |
| ne ... rien | nothing | *Je ne vois rien.* — I see nothing. |
| ne ... personne | nobody | *Je ne connais personne.* — I know nobody. |
| ne ... que | only | *Je n'ai qu'un euro.* — I only have one euro. |
| ne ... ni ... ni | neither ... nor | *Je n'aime ni le lait ni le café.* |
| ne ... guère | hardly | *Il ne travaille guère.* — He hardly works. |
| ne ... nulle part | nowhere | *Je ne vais nulle part.* — I'm going nowhere. |
| ne ... aucun(e) | no / not any | *Je n'ai aucune idée.* — I have no idea. |

> [!note] After a negative, *un/une/des* becomes **de/d'**: *J'ai des amis* → *Je n'ai pas d'amis*.

> [!note] *Rien* and *personne* can act as subjects: *Rien ne se passe.* (Nothing is happening.) *Personne n'est venu.* (Nobody came.)

> 💡 **Exam tip:** In informal spoken French, the *ne* is often dropped (*Je sais pas*). However, in IGCSE **written** work, **always include both parts** of the negative for full marks.
