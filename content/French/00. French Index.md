## 📌 How to Use These Notes

1. **Grammar first:** Work through Sections 1 and 2 to master the core grammar structures.
2. **Vocabulary by theme:** Chapters 9–14 cover every major IGCSE topic — learn vocabulary in context using the example sentences.
3. **Exam skills last:** Chapter 15 gives you paper-specific strategies, connective phrases, and a full false friends table.
4. **Active revision:** Cover the English column in vocabulary tables and test yourself; then reverse and test from English to French.

---

> 💡 **Exam tip:** Always learn the **gender** of every noun when you first encounter it — write *le chien*, not just *chien*. Gender affects articles, adjective agreement, and past participle agreement with être verbs. Getting gender right from the very start saves marks across every single paper.
