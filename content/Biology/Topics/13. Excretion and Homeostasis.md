## Excretion

> **Excretion** is the removal of waste products of **metabolism** (chemical reactions in the body) from the body.

> ⚠️ Excretion ≠ Egestion (removal of undigested food = faeces)

### Metabolic Waste Products

| Waste product | Source | Removed by |
|--------------|--------|-----------|
| **Carbon dioxide** | Aerobic respiration | Lungs (exhaled) |
| **Urea** | Breakdown of excess amino acids (deamination in liver) | Kidneys (in urine) |
| **Water** | Respiration, metabolic reactions | Kidneys (urine), lungs, skin |
| **Bile pigments** | Breakdown of haemoglobin | Liver → bile → faeces |

### Deamination
- Excess amino acids cannot be stored
- Amino group (NH₂) removed → **ammonia** (very toxic)
- Ammonia converted to **urea** (less toxic) in the liver
- Urea transported in blood to kidneys → excreted in urine

## The Kidneys

### Functions
- **Filtration** — removes waste and excess substances from blood
- **Reabsorption** — returns useful substances to blood
- **Osmoregulation** — controls water content of blood/body
- **Excretion** — urea removed in urine

### Kidney Structure
| Part | Function |
|------|---------|
| **Cortex** | Outer layer; contains nephrons (filtration and reabsorption) |
| **Medulla** | Inner layer; collecting ducts |
| **Pelvis** | Collects urine |
| **Ureter** | Carries urine to bladder |
| **Bladder** | Stores urine |
| **Urethra** | Urine exits body |
| **Renal artery** | Carries blood to kidney |
| **Renal vein** | Carries filtered blood away |

## The Nephron — The Functional Unit of the Kidney

Each kidney has ~1 million nephrons. Stages of urine formation:

### 1. Ultrafiltration (in Bowman's capsule)
- High blood pressure in **glomerular capillaries** (due to efferent arteriole being narrower)
- Forces small molecules out of blood into **Bowman's capsule** (glomerular filtrate)
- Filtered out: water, glucose, urea, uric acid, mineral ions, amino acids
- NOT filtered: blood cells, proteins (too large)

### 2. Selective Reabsorption (in proximal convoluted tubule)
- All **glucose** reabsorbed by active transport
- All **amino acids** reabsorbed
- Water reabsorbed by osmosis
- Some mineral ions reabsorbed
- **Urea** stays (small amount reabsorbed passively)

### 3. Loop of Henle
- Creates concentration gradient in medulla
- Allows water reabsorption in collecting duct

### 4. Collecting Duct — Water Reabsorption (controlled by ADH)
- More ADH → more water channels (aquaporins) → more water reabsorbed → concentrated urine
- Less ADH → less reabsorption → dilute, large volume urine

## Homeostasis

> **Homeostasis** is the maintenance of a constant internal environment despite changes in external conditions.

### Variables Controlled
| Variable | Normal range |
|---------|-------------|
| Blood glucose | ~4–8 mmol/L |
| Body temperature | ~37°C |
| Blood water potential | ~−700 kPa |
| Blood pH | ~7.35–7.45 |

### Negative Feedback

> **Negative feedback** is the mechanism by which a change in a variable triggers a response that reverses the change, restoring the variable to its set point.

```
Change detected by receptor
↓
Control centre sends signal
↓
Effector acts to reverse change
↓
Variable returns to normal
↓
Response reduced (negative feedback)
```

## Blood Glucose Regulation

| Situation | Hormone | Produced by | Effect |
|-----------|---------|------------|--------|
| Blood glucose **too high** (after meal) | **Insulin** | Pancreas (β cells) | Glucose → glycogen (glycogenesis); stored in liver/muscle; cells take up more glucose |
| Blood glucose **too low** (between meals) | **Glucagon** | Pancreas (α cells) | Glycogen → glucose (glycogenolysis); released into blood |

### Diabetes Mellitus

| Feature | Type 1 | Type 2 |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Cause | Autoimmune destruction of β cells → no insulin produced | Cells become resistant to insulin; often insufficient insulin |
| Age of onset | Usually childhood/young adult | Usually adult (increasing in young people) |
| Treatment | Insulin injections (essential), diet management | Diet, exercise, weight loss, oral medication, sometimes insulin |
| Risk factors | Genetic, environmental triggers | Obesity, lack of exercise, age, genetics |

## Temperature Regulation (Thermoregulation)

### Control Centre: **Hypothalamus**
- Detects changes in blood temperature
- Receives information from thermoreceptors in skin and core

### Too Hot:
| Response | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Vasodilation** | Blood vessels in skin dilate → more blood near surface → more heat lost by radiation |
| **Sweating** | Water evaporates from skin → takes heat away |
| **Erector muscles relax** | Hairs lie flat → less insulating air layer |
| **Reduced metabolic rate** | Less heat generated |
| **Behavioural** | Move to shade, remove clothing |

### Too Cold:
| Response | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Vasoconstriction** | Blood vessels in skin constrict → less blood near surface → less heat lost |
| **Shivering** | Rapid muscle contraction → generates heat |
| **Erector muscles contract** | Hair stands on end → traps air layer → insulation (not very effective in humans) |
| **Increased metabolic rate** | More heat generated |
| **Behavioural** | Put on clothes, move to warmth |

## Osmoregulation (Water Balance)

Controlled by **ADH (antidiuretic hormone)**:
- Produced by **hypothalamus**, stored/released by **pituitary gland**
- Detects blood water potential

| Blood too concentrated (dehydrated) | Blood too dilute (overhydrated) |
|-------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| ADH released | Less ADH released |
| Kidney tubules more permeable to water | Kidney tubules less permeable |
| More water reabsorbed | Less water reabsorbed |
| Small volume, concentrated urine | Large volume, dilute urine |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Excretion** | Removal of metabolic waste products from the body |
| **Homeostasis** | Maintenance of a constant internal environment |
| **Negative feedback** | Mechanism where a change triggers a response that reverses the change |
| **Osmoregulation** | Regulation of water and ion content of body fluids |
| **ADH** | Hormone controlling kidney water reabsorption |
| **Deamination** | Removal of amino group from excess amino acids; produces ammonia → urea |
| **Ultrafiltration** | High-pressure filtration of blood in glomerulus |
