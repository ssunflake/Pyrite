## The Human Circulatory System

- A **closed, double circulatory system**
- **Double** = blood passes through the heart **twice** per circuit
- **Circuit 1:** Heart → Lungs → Heart (pulmonary circulation)
- **Circuit 2:** Heart → Body → Heart (systemic circulation)

### Advantages of Double Circulation
- Higher pressure maintained in systemic circuit
- Faster delivery of oxygen to tissues
- Separates oxygenated and deoxygenated blood

## The Heart

### Structure
| Chamber | Side | Function |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Right atrium** | Right | Receives deoxygenated blood from body (via vena cava) |
| **Right ventricle** | Right | Pumps deoxygenated blood to lungs (via pulmonary artery) |
| **Left atrium** | Left | Receives oxygenated blood from lungs (via pulmonary vein) |
| **Left ventricle** | Left | Pumps oxygenated blood to body (via aorta) |

> ⚠️ The left ventricle has **thicker muscular walls** than the right — it pumps blood to the whole body (higher pressure needed)

### Valves
| Valve | Location | Function |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Atrioventricular (AV) valves** | Between atria and ventricles | Prevent backflow into atria when ventricles contract |
| **Semilunar valves** | At base of aorta and pulmonary artery | Prevent backflow into ventricles |

### The Cardiac Cycle
1. **Atrial systole** — atria contract → blood pushed into ventricles
2. **Ventricular systole** — ventricles contract → blood pushed into arteries
3. **Diastole** — heart relaxes → chambers fill with blood

### The Heartbeat
- **Sinoatrial node (SAN)** — pacemaker; generates electrical impulses in right atrium
- Impulse spreads across atria → both atria contract
- **Atrioventricular node (AVN)** — delays signal slightly, then sends down Bundle of His
- Impulse passes down **Bundle of His** → Purkinje fibres → ventricles contract from base upward

## Blood Vessels

| Feature | Artery | Vein | Capillary |
|---------|--------|------|-----------|
| Direction | Away from heart | To heart | Between arteries and veins |
| Blood type | Oxygenated (except pulmonary) | Deoxygenated (except pulmonary) | Both |
| Wall thickness | Thick (elastic, muscular) | Thin | One cell thick |
| Lumen | Narrow | Wide | Very narrow |
| Valves | No | Yes (prevent backflow) | No |
| Pressure | High | Low | Very low |
| Pulse | Yes | No | No |

### Capillaries — Exchange
- Walls only one cell thick → short diffusion distance
- Huge number → very large surface area
- Tissue fluid forms from blood plasma → bathes cells
- Exchange: O₂ and glucose → cells; CO₂ and waste → capillaries

## Blood

### Components of Blood

| Component | Appearance | Function |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Red blood cells (erythrocytes)** | Biconcave disc, no nucleus | Carry oxygen (haemoglobin binds O₂) |
| **White blood cells (leucocytes)** | Large, irregular, nucleus | Immune defence |
| **Platelets (thrombocytes)** | Tiny cell fragments | Blood clotting |
| **Plasma** | Pale yellow liquid | Transport of CO₂, digested food, hormones, urea, heat |

### Red Blood Cells — Adaptations
- **Biconcave shape** — large surface area for O₂ diffusion
- **No nucleus** — more room for haemoglobin
- **Haemoglobin** — protein that binds O₂ in lungs, releases it in tissues
- **Flexible** — can squeeze through narrow capillaries

### Haemoglobin
$$\text{Haemoglobin} + 4O_2 \rightleftharpoons \text{Oxyhaemoglobin}$$

- In **lungs**: high O₂ concentration → O₂ binds (becomes oxyhaemoglobin)
- In **tissues**: low O₂ concentration → O₂ released

### White Blood Cells
| Type | Function |
|------|---------|
| **Phagocytes (neutrophils, monocytes)** | Engulf and digest pathogens (phagocytosis) |
| **Lymphocytes** | Produce antibodies; immunological memory |

### Blood Clotting
1. Platelets gather at wound
2. Cascade of reactions → **fibrinogen** (soluble) → **fibrin** (insoluble mesh)
3. Blood cells caught in mesh → **clot** forms → scab
- Prevents blood loss
- Prevents entry of pathogens

## The Lymphatic System

- Network of vessels carrying **lymph** (fluid similar to plasma, no RBCs)
- Drains excess tissue fluid back into blood
- Contains **lymph nodes** — filter lymph, contain white blood cells
- **Lacteals** in villi — absorb fatty acids and glycerol from gut

## Coronary Heart Disease (CHD)

### Causes
- Build-up of fatty **atherosclerotic plaques** (cholesterol + calcium) in coronary arteries
- **Atherosclerosis** — narrowing and hardening of arteries
- Blood clot may block narrowed artery → **heart attack (myocardial infarction)**

### Risk Factors
| Modifiable | Non-modifiable |
|-----------|---------------|
| High-fat diet (esp. saturated) | Age |
| Smoking | Genetics/family history |
| Lack of exercise | Sex (males higher risk) |
| High blood pressure | |
| Obesity | |
| Stress | |
| Diabetes | |

### Prevention and Treatment
- Diet changes, exercise, stop smoking
- Drugs: statins (lower cholesterol), aspirin (prevent clots)
- Surgery: bypass surgery, angioplasty, stents

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Double circulation** | Blood passes through heart twice per complete circuit |
| **Systole** | Contraction of heart muscle |
| **Diastole** | Relaxation of heart muscle |
| **Atherosclerosis** | Build-up of fatty plaques in arterial walls |
| **Haemoglobin** | Iron-containing protein in RBCs that carries oxygen |
| **Tissue fluid** | Fluid that leaks from capillaries; bathes cells |
