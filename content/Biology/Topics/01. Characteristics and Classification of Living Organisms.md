## The Seven Characteristics of Living Organisms (MRS GREN)

| Letter | Characteristic | Definition |
|--------|---------------|-----------|
| **M** | **Movement** | Changing position or shape |
| **R** | **Respiration** | Chemical process releasing energy from food |
| **S** | **Sensitivity** | Detecting and responding to changes in the environment (stimuli) |
| **G** | **Growth** | Permanent increase in size and dry mass |
| **R** | **Reproduction** | Producing offspring |
| **E** | **Excretion** | Removal of toxic waste products of metabolism |
| **N** | **Nutrition** | Taking in and using food for energy and growth |

> ⚠️ **Excretion** is removal of *metabolic waste* — NOT egestion (removal of undigested food).

## Classification

> **Classification** is the organisation of living organisms into groups based on their similarities and differences.

### The Binomial System (Nomenclature)
- Every species has a **two-part Latin name**
- **Genus** (first word — capital letter) + **species** (second word — lower case)
- Written in *italics* or underlined
- e.g. *Homo sapiens* (humans), *Panthera leo* (lion)

### Taxonomy — Levels of Classification (Domain to Species)
From largest to smallest group:
**Kingdom → Phylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species**

> Mnemonic: **K**ing **P**hilip **C**ame **O**ver **F**or **G**ood **S**oup

## The Five Kingdoms

| Kingdom | Features | Examples |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Prokaryotae** (Bacteria) | No nucleus, no membrane-bound organelles, small | *E. coli*, *Streptococcus* |
| **Protoctista** | Mostly unicellular, eukaryotic, don't fit other kingdoms | Amoeba, Euglena, algae |
| **Fungi** | Eukaryotic, cell walls of chitin, saprotrophic, no chlorophyll | Mushrooms, moulds, yeast |
| **Plantae** | Eukaryotic, cell walls of cellulose, photosynthesis, multicellular | Mosses, ferns, flowering plants |
| **Animalia** | Eukaryotic, no cell wall, heterotrophic, multicellular | Insects, fish, mammals |

## The Animal Kingdom — Major Phyla

### Vertebrates (have a backbone)
| Class | Key Features | Examples |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Fish** | Scales, gills, ectothermic, fins | Salmon, shark |
| **Amphibians** | Moist skin, lay eggs in water, ectothermic | Frog, newt |
| **Reptiles** | Dry scaly skin, lay shelled eggs on land, ectothermic | Lizard, snake |
| **Birds** | Feathers, wings, endothermic, lay hard-shelled eggs | Eagle, penguin |
| **Mammals** | Hair/fur, endothermic, give birth to live young (mostly), suckle young on milk | Human, dog, whale |

### Invertebrates — Key Groups
| Group | Features | Examples |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Arthropods** | Jointed legs, exoskeleton, segmented body | Insects, spiders, crabs |
| **Insects** | 3 body parts, 3 pairs legs, 1 pair antennae | Bee, butterfly |
| **Arachnids** | 2 body parts, 4 pairs legs | Spider, scorpion |
| **Myriapods** | Many legs, many segments | Centipede, millipede |
| **Crustaceans** | 2 pairs antennae, hard shell | Crab, lobster, woodlouse |
| **Molluscs** | Soft body, often with shell | Snail, octopus, mussel |
| **Annelids** | Segmented worms | Earthworm, leech |
| **Nematodes** | Roundworms, non-segmented | Hookworm |

## The Plant Kingdom

| Group | Features | Examples |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Ferns** | No seeds, reproduce by spores, have roots/stems/leaves | Bracken fern |
| **Conifers** | Seeds in cones, needle-like leaves, no flowers | Pine, spruce |
| **Flowering plants (Angiosperms)** | Seeds in fruits, flowers, pollination | Most plants |
| **Monocots** | One seed leaf (cotyledon), parallel leaf veins | Grass, lily, maize |
| **Dicots** | Two cotyledons, branching leaf veins | Rose, oak, pea |

## Fungi

- Cell walls made of **chitin** (not cellulose)
- **Saprotrophic** — digest dead organic matter externally (secreting enzymes), absorb products
- No chlorophyll — cannot photosynthesise
- Body made of **hyphae** (thread-like structures) forming a **mycelium**
- Examples: *Mucor* (bread mould), *Saccharomyces* (yeast), mushrooms

---

## Viruses

- **Not classified as living** — they do not carry out all 7 life processes independently
- No cells, no cytoplasm, no metabolism
- Structure: protein coat (**capsid**) + genetic material (DNA or RNA)
- Can only reproduce inside a host cell
- Smaller than bacteria
- Cause diseases: influenza, HIV, COVID-19, measles

## Dichotomous Keys

- Used to identify unknown organisms
- Series of paired statements (yes/no questions) leading to identification
- **How to construct:** identify distinguishing features, write paired statements

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Species** | Group of organisms that can interbreed to produce fertile offspring |
| **Genus** | Group of closely related species |
| **Vertebrate** | Animal with a backbone (vertebral column) |
| **Invertebrate** | Animal without a backbone |
| **Ectothermic** | Body temperature varies with surroundings (cold-blooded) |
| **Endothermic** | Maintains constant body temperature (warm-blooded) |
