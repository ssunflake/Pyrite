## A Balanced Diet

A balanced diet contains the right amounts of all nutrients needed for health:

| Nutrient | Function | Sources |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Carbohydrates** | Energy source | Bread, rice, pasta, sugar |
| **Proteins** | Growth, repair, enzymes, hormones | Meat, fish, eggs, beans |
| **Lipids (fats/oils)** | Energy store, insulation, cell membranes, vitamins | Butter, oil, nuts |
| **Vitamins** | Various metabolic functions | Fruit, vegetables |
| **Mineral salts** | Various functions (bones, enzymes) | Varied |
| **Dietary fibre** | Prevents constipation; aids gut movement | Vegetables, wholegrain |
| **Water** | Solvent; transport; reactions | Drink, food |

### Key Vitamins and Minerals
| Nutrient | Role | Deficiency |
|----------|------|-----------|
| Vitamin C | Collagen synthesis, antioxidant | Scurvy (bleeding gums, weak skin) |
| Vitamin D | Calcium absorption for bones/teeth | Rickets (soft, deformed bones) |
| Iron | Making haemoglobin | Anaemia (fatigue, pale) |
| Calcium | Bones, teeth, muscle contraction | Weak bones, rickets |
| Iodine | Making thyroid hormones | Goitre (enlarged thyroid), hypothyroidism |

## The Human Digestive System

### Overview
Mouth → Oesophagus → Stomach → Small intestine → Large intestine → Rectum → Anus

| Organ | Function |
|-------|---------|
| **Mouth** | Chewing (mechanical digestion); salivary amylase (chemical digestion of starch) |
| **Oesophagus** | Moves food to stomach by peristalsis |
| **Stomach** | Churns food; pepsin (protein digestion); HCl (kills bacteria, activates pepsin); stores food 2–4 hrs |
| **Small intestine (duodenum + ileum)** | Main site of digestion and absorption |
| **Pancreas** | Secretes amylase, protease, lipase into small intestine |
| **Liver** | Produces bile; metabolises nutrients |
| **Gall bladder** | Stores bile |
| **Large intestine (colon)** | Absorbs water and mineral ions from undigested food |
| **Rectum** | Stores faeces |
| **Anus** | Egests faeces |

## Digestion

### Physical (Mechanical) Digestion
- **Teeth** — cutting, grinding food into smaller pieces
- **Stomach churning** — breaks food into smaller pieces
- **Bile salts** — emulsify fats into tiny droplets (increase surface area for lipase)

### Chemical Digestion
- Enzymes break down large insoluble molecules into small soluble ones
- **Starch → glucose** (via maltose): amylase, maltase
- **Proteins → amino acids**: pepsin, trypsin, peptidase
- **Lipids → fatty acids + glycerol**: lipase

### Role of Bile
- Produced in the **liver**, stored in **gall bladder**, released into duodenum
- **Alkaline** — neutralises stomach acid → optimum pH for pancreatic enzymes
- **Emulsifies fats** — breaks fat globules into tiny droplets → increases SA for lipase
- Not an enzyme — does not chemically digest fats

## Absorption

### Small Intestine Villi
The inner wall of the small intestine is covered with **villi** (singular: villus), which have **microvilli** on their surface:

**Adaptations for absorption:**
- Very large surface area (villi + microvilli)
- Thin epithelium (one cell thick) — short diffusion distance
- Rich blood supply (capillaries) — maintains concentration gradient
- Lacteals (lymph vessels) — absorb fatty acids and glycerol (reformed into fats → lymph)
- Good blood supply removes absorbed products quickly

| Product | How absorbed |
|---------|-------------|
| Glucose | Active transport into blood capillaries |
| Amino acids | Active transport into blood capillaries |
| Fatty acids + glycerol | Diffusion → reformed into fats → lacteals (lymph) |
| Water | Osmosis |
| Mineral ions | Active transport |

## Peristalsis

- Wave-like contractions of the **circular and longitudinal muscles** in the gut wall
- Moves food along the digestive system
- Does NOT require gravity (you can swallow upside down!)

## Teeth

| Type | Shape | Function | Number (adults) |
|------|-------|---------|----------------|
| **Incisors** | Flat, chisel-like | Cutting food | 8 |
| **Canines** | Pointed | Tearing food | 4 |
| **Premolars** | Two cusps | Crushing and grinding | 8 |
| **Molars** | Large, flat, many cusps | Crushing and grinding | 12 (incl. wisdom) |

### Tooth Structure
- **Enamel** — hardest substance in body; covers crown
- **Dentine** — bone-like material; beneath enamel
- **Pulp cavity** — contains blood vessels and nerves
- **Cement** — anchors root to jaw
- **Root** — in the jawbone

### Dental Decay
1. Bacteria in plaque feed on sugar → produce acid
2. Acid dissolves enamel → cavity forms
3. Prevention: brushing, flossing, reducing sugar, fluoride toothpaste

## Malnutrition

| Condition | Cause | Effects |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Starvation** | Insufficient total food | Weight loss, muscle wasting, immune deficiency |
| **Obesity** | Excess calorie intake | Type 2 diabetes, heart disease, joint problems |
| **Kwashiorkor** | Protein deficiency | Swollen abdomen, muscle loss, poor growth |
| **Anaemia** | Iron deficiency | Fatigue, breathlessness |
| **Rickets** | Vitamin D deficiency | Soft, deformed bones |
| **Scurvy** | Vitamin C deficiency | Bleeding gums, poor wound healing |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Digestion** | Breaking down large insoluble molecules into small soluble ones |
| **Absorption** | Passage of small molecules from gut into blood |
| **Assimilation** | Use of absorbed nutrients by cells |
| **Egestion** | Removal of undigested food (faeces) via anus |
| **Peristalsis** | Wave-like muscle contractions moving food along gut |
| **Emulsification** | Breaking fat into tiny droplets (by bile) |
