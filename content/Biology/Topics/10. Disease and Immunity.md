## Types of Disease

| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|------------|---------|
| **Infectious (communicable)** | Caused by pathogens; can spread between individuals | Malaria, tuberculosis, HIV, influenza |
| **Non-infectious** | Cannot spread from person to person | Cancer, diabetes, CHD, genetic disorders |

---

## Pathogens

> A **pathogen** is a microorganism that causes disease.

| Pathogen | Examples | Characteristics |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Bacteria** | *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* (TB), *Salmonella* | Prokaryotic; some produce toxins |
| **Viruses** | HIV, influenza, COVID-19 | Non-cellular; hijack host cells to replicate |
| **Fungi** | Athlete's foot, *Candida* (thrush) | Eukaryotic; mostly external |
| **Protists (Protoctista)** | *Plasmodium* (malaria) | Eukaryotic unicellular; some complex life cycles |

---

## Transmission of Disease

| Route | Examples |
|-------|---------|
| **Direct contact** | STIs, skin infections, athlete's foot |
| **Air (droplets)** | Influenza, TB, COVID-19 (coughing, sneezing) |
| **Water/food** | Cholera, typhoid, food poisoning (*Salmonella*) |
| **Vector** | Malaria (*Plasmodium* via *Anopheles* mosquito) |
| **Blood** | HIV (sharing needles, unprotected sex, blood transfusion) |
| **Fomites** | Objects (door handles, towels) |

## Malaria

- Caused by **Plasmodium** (protist)
- Vector: **female *Anopheles* mosquito**
- Life cycle:
  1. Infected mosquito bites human → Plasmodium enters bloodstream
  2. Plasmodium reproduces in liver cells
  3. Enters red blood cells → reproduces → cells burst → fever
  4. Mosquito bites infected person → takes up Plasmodium → spreads to next host

### Control of Malaria
| Method | How it helps |
|--------|-------------|
| Insecticides (e.g. DDT) | Kill mosquitoes |
| Mosquito nets (insecticide-treated) | Prevent bites at night |
| Drain stagnant water | Remove mosquito breeding grounds |
| Cover water sources | Prevent egg-laying |
| Antimalarial drugs | Prevent/treat infection |
| Biological control | Introduce mosquito predators |

## HIV and AIDS

- **HIV** = Human Immunodeficiency Virus
- Attacks and destroys **helper T lymphocytes** (CD4 cells)
- **AIDS** = Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome — when immune system so damaged it cannot fight infections
- **Transmission:** unprotected sex, sharing needles, blood transfusions, mother to child (pregnancy, birth, breastfeeding)
- **Prevention:** condoms, not sharing needles, screening blood products, antiretroviral therapy (ART)
- **Treatment:** antiretroviral drugs — suppress HIV replication, do not cure

## Defence Against Disease

### Physical/Chemical Barriers (First Line)

| Barrier | How it works |
|---------|-------------|
| **Skin** | Physical barrier; sebum (oil) is acidic → antimicrobial |
| **Mucus** | Traps pathogens in airways |
| **Cilia** | Move mucus (with trapped pathogens) up and out of airways |
| **Stomach acid** | Low pH kills most pathogens |
| **Lysozyme** | Enzyme in tears and saliva; breaks down bacterial cell walls |
| **Blood clotting** | Seals wounds, prevents pathogen entry |

### The Immune System (Second and Third Lines)

#### Non-specific (innate) immunity
- **Phagocytosis:** phagocytes engulf and digest pathogens
  1. Phagocyte detects pathogen
  2. Extends pseudopodia to engulf pathogen (forming phagosome)
  3. Lysosome fuses → digestive enzymes destroy pathogen
  4. Antigens presented on surface

#### Specific (adaptive) immunity
- **Lymphocytes** respond to **specific antigens**
- **Antigens** = molecules (usually proteins) on pathogen surface that trigger immune response

| Cell | Function |
|------|---------|
| **B lymphocytes** | Produce **antibodies** specific to antigen; produce memory cells |
| **T helper lymphocytes** | Activate B cells and other T cells |
| **T killer (cytotoxic) lymphocytes** | Kill infected cells directly |
| **Memory cells** | Remain after infection → faster response on re-exposure |

### Antibodies
- Proteins produced by B lymphocytes
- Specific to one antigen (complementary shape)
- Actions:
  - **Agglutination** — clump pathogens together for phagocytosis
  - **Neutralisation** — block toxins or virus entry
  - **Opsonisation** — label pathogens for phagocytes

## Immunisation (Vaccination)

> **Vaccination** introduces antigens (dead/weakened pathogens or parts of them) → immune response without causing disease → memory cells formed → protection on future exposure

### How Vaccines Work
1. Vaccine injected → contains antigen
2. B lymphocytes respond → produce antibodies + memory B cells
3. On real infection → memory cells recognise antigen → rapid, large antibody response
4. Pathogen destroyed before symptoms develop

### Herd Immunity
- When enough people are vaccinated, pathogen cannot spread even to unvaccinated
- Protects vulnerable people (elderly, immunocompromised)
- Threshold typically 70–95% depending on disease

### Active vs Passive Immunity

| Feature | Active Immunity | Passive Immunity |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| How acquired | Body makes its own antibodies | Antibodies received from another source |
| Duration | Long-lasting (memory cells) | Short-lived (no memory cells) |
| Examples | Natural infection, vaccination | Breast milk (IgA), antiserum injection, antibodies across placenta |

---

## Antibiotics

- Chemicals that kill bacteria or inhibit their growth
- **Do NOT work on viruses** (no cell wall, use host cell machinery)
- e.g. Penicillin, amoxicillin, streptomycin
- Mechanism: damage bacterial cell walls, inhibit protein synthesis

### Antibiotic Resistance
- Bacteria evolve resistance through natural selection
- Overuse/misuse of antibiotics accelerates resistance
- **MRSA** (*Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus*) — resistant to most antibiotics

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Pathogen** | Microorganism that causes disease |
| **Antigen** | Foreign molecule (usually protein) that triggers immune response |
| **Antibody** | Protein produced by lymphocytes that binds specifically to one antigen |
| **Vaccine** | Preparation containing antigens that stimulates immune response without causing disease |
| **Antibiotic** | Chemical that kills bacteria or inhibits their growth |
| **Phagocytosis** | Engulfing and digesting pathogens by phagocytes |
