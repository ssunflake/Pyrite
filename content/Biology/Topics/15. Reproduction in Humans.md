## The Male Reproductive System

| Structure | Function |
|-----------|---------|
| **Testes** | Produce sperm (spermatogenesis) and testosterone |
| **Epididymis** | Stores and matures sperm |
| **Vas deferens (sperm duct)** | Carries sperm to urethra |
| **Seminal vesicles** | Add fluid to nourish sperm |
| **Prostate gland** | Adds alkaline fluid to neutralise vaginal acidity |
| **Urethra** | Carries semen and urine out of body |
| **Penis** | Deposits semen in vagina; transfers urine |
| **Scrotum** | Holds testes outside body (~2°C cooler than body) — optimal for sperm production |

### Sperm Cell Structure
- Head: nucleus (haploid, 23 chromosomes) + acrosome (enzymes to penetrate egg)
- Midpiece: many mitochondria (energy for swimming)
- Tail (flagellum): for swimming

## The Female Reproductive System

| Structure | Function |
|-----------|---------|
| **Ovaries** | Produce eggs (oogenesis) and hormones (oestrogen, progesterone) |
| **Fallopian tubes (oviducts)** | Carry egg from ovary to uterus; site of fertilisation |
| **Uterus (womb)** | Site of embryo implantation and fetal development |
| **Endometrium** | Inner lining of uterus; shed during menstruation |
| **Cervix** | Lower part of uterus; dilates during birth |
| **Vagina** | Birth canal; receives penis during intercourse |

## The Menstrual Cycle

A **28-day cycle** controlled by hormones:

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| 1–5 | Menstruation — uterus lining shed |
| 1–13 | Follicle develops in ovary (FSH); uterus lining rebuilds (oestrogen) |
| ~14 | **Ovulation** — LH surge → egg released from ovary |
| 15–28 | Corpus luteum forms → secretes progesterone → maintains thick lining |
| ~28 | If no fertilisation → progesterone falls → lining breaks down → menstruation begins |

### Hormones of the Menstrual Cycle

| Hormone | Produced by | Effect |
|---------|------------|--------|
| **FSH** (Follicle Stimulating Hormone) | Pituitary gland | Stimulates follicle development; stimulates oestrogen production |
| **Oestrogen** | Ovarian follicle | Rebuilds uterus lining; inhibits FSH; triggers LH surge |
| **LH** (Luteinising Hormone) | Pituitary gland | Triggers ovulation; stimulates corpus luteum |
| **Progesterone** | Corpus luteum | Maintains uterus lining; inhibits FSH and LH |

## Fertilisation and Implantation

1. Sperm released into vagina → swim through cervix → uterus → fallopian tube
2. Sperm reach egg in fallopian tube
3. **Fertilisation** — one sperm penetrates egg → nuclei fuse → **zygote** (diploid, 46 chromosomes)
4. Zygote divides by mitosis → **embryo** formed
5. Embryo moves down fallopian tube to uterus
6. **Implantation** — embryo embeds in thick uterus lining (~7 days after fertilisation)

## Pregnancy and Development

### Placenta
| Function |
|---------|
| Exchange of O₂ and CO₂ between mother and fetus (by diffusion) |
| Glucose and amino acids pass from mother to fetus |
| Urea passes from fetus to mother |
| Hormones produced (HCG, progesterone) to maintain pregnancy |
| Acts as a barrier to most pathogens (but not all viruses — e.g. rubella, HIV) |
| Does NOT allow direct mixing of mother's and fetus's blood |

### Umbilical cord
- Contains blood vessels connecting fetus to placenta
- **Two arteries** (deoxygenated blood fetus → placenta)
- **One vein** (oxygenated blood placenta → fetus)

### Amniotic fluid
- Surrounds fetus in amniotic sac
- Protects from physical shock, provides stable temperature, allows movement

### Development Timeline
- **Weeks 1–8** (embryo stage): major organs form
- **Week 8 onwards** (fetal stage): growth and development
- **Weeks 12–26**: most organ systems developed
- **Weeks 26–40**: growth and lung maturation

## Birth

1. Uterine contractions begin (stimulated by oxytocin)
2. Cervix dilates
3. Amnion breaks ("waters break")
4. Baby born head first
5. Umbilical cord cut and tied
6. Placenta expelled as afterbirth

## Antenatal Care

| Test/Action | Purpose |
|------------|---------|
| Ultrasound scan | Check fetal development, position |
| Blood tests | Check for anaemia, HIV, rubella immunity, blood group |
| Urine tests | Check for glucose (diabetes), protein (preeclampsia) |
| Advice on diet, smoking, alcohol | Prevent harm to fetus |

## Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)

| Infection | Pathogen | Symptoms | Prevention/Treatment |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------------------|
| **HIV/AIDS** | Virus | Often none initially; AIDS symptoms later | Condoms, antiretrovirals |
| **Gonorrhoea** | Bacteria (*Neisseria*) | Discharge, pain | Condoms, antibiotics |
| **Chlamydia** | Bacteria | Often none; infertility if untreated | Condoms, antibiotics |
| **Herpes** | Virus (HSV) | Sores | Condoms, antiviral drugs |
| **Syphilis** | Bacteria | Painless sores, rash | Condoms, antibiotics |

### Prevention of STIs
- Use condoms consistently
- Limit number of sexual partners
- Regular testing and treatment

## Contraception

| Method | Type | How it works | Effectiveness |
|--------|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Condom** | Barrier | Physical barrier; blocks sperm | ~98% if used correctly; also prevents STIs |
| **Oral contraceptive (pill)** | Hormonal | Oestrogen/progesterone suppress ovulation | >99% |
| **Intrauterine device (IUD)** | Physical/hormonal | Prevents implantation | >99% |
| **Diaphragm** | Barrier | Covers cervix | ~92–96% |
| **Abstinence** | Behavioural | No sex | 100% |
| **Sterilisation** | Surgical | Vasectomy (male) or tubal ligation (female) | ~100% |
| **Emergency contraception** | Hormonal | High dose progesterone within 72 hours | ~85–95% |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Ovulation** | Release of egg from ovary |
| **Fertilisation** | Fusion of egg and sperm nuclei |
| **Implantation** | Embedding of embryo in uterus wall |
| **Placenta** | Organ of exchange between mother and fetus |
| **Menstrual cycle** | Monthly cycle of ovulation and uterine lining preparation |
| **Gestation** | Period of development from fertilisation to birth (~40 weeks in humans) |
