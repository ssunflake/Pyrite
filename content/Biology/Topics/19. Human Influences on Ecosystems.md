## Human Population Growth

- Human population has grown **exponentially**, especially since the industrial revolution
- Key drivers: improved medicine, sanitation, agriculture, reduced infant mortality
- Current world population: ~8 billion (2024)
- Consequences: increased demand for food, water, energy, land → environmental degradation

## Pollution

### Water Pollution

#### Eutrophication
1. Fertilisers (nitrates, phosphates) washed from fields into rivers/lakes (**leaching/run-off**)
2. Algae and aquatic plants multiply rapidly → **algal bloom**
3. Algae block sunlight → underwater plants die
4. Dead plants and algae decomposed by bacteria
5. Bacteria multiply rapidly → consume all dissolved **O₂** (**deoxygenation**)
6. Fish and other aerobic organisms die (hypoxia)
7. Anaerobic bacteria flourish → release toxic H₂S and methane

> ⚠️ **Sewage** also causes eutrophication — contains nitrates and phosphates

#### Other Water Pollutants
| Pollutant | Source | Effect |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Pesticides** (e.g. DDT) | Agriculture | Bioaccumulation in food chains; kills non-target organisms |
| **Heavy metals** (Pb, Hg) | Industry, mining | Toxic to organisms; bioaccumulate |
| **Oil spills** | Tankers, pipelines | Coats animals, smothers habitats |
| **Plastic waste** | Consumer products | Ingestion by wildlife; microplastics in food chains |
| **Untreated sewage** | Urban areas | Eutrophication, pathogens in water |

### Air Pollution

| Pollutant | Source | Effect |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **CO₂** | Burning fossil fuels, deforestation | Greenhouse gas → global warming |
| **Methane (CH₄)** | Livestock, rice paddies, landfill | Potent greenhouse gas |
| **Sulfur dioxide (SO₂)** | Burning fossil fuels | Acid rain → kills trees, acidifies lakes |
| **Nitrogen oxides (NOₓ)** | Vehicle engines, power stations | Acid rain; smog; respiratory problems |
| **Carbon monoxide (CO)** | Incomplete combustion | Toxic; binds haemoglobin |
| **CFCs** | Old aerosols, fridges | Ozone layer depletion |
| **Particulates (soot)** | Diesel engines, burning | Respiratory disease; climate effects |
| **Smoke** | Burning, industry | Reduces photosynthesis; respiratory harm |

### Acid Rain
- SO₂ + NOₓ + water vapour → sulfuric acid + nitric acid → acid rain
- Effects:
  - Acidifies lakes → kills fish and invertebrates
  - Leaches nutrients from soil → stunts tree growth
  - Damages buildings and statues (limestone, marble)
  - Harms germination and plant growth

## Global Warming and Climate Change

### The Greenhouse Effect (Natural)
- Sun's radiation passes through atmosphere, absorbed by Earth
- Earth re-emits infrared radiation
- **Greenhouse gases** (CO₂, CH₄, H₂O, N₂O) absorb IR → re-emit → warm atmosphere
- This is **essential** — without it Earth would be ~33°C colder

### Enhanced Greenhouse Effect (Human-caused)
- Burning fossil fuels → more CO₂
- Deforestation → less CO₂ absorbed
- Agriculture → more CH₄ and N₂O
- Result: **global average temperature rising** (~1.2°C above pre-industrial levels)

### Consequences of Global Warming
| Consequence | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Melting ice caps** | Sea level rise → flooding of low-lying areas |
| **Ocean acidification** | More CO₂ dissolved → carbonic acid → harms coral reefs, shellfish |
| **Changes in rainfall** | More flooding in some areas, drought in others |
| **Habitat loss** | Shifts in suitable climate zones |
| **Species extinction** | Cannot adapt fast enough |
| **More extreme weather** | Hurricanes, heatwaves more frequent/severe |
| **Loss of biodiversity** | Coral bleaching, polar bear habitat loss |

## Deforestation

### Causes
- Clearing land for agriculture (cattle ranching, palm oil, soy)
- Logging for timber
- Urban expansion
- Mining
- Fuelwood

### Consequences
| Consequence | Explanation |
|------------|-------------|
| **Loss of biodiversity** | Habitats destroyed → many species extinct |
| **Increased CO₂** | Trees no longer absorb CO₂; burning releases it |
| **Soil erosion** | Tree roots hold soil; without trees, rain washes soil away |
| **Disruption of water cycle** | Less transpiration → less rain in some regions |
| **Loss of medicines** | Unknown plant/animal species lost before discovery |
| **Flooding** | No root systems to absorb water |

## Land Use and Monoculture

### Intensive Farming
- Large-scale, monoculture (one crop)
- High inputs: fertilisers, pesticides, herbicides, irrigation

| Advantage | Disadvantage |
|-----------|-------------|
| High yield | Biodiversity loss |
| Cheap food | Pesticide pollution |
| Efficient land use | Soil degradation |
| | Eutrophication from fertilisers |

### Bioaccumulation and Biomagnification
- **Bioaccumulation:** toxic substances (e.g. DDT, heavy metals) build up in organisms
- **Biomagnification:** concentration increases at each trophic level because predators eat many prey

```
Plankton: 0.000003 ppm DDT
↓
Small fish: 0.5 ppm
↓
Large fish: 2 ppm
↓
Bird (eagle): 25 ppm → reproductive failure
```

## Conservation

> **Conservation** is the management of natural resources to maintain biodiversity and ecosystem services.

### In Situ Conservation (on-site)
| Method | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Nature reserves and national parks** | Protect habitats; restrict development |
| **Marine protected areas** | Protect ocean habitats; restrict fishing |
| **Wildlife corridors** | Connect fragmented habitats |
| **Rewilding** | Restoring natural processes, reintroducing species |

### Ex Situ Conservation (off-site)
| Method | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Zoos and wildlife parks** | Captive breeding programmes |
| **Seed banks** | Preserve seeds of endangered plants (e.g. Svalbard Global Seed Vault) |
| **Botanical gardens** | Maintain living collections of rare plants |
| **Gene banks** | Store DNA and cells of endangered species |

### Sustainable Development
> Development that meets current needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

Examples:
- **Sustainable fishing:** catch quotas, no-take zones, mesh size regulation
- **Sustainable forestry:** replant trees at same rate as felling
- **Reduced fossil fuel use:** renewable energy, electric vehicles

## Endangered Species and Extinction

### Causes of Biodiversity Loss
- Habitat destruction (main cause)
- Overexploitation (hunting, fishing, collection)
- Invasive species (outcompete native species)
- Pollution
- Climate change
- Disease

### Why Biodiversity Matters
- Ecosystem stability and resilience
- Food security
- Medicine (many drugs from plants/animals)
- Economic value (tourism, crops)
- Ethical responsibility

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Eutrophication** | Increase in nutrients in water → algal bloom → deoxygenation → death of aquatic life |
| **Acid rain** | Rainwater made acidic by dissolved SO₂ and NOₓ from combustion |
| **Greenhouse effect** | Warming of Earth due to greenhouse gases trapping heat in atmosphere |
| **Deforestation** | Clearing of forests by humans |
| **Bioaccumulation** | Build-up of toxic substances in an organism's body |
| **Biomagnification** | Increasing concentration of toxin at higher trophic levels |
| **Conservation** | Management of natural resources to protect biodiversity |
| **Biodiversity** | Variety of species in an ecosystem or on Earth |
