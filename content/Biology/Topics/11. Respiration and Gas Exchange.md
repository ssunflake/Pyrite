## Respiration

> **Respiration** is the process of releasing energy from organic molecules (usually glucose) in cells. It is NOT breathing.

- Occurs in **all living cells**, all the time
- Energy released stored as **ATP**
- ATP is used for: movement, active transport, protein synthesis, cell division

---

## Aerobic Respiration

> Respiration using **oxygen** — produces more ATP.

$$\text{Glucose} + \text{Oxygen} \rightarrow \text{Carbon dioxide} + \text{Water} (\text{+ energy})$$

$$C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 \rightarrow 6CO_2 + 6H_2O \quad \Delta G = -2870 \text{ kJ/mol}$$

- Takes place in **mitochondria**
- Yields ~36–38 ATP per glucose molecule
- Complete oxidation of glucose

---

## Anaerobic Respiration

> Respiration **without oxygen** — produces much less ATP.

### In animals (and humans):
$$\text{Glucose} \rightarrow \text{Lactic acid} (\text{+ small amount of energy})$$

- Occurs during intense exercise when O₂ delivery can't keep up with demand
- **Lactic acid** builds up → muscle fatigue, cramp
- After exercise: lactic acid repaid with O₂ = **oxygen debt**

### In yeast (and some plants):
$$\text{Glucose} \rightarrow \text{Ethanol} + \text{Carbon dioxide} (\text{+ small amount of energy})$$

- Used in: brewing (alcohol), baking (CO₂ makes dough rise)
- Called **fermentation**

### Aerobic vs Anaerobic Comparison

| Feature | Aerobic | Anaerobic |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| O₂ required? | Yes | No |
| ATP produced | ~36–38 (much more) | ~2 (much less) |
| Products | CO₂ + H₂O | Lactic acid (animals) / Ethanol + CO₂ (yeast) |
| Location | Mitochondria | Cytoplasm |
| Duration | Can continue long-term | Short-term only |

## Gas Exchange in Humans

### The Respiratory System

| Structure | Function |
|-----------|---------|
| **Nose/mouth** | Air entry; nose warms, filters, moistens air |
| **Trachea** | Tube to lungs; C-shaped cartilage rings keep it open |
| **Bronchi (2)** | Branch from trachea to each lung |
| **Bronchioles** | Smaller branches within lungs |
| **Alveoli** | Air sacs where gas exchange occurs |
| **Diaphragm** | Muscle below lungs; controls breathing movements |
| **Intercostal muscles** | Between ribs; aid breathing |
| **Pleural membranes** | Surround lungs; secrete pleural fluid (reduce friction) |

### Alveoli — Adaptations for Gas Exchange
| Adaptation | How it helps |
|-----------|-------------|
| Very large number (~300 million) | Huge total surface area (~70 m²) |
| One cell thick (squamous epithelium) | Short diffusion distance |
| Rich capillary network | Maintains steep concentration gradient |
| Moist lining | Gases dissolve for diffusion |
| Thin capillary walls | Short diffusion distance |

### Gas Exchange at Alveoli
- **O₂** diffuses from alveolar air → blood (high [O₂] in air, low in blood)
- **CO₂** diffuses from blood → alveolar air (high [CO₂] in blood, low in air)
- Blood capillaries constantly remove O₂ and bring CO₂ → maintains gradients

## Breathing Mechanics

### Inspiration (breathing in)
1. Diaphragm contracts → moves down and flattens
2. External intercostal muscles contract → ribs move up and out
3. Volume of thorax increases → pressure decreases
4. Air flows IN (from high pressure outside to low pressure inside)

### Expiration (breathing out)
1. Diaphragm relaxes → moves up
2. Internal intercostal muscles contract (or external relax) → ribs move down and in
3. Volume of thorax decreases → pressure increases
4. Air flows OUT

> Remember: **Volume ↑ → Pressure ↓ → Air flows IN** (Boyle's Law)

### Breathing Rate
- Controlled by **medulla oblongata** in brain
- Chemoreceptors detect [CO₂] in blood
- CO₂ ↑ → breathing rate and depth increase

## Effects of Smoking

| Component | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Nicotine** | Addictive; increases heart rate and blood pressure; causes vasoconstriction |
| **Tar** | Carcinogen → lung cancer; coats cilia; contains chemicals that damage airways |
| **Carbon monoxide** | Binds to haemoglobin (more strongly than O₂) → reduces oxygen-carrying capacity |
| **Irritants** | Increase mucus production; damage cilia → "smoker's cough" |

### Diseases Caused by Smoking
- **Lung cancer** — carcinogens in tar cause mutations in DNA
- **Emphysema** — alveoli walls break down → reduced surface area → breathlessness
- **Chronic bronchitis** — inflammation and mucus in airways
- **Coronary heart disease** — nicotine and CO damage arteries
- **Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)**

## Gas Exchange in Plants

- Occurs through **stomata** (mainly in leaves)
- During photosynthesis: CO₂ IN, O₂ OUT
- During respiration (always): O₂ IN, CO₂ OUT
- At high light intensity: photosynthesis > respiration → net gas exchange reverses
- **Compensation point** = light intensity at which rate of photosynthesis = rate of respiration

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Respiration** | Release of energy from glucose in cells (NOT breathing) |
| **Aerobic respiration** | Respiration using oxygen; products are CO₂ and water |
| **Anaerobic respiration** | Respiration without oxygen; less ATP produced |
| **Oxygen debt** | Extra O₂ needed after exercise to oxidise accumulated lactic acid |
| **Fermentation** | Anaerobic respiration in yeast, producing ethanol + CO₂ |
| **Alveoli** | Tiny air sacs in lungs; site of gas exchange |
