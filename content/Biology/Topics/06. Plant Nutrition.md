## Photosynthesis

> **Photosynthesis** is the process by which plants use light energy to convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen.

$$\text{Carbon dioxide} + \text{Water} \xrightarrow{\text{light energy}} \text{Glucose} + \text{Oxygen}$$

$$6CO_2 + 6H_2O \xrightarrow{\text{light}} C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2$$

- Takes place in **chloroplasts** (containing **chlorophyll**)
- An **endothermic** process (light energy is stored as chemical energy in glucose)

---

## The Chloroplast

- Contains **chlorophyll** — the green pigment that absorbs light energy
- Light is mainly absorbed in the **red** and **blue** wavelengths; **green** is reflected (why plants look green)
- Inner membrane system (**thylakoids**) — light reactions occur here
- **Stroma** — dark reactions (Calvin cycle) occur here

---

## Limiting Factors of Photosynthesis

### 1. Light Intensity
- More light → more photosynthesis (up to a point)
- After a certain intensity, another factor becomes limiting

### 2. Carbon Dioxide Concentration
- More CO₂ → more photosynthesis (up to a point)
- Normal atmospheric CO₂ is ~0.04% — often the limiting factor

### 3. Temperature
- Higher temperature → faster enzyme-controlled reactions
- Above ~40°C → enzymes denature → rate drops sharply
- Optimum ~25–35°C for most plants

### Graphs
- All three show the same pattern: rate increases then levels off (plateaus) when another factor becomes limiting
- When two factors are varied simultaneously, the one in lower supply is the limiting factor

---

## The Two Stages of Photosynthesis

### Stage 1: Light-Dependent Reactions (in thylakoids)
- Light energy absorbed by chlorophyll
- Water is **split** (photolysis): H₂O → 2H⁺ + ½O₂ + 2e⁻
- Oxygen is released as a **by-product**
- ATP and NADPH are produced
- These are used in stage 2

### Stage 2: Light-Independent Reactions / Calvin Cycle (in stroma)
- CO₂ fixed (combined with RuBP)
- ATP and NADPH used to produce G3P
- G3P used to make **glucose** and other organic molecules
- RuBP is regenerated

## Leaf Structure and Adaptations for Photosynthesis

| Feature | Adaptation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Broad, flat shape** | Large surface area to absorb light |
| **Thin** | Short diffusion distance for CO₂ |
| **Transparent epidermis** | Light reaches mesophyll cells |
| **Palisade mesophyll (top layer)** | Packed with chloroplasts; receives most light |
| **Spongy mesophyll** | Air spaces for gas diffusion |
| **Stomata** | Allow CO₂ in and O₂ out |
| **Vascular bundles (veins)** | Supply water and minerals; remove products |
| **Waxy cuticle** | Reduces water loss |

---

## Stomata and Guard Cells

- **Stomata** (singular: stoma) — pores in the lower epidermis of leaves
- **Guard cells** — surround stomata; control opening and closing

### How stomata open (in light):
1. Guard cells absorb water by osmosis → become turgid
2. Thick inner wall curves away from the pore
3. Stoma opens → CO₂ enters, O₂ and water vapour exit

### How stomata close (in dark or drought):
1. Guard cells lose water → become flaccid
2. Stoma closes → reduces water loss (transpiration)

## Mineral Nutrition

Plants need minerals for healthy growth:

| Mineral ion | Function | Deficiency symptoms |
|-------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Nitrate (NO₃⁻)** | Making amino acids and proteins | Stunted growth, yellow leaves |
| **Magnesium (Mg²⁺)** | Making chlorophyll | Yellow leaves (chlorosis) |
| **Phosphate (PO₄³⁻)** | Making DNA, ATP, cell membranes | Poor root growth |
| **Potassium (K⁺)** | Enzyme cofactor, guard cell function | Yellow/purple leaves |

- Minerals absorbed by **active transport** in root hair cells
- Soil water must contain dissolved mineral ions

---

## Uses of Glucose in Plants

- **Respiration** — releases energy for cell processes
- **Cellulose synthesis** — for cell walls
- **Starch synthesis** — energy storage
- **Sucrose synthesis** — transport in phloem
- **Protein synthesis** — combined with nitrates to make amino acids
- **Fat/oil synthesis** — energy storage in seeds

## Variegated Leaves Experiment

- Variegated leaves have green and white patches
- White areas have no chlorophyll
- After starch test (iodine): only green areas turn blue-black
- Proves chlorophyll is needed for photosynthesis

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Photosynthesis** | Process using light energy to make glucose from CO₂ and water |
| **Chlorophyll** | Green pigment in chloroplasts that absorbs light energy |
| **Limiting factor** | Factor that limits the rate of photosynthesis when in short supply |
| **Stomata** | Pores in leaves that allow gas exchange |
| **Transpiration** | Loss of water vapour from leaves through stomata |
