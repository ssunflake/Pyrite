## Overview of Biological Molecules

| Molecule | Elements | Monomers | Polymers | Function |
|----------|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Carbohydrates** | C, H, O | Monosaccharides | Polysaccharides | Energy source, structural |
| **Proteins** | C, H, O, N, S | Amino acids | Polypeptides/Proteins | Enzymes, structural, transport |
| **Lipids** | C, H, O | Fatty acids + glycerol | — | Energy storage, insulation, membranes |
| **Nucleic acids** | C, H, O, N, P | Nucleotides | DNA, RNA | Genetic information, protein synthesis |

## Carbohydrates

### Monosaccharides (simple sugars)
- Single sugar units
- General formula: (CH₂O)ₙ
- **Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆)** — main respiratory substrate; two forms: α-glucose and β-glucose
- **Fructose** — found in fruit
- **Galactose** — found in milk

### Disaccharides (two sugars joined)
| Disaccharide | Made from | Found in |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **Maltose** | Glucose + Glucose | Germinating seeds |
| **Sucrose** | Glucose + Fructose | Sugar cane, table sugar |
| **Lactose** | Glucose + Galactose | Milk |

- Formed by **condensation reaction** (water released)
- Broken apart by **hydrolysis** (water added)

### Polysaccharides (many sugars joined)
| Polysaccharide | Monomer | Function |
|---------------|---------|---------|
| **Starch** | α-glucose | Energy storage in plants |
| **Glycogen** | α-glucose | Energy storage in animals (liver, muscle) |
| **Cellulose** | β-glucose | Structural — cell walls in plants |

## Proteins

- Made of **amino acids** joined by **peptide bonds**
- Formed by condensation reactions
- 20 different amino acids used in organisms

### Levels of Protein Structure
| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Primary** | Sequence of amino acids |
| **Secondary** | Alpha helix or beta sheet (hydrogen bonds) |
| **Tertiary** | Overall 3D shape (various bonds) |
| **Quaternary** | Multiple polypeptide chains |

### Functions of Proteins
- **Enzymes** — biological catalysts (e.g. amylase, pepsin)
- **Structural** — collagen, keratin, actin
- **Transport** — haemoglobin, channel proteins
- **Hormones** — insulin, glucagon
- **Antibodies** — immune defence
- **Receptors** — on cell surfaces

## Lipids (Fats and Oils)

- Made of **glycerol** + **3 fatty acids** (triglyceride)
- Formed by condensation reactions
- No polymer — not made of repeating monomers

### Saturated vs Unsaturated Fats
| Type | Fatty acid chains | State at room temp | Examples |
|------|------------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Saturated** | No double bonds; straight chains | Solid | Butter, lard |
| **Unsaturated** | One or more double bonds; kinked | Liquid | Olive oil, fish oil |

### Functions of Lipids
- Long-term energy storage (more energy per gram than carbohydrates)
- Thermal insulation (subcutaneous fat)
- Cell membranes (phospholipids)
- Protection of organs
- Hormones (steroid hormones)
- Fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K

## Water

- Vital for life; most chemical reactions occur in aqueous solution
- **Polar molecule** — slightly + and − ends → hydrogen bonds between molecules

### Properties and Importance
| Property | Why important |
|---------|--------------|
| Good solvent | Transports dissolved substances in blood, cytoplasm |
| High specific heat capacity | Helps maintain stable body temperature |
| High latent heat of vaporisation | Effective cooling by sweating/transpiration |
| Transparent | Light penetrates water for photosynthesis |
| Incompressible | Supports plant cells (turgor), hydrostatic skeleton |
| Reactant | Used in hydrolysis, photosynthesis |

## DNA and RNA

### DNA Structure
- **Double helix** — two strands wound around each other
- Made of **nucleotides**: deoxyribose sugar + phosphate + nitrogenous base
- Four bases: **Adenine (A), Thymine (T), Guanine (G), Cytosine (C)**
- **Complementary base pairing:** A–T and G–C (held by hydrogen bonds)

### RNA
- Single-stranded
- Ribose sugar (not deoxyribose)
- **Uracil (U)** instead of Thymine
- **mRNA** — carries genetic code from nucleus to ribosomes
- **tRNA** — carries amino acids to ribosomes during translation

## Food Tests

| Substance | Test | Reagent | Positive result |
|-----------|------|---------|----------------|
| **Starch** | Iodine test | Iodine solution (brown) | Blue-black colour |
| **Reducing sugars** (e.g. glucose, maltose) | Benedict's test | Benedict's solution (blue) + heat | Orange/brick-red precipitate |
| **Non-reducing sugars** (e.g. sucrose) | Benedict's test after acid hydrolysis | Boil with HCl, neutralise, then Benedict's | Orange precipitate |
| **Proteins** | Biuret test | Biuret reagent (sodium hydroxide + copper sulfate) | Purple/violet colour |
| **Lipids** | Emulsion test | Ethanol, then water | Milky white emulsion |
| **Lipids** | Sudan III/IV | Sudan III stain | Red/orange colour |
| **Vitamin C** | DCPIP test | DCPIP solution (blue) | Decolourises to colourless |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Monomer** | Small molecule that can join with others to form a polymer |
| **Polymer** | Large molecule made of repeating monomer units |
| **Condensation reaction** | Joins monomers; releases water |
| **Hydrolysis** | Breaks polymers; adds water |
| **Peptide bond** | Bond joining amino acids in proteins |
