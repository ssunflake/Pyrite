## Nervous vs Hormonal Control

| Feature | Nervous system | Hormonal (endocrine) system |
|---------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Speed | Fast (milliseconds) | Slow (seconds to minutes) |
| Transmission | Electrical impulses via nerves | Chemical hormones via blood |
| Duration | Short-lived | Longer-lasting |
| Specificity | Specific (to target organ via nerve) | Less specific (bloodstream) |
| Effect | Usually localised | Can be widespread |

---

## The Nervous System

### Central Nervous System (CNS)
- **Brain** — coordinates responses; conscious thought
- **Spinal cord** — transmits signals between brain and body; reflex arcs

### Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)
- All nerves outside brain and spinal cord
- **Sensory neurones** — carry signals from receptors to CNS
- **Motor neurones** — carry signals from CNS to effectors
- **Relay (connector) neurones** — connect sensory and motor neurones within CNS

---

## Neurones (Nerve Cells)

### Structure of a Motor Neurone
| Part | Function |
|------|---------|
| **Cell body** | Contains nucleus and most cytoplasm |
| **Axon** | Long fibre that carries impulse away from cell body |
| **Myelin sheath** | Fatty insulation around axon; speeds up impulse transmission |
| **Dendrites** | Short fibres that receive impulses from other neurones |
| **Synaptic knobs** | At end of axon; release neurotransmitters |

### Types of Neurones
| Type | Direction | Location |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Sensory** | Receptor → CNS | Peripheral nervous system |
| **Relay** | Within CNS | Brain and spinal cord |
| **Motor** | CNS → effector | Peripheral nervous system |

## The Reflex Arc

> A **reflex action** is a rapid, automatic, involuntary response to a stimulus, not involving the conscious brain.

### Pathway:
**Stimulus → Receptor → Sensory neurone → Relay neurone (spinal cord) → Motor neurone → Effector → Response**

### Advantages of Reflexes
- Very fast (no time for brain processing)
- Protective (e.g. withdrawing hand from heat)
- Conserves brain resources

### Examples
| Stimulus | Reflex response |
|----------|----------------|
| Touching hot object | Withdrawing hand |
| Bright light | Pupil constricting |
| Tapping below knee | Knee-jerk |
| Food in throat | Swallowing/coughing |

## Synapses

> A **synapse** is the junction between two neurones (or between neurone and effector).

### How a Synapse Works
1. Impulse arrives at **pre-synaptic knob**
2. **Vesicles** release **neurotransmitter** (e.g. acetylcholine) into synaptic cleft
3. Neurotransmitter diffuses across gap (synaptic cleft)
4. Binds to **receptors** on post-synaptic membrane
5. If enough neurotransmitter binds → new impulse generated
6. Neurotransmitter broken down by enzymes (e.g. acetylcholinesterase) or reabsorbed

### Why Synapses are Useful
- Allow signals to pass in **one direction only**
- Allow **integration** of signals (summation)
- Allow drugs/toxins to affect transmission selectively

## The Brain

| Region | Functions |
|--------|---------|
| **Cerebrum** | Conscious thought, memory, language, personality, sensory processing, voluntary movement |
| **Cerebellum** | Coordination, balance, fine motor control |
| **Medulla oblongata** | Controls unconscious processes: breathing rate, heart rate, blood pressure |
| **Hypothalamus** | Homeostasis: temperature, water, hunger; controls pituitary gland |
| **Pituitary gland** | Master endocrine gland; releases many hormones |

## Sense Organs

### The Eye
| Structure | Function |
|-----------|---------|
| **Cornea** | Transparent; most refraction of light |
| **Iris** | Controls amount of light entering (changes pupil size) |
| **Pupil** | Hole in iris; light passes through |
| **Lens** | Fine-focusing by changing shape (accommodation) |
| **Retina** | Contains light receptors (rods and cones) |
| **Rods** | Detect light intensity; black and white; active in dim light |
| **Cones** | Detect colour (red, green, blue); need bright light; concentrated in fovea |
| **Optic nerve** | Carries impulses to brain |
| **Fovea (yellow spot)** | Most sensitive area; highest cone density |
| **Blind spot** | Where optic nerve leaves — no receptors |
| **Vitreous humour** | Jelly filling back of eye; maintains shape |

### The Pupil Reflex
| Condition | Response | Muscle action |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| Bright light | Pupil constricts | Circular muscles contract, radial relax |
| Dim light | Pupil dilates | Radial muscles contract, circular relax |

### Accommodation (Focusing)
| Near object | Far object |
|------------|-----------|
| Ciliary muscles contract | Ciliary muscles relax |
| Suspensory ligaments relax | Suspensory ligaments taut |
| Lens becomes more spherical (fat) | Lens becomes less spherical (thin) |
| More refraction | Less refraction |

## The Hormonal System

### Key Hormones
| Hormone | Produced by | Target | Effect |
|---------|------------|--------|--------|
| **Insulin** | Pancreas (β cells) | Liver, muscle | Lowers blood glucose; promotes glycogen storage |
| **Glucagon** | Pancreas (α cells) | Liver | Raises blood glucose; promotes glycogen breakdown |
| **Adrenaline** | Adrenal glands | Heart, liver, muscles | "Fight or flight"; increases heart rate, releases glucose |
| **Testosterone** | Testes | Various | Male secondary sexual characteristics |
| **Oestrogen** | Ovaries | Uterus, various | Female secondary sexual characteristics; menstrual cycle |
| **Progesterone** | Ovaries/corpus luteum | Uterus | Maintains uterus lining in pregnancy |
| **ADH** | Pituitary gland | Kidney tubules | Increases water reabsorption |
| **FSH** | Pituitary gland | Ovaries | Stimulates follicle development |
| **LH** | Pituitary gland | Ovaries | Triggers ovulation |
| **Thyroxine** | Thyroid gland | All cells | Controls metabolic rate |

## Plant Responses — Tropisms

> A **tropism** is a directional growth response to a directional stimulus.

| Tropism | Stimulus | Response | Importance |
|---------|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Phototropism** | Light | Shoot grows towards light (+); root grows away (−) | Maximises photosynthesis |
| **Geotropism (gravitropism)** | Gravity | Shoot grows upward (−); root grows downward (+) | Anchoring, water uptake |
| **Hydrotropism** | Water | Roots grow toward water (+) | Water uptake |
| **Thigmotropism** | Touch | Tendrils coil around support (+) | Climbing, support |

### Auxins
- Plant growth hormones produced at **shoot tip**
- Stimulate cell elongation on the side they accumulate
- In phototropism:
  1. Light hits one side of shoot
  2. Auxin moves to shaded side
  3. Shaded side elongates more → shoot curves toward light

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Stimulus** | Change in the environment detected by receptors |
| **Response** | Action of an effector in reaction to a stimulus |
| **Receptor** | Cell or organ that detects a stimulus |
| **Effector** | Muscle or gland that brings about a response |
| **Reflex action** | Rapid, automatic, involuntary response to a stimulus |
| **Hormone** | Chemical messenger transported in blood from endocrine gland to target organ |
| **Tropism** | Directional growth response to directional stimulus |
| **Auxin** | Plant hormone controlling growth and tropisms |
