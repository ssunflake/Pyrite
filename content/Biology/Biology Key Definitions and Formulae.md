## ⚗️ Formulae

| Formula | Use |
|---------|-----|
| $\text{Magnification} = \dfrac{\text{image size}}{\text{actual size}}$ | Microscopy |
| $\text{Rate of diffusion} \propto \dfrac{\text{SA} \times \text{concentration difference}}{\text{membrane thickness}}$ | Fick's Law |
| $\text{Efficiency} = \dfrac{\text{useful energy out}}{\text{total energy in}} \times 100\%$ | Energy transfers |
| $N = \dfrac{n_1 \times n_2}{n_r}$ | Mark-release-recapture (Lincoln Index) |
| $\dfrac{V_s}{V_p} = \dfrac{N_s}{N_p}$ | Transformer (from Physics crossover) |
| $\text{BMI} = \dfrac{\text{mass (kg)}}{\text{height}^2 \text{(m}^2\text{)}}$ | Body mass index |

## 🔑 Definitions by Chapter

### Ch 1 — Classification
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Species | Group of organisms that can interbreed to produce fertile offspring |
| Binomial nomenclature | System of naming organisms with a two-part Latin name (genus + species) |
| Vertebrate | Animal with a vertebral column (backbone) |
| Taxonomy | Classification of organisms into groups based on similarities |

### Ch 2 — Cells
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Organelle | Specialised structure within a cell with a specific function |
| Prokaryote | Organism whose cells lack a membrane-bound nucleus |
| Eukaryote | Organism whose cells contain a membrane-bound nucleus |
| Differentiation | Process by which a cell becomes specialised for a particular function |
| Mitosis | Cell division producing two genetically identical daughter cells |
| Meiosis | Cell division producing four genetically different haploid cells |

### Ch 3 — Movement into and out of Cells
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Diffusion | Net movement of molecules from high to low concentration; passive |
| Osmosis | Net movement of water from high to low water potential through partially permeable membrane |
| Active transport | Movement against concentration gradient using ATP and carrier proteins |
| Turgor | Pressure exerted by cell contents against cell wall when cell is full of water |
| Plasmolysis | Shrinkage of cell membrane away from cell wall due to water loss |

### Ch 4 — Biological Molecules
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Monomer | Small molecule that joins with others to form a polymer |
| Polymer | Large molecule made of repeating monomer subunits |
| Condensation reaction | Joins monomers together; releases water |
| Hydrolysis | Breaks polymers apart; requires water |
| Denaturation | Permanent change in protein's tertiary structure |

### Ch 5 — Enzymes
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Enzyme | Biological catalyst — protein that speeds up reactions without being consumed |
| Active site | Region of enzyme with specific shape complementary to its substrate |
| Enzyme-substrate complex | Temporary structure formed when substrate binds to active site |
| Optimum | Conditions at which enzyme activity is maximum |
| Inhibitor | Molecule that reduces enzyme activity |

### Ch 6 — Plant Nutrition
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Photosynthesis | Process using light energy to synthesise glucose from CO₂ and water |
| Chlorophyll | Green pigment in chloroplasts that absorbs light energy |
| Limiting factor | Factor in shortest supply that limits rate of photosynthesis |
| Transpiration | Loss of water vapour from plant surfaces, mainly through stomata |

### Ch 7 — Human Nutrition
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Digestion | Breaking large insoluble molecules into small soluble ones |
| Absorption | Movement of digested food molecules from gut into blood |
| Assimilation | Use of absorbed nutrients by cells |
| Egestion | Removal of undigested food as faeces |
| Peristalsis | Wave-like muscle contractions that move food along the digestive system |

### Ch 8 — Transport in Plants
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Xylem | Tissue that transports water and mineral ions from roots to leaves |
| Phloem | Tissue that transports sugars from source to sink |
| Translocation | Movement of sugars in phloem from source to sink |
| Transpiration stream | Flow of water from roots to leaves driven by evaporation |

### Ch 9 — Transport in Animals
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Haemoglobin | Iron-containing protein in RBCs that carries oxygen |
| Atherosclerosis | Narrowing and hardening of arteries due to fatty plaque build-up |
| Systole | Contraction of heart muscle |
| Diastole | Relaxation of heart muscle |
| Double circulation | Blood passes through heart twice per complete circuit of body |

### Ch 10 — Disease and Immunity
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Pathogen | Microorganism that causes disease |
| Antigen | Foreign molecule (usually protein) that triggers immune response |
| Antibody | Protein produced by B lymphocytes; specific to one antigen |
| Phagocytosis | Engulfing and digesting pathogens by phagocytes |
| Vaccination | Introducing antigen to stimulate immune response and create memory cells |

### Ch 11 — Respiration and Gas Exchange
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Respiration | Release of energy from organic molecules in cells (not breathing) |
| Aerobic respiration | Respiration using oxygen; produces CO₂, H₂O, ATP |
| Anaerobic respiration | Respiration without oxygen; produces lactic acid (animals) or ethanol + CO₂ (yeast) |
| Oxygen debt | Extra oxygen needed after exercise to oxidise accumulated lactic acid |

### Ch 12 — Coordination and Response
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Stimulus | Change in the environment detected by a receptor |
| Reflex action | Rapid, automatic, involuntary response to a stimulus |
| Synapse | Junction between two neurones |
| Hormone | Chemical messenger transported in blood to target organ |
| Tropism | Directional growth response of a plant to a directional stimulus |

### Ch 13 — Excretion and Homeostasis
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Excretion | Removal of toxic metabolic waste products from the body |
| Homeostasis | Maintenance of a constant internal environment |
| Negative feedback | Mechanism where a deviation from set point triggers response to restore it |
| Osmoregulation | Control of water and ion balance in body fluids |
| Deamination | Removal of amino group from excess amino acids in liver |

### Ch 14 — Reproduction in Plants
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Pollination | Transfer of pollen from anther to stigma of same species |
| Fertilisation | Fusion of male and female gamete nuclei to form a zygote |
| Germination | Development of a seedling from a seed |
| Dispersal | Spreading of seeds away from the parent plant |

### Ch 15 — Reproduction in Humans
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Ovulation | Release of a mature egg from the ovary |
| Implantation | Embedding of embryo in the uterus wall |
| Placenta | Organ that allows exchange of substances between mother and fetus |
| Gestation | Period from fertilisation to birth (~40 weeks in humans) |

### Ch 16 — Chromosomes, Genes and Proteins
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Gene | Section of DNA that codes for a specific protein |
| Allele | A version of a gene |
| Genotype | The alleles an organism possesses |
| Phenotype | The observable characteristics of an organism |
| Dominant | Allele expressed in both homozygous and heterozygous states |
| Recessive | Allele only expressed when homozygous |
| Mutation | Change in the base sequence of DNA |
| Transcription | Synthesis of mRNA from a DNA template in the nucleus |
| Translation | Synthesis of protein from mRNA at a ribosome |

### Ch 17 — Variation and Selection
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Variation | Differences in characteristics between individuals of the same species |
| Natural selection | Process by which better-adapted organisms survive and reproduce more |
| Adaptation | Feature that increases an organism's survival in its environment |
| Evolution | Change in inherited characteristics of a population over many generations |
| Speciation | Formation of new species through isolation and divergence |

### Ch 18 — Organisms and Environment
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Ecosystem | Community of organisms interacting with their abiotic environment |
| Trophic level | Position of an organism in a food chain |
| Decomposer | Organism that breaks down dead organic matter |
| Carrying capacity | Maximum population size an environment can sustainably support |
| Nitrogen fixation | Conversion of atmospheric N₂ to useable forms by bacteria |

### Ch 19 — Human Influences
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Eutrophication | Nutrient enrichment of water → algal bloom → deoxygenation |
| Acid rain | Precipitation acidified by SO₂ and NOₓ from fossil fuel combustion |
| Bioaccumulation | Build-up of toxic substances in an organism's tissues |
| Biomagnification | Increasing concentration of toxin at successive trophic levels |
| Conservation | Management of natural resources to protect biodiversity |

### Ch 20 — Biotechnology
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Biotechnology | Use of living organisms to produce useful products |
| Fermentation | Anaerobic breakdown of glucose by microorganisms |
| Genetic modification | Inserting a gene from one organism into another |
| Restriction enzyme | Enzyme that cuts DNA at specific base sequences |
| Ligase | Enzyme that joins DNA fragments (sticky ends) |
| Recombinant DNA | DNA containing genes from two or more different organisms |
| Clone | Genetically identical copy of an organism |
| PCR | Polymerase chain reaction — amplifies specific DNA sequences |
