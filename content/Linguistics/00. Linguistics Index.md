## 🔍 English vs French — Key Phonetic Differences

| Feature | English | French |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Vowel count** | ~20 vowel sounds (monophthongs + diphthongs) | ~16 vowel sounds (fewer diphthongs) |
| **Diphthongs** | Many: /eɪ/, /aɪ/, /ɔɪ/, /əʊ/, /aʊ/, /ɪə/, /eə/, /ʊə/ | Almost none — vowels are pure/monophthongal |
| **Nasal vowels** | None | Four: /ɑ̃/, /ɛ̃/, /ɔ̃/, /œ̃/ |
| **Front rounded vowels** | None | Three: /y/, /ø/, /œ/ |
| **R sound** | Alveolar approximant /r/ (tip of tongue) | Uvular fricative /ʁ/ (back of throat) |
| **Stress** | Strong stress-timing; unstressed syllables reduce to schwa | Syllable-timed; stress always on final syllable of a group |
| **Final consonants** | Usually pronounced | Usually SILENT (with liaison as exception) |
| **Liaison** | Not applicable | Silent consonants pronounced before vowel-initial words |
| **Aspiration** | /p t k/ are aspirated at start of stressed syllables | /p t k/ are NEVER aspirated |
| **Schwa** | Very common in unstressed syllables | Less frequent; e muet can be silent or dropped |
| **Silent letters** | Many (gh, k in kn-, w in wr-, etc.) | Many final consonants; e at end of words |

> 💡 **Study tip:** Learn IPA symbols first — they let you decode pronunciation of any English or French word without relying on guesswork from spelling. A good approach: master the vowel chart, then the consonant pairs.
