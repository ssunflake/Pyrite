## 4.1 Word Stress

English is a **stress-timed language**: stressed syllables occur at roughly regular intervals, and unstressed syllables compress to fit the rhythm.

**Stressed syllables** are: louder, longer, and have a clearer (non-reduced) vowel.
**Unstressed syllables** are: shorter and often reduced — vowels frequently become /ə/ (schwa).

Stress is marked with **ˈ** before the stressed syllable: */ˈhæp.i/*, */kəmˈpjuː.tər/*

### Stress Placement Tendencies

| Pattern | Rule | Examples |
|---------|------|---------|
| **2-syllable nouns & adjectives** | Stress on 1st syllable | **TA**-ble, **HAP**-py, **PEN**-cil, **BI**-cycle |
| **2-syllable verbs** | Stress on 2nd syllable | re-**LAX**, de-**CIDE**, be-**GIN**, pre-**FER** |
| **-tion / -sion endings** | Stress on syllable before suffix | na-**TION**, de-ci-**SION**, in-for-ma-**TION** |
| **-ic ending** | Stress on syllable before -ic | de-mo-**CRAT**-ic, pho-**NET**-ic, e-**LECT**-ric |
| **-ity ending** | Stress on syllable before -ity | re-al-**I**-ty, e-lec-**TRI**-ci-ty |
| **-ify ending** | Stress on syllable before -ify | i-**DEN**-ti-fy, **CLAR**-i-fy |

**Suffixes that do NOT shift stress**: -ness, -ful, -less, -ly, -er, -ment, -ish, -hood

### Noun/Verb Stress Pairs (same spelling, different stress)

| Noun | Verb | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| **RE**-cord | re-**CORD** | a recording / to record |
| **RE**-fund | re-**FUND** | a refund / to refund |
| **PRO**-test | pro-**TEST** | a protest / to protest |
| **CON**-tent | con-**TENT** | content (noun) / to content |
| **OB**-ject | ob-**JECT** | an object / to object |
| **PER**-mit | per-**MIT** | a permit / to permit |
| **PRE**-sent | pre-**SENT** | a present / to present |
| **IN**-crease | in-**CREASE** | an increase / to increase |

> 💡 **Tip:** When in doubt about a noun vs verb, try shifting stress to the second syllable — if it sounds like an action, it's probably the verb.

## 4.2 Weak Forms

**Function words** (prepositions, conjunctions, auxiliaries, articles, pronouns) are usually unstressed in natural speech and take a **weak form** — with reduced or changed vowels.

| Word | Strong form | Weak form | Example in context |
|------|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| *and* | /ænd/ | /ənd/, /ən/ | *bread and butter* /brɛd ən ˈbʌtə/ |
| *the* (before C) | /ðiː/ | /ðə/ | *the cat* /ðə kæt/ |
| *the* (before V) | /ðiː/ | /ði/ | *the apple* /ði ˈæpəl/ |
| *a* | /eɪ/ | /ə/ | *a dog* /ə dɒg/ |
| *to* | /tuː/ | /tə/ | *I want to go* /aɪ wɒnt tə gəʊ/ |
| *of* | /ɒv/ | /əv/, /ə/ | *cup of tea* /kʌp ə tiː/ |
| *can* | /kæn/ | /kən/ | *I can do it* /aɪ kən duː ɪt/ |
| *have* | /hæv/ | /həv/, /əv/ | *I should have gone* /aɪ ʃʊd əv gɒn/ |
| *was* | /wɒz/ | /wəz/ | *it was good* /ɪt wəz gʊd/ |
| *for* | /fɔː/ | /fə/ | *wait for me* /weɪt fə miː/ |
| *at* | /æt/ | /ət/ | *look at him* /lʊk ət ɪm/ |
| *him* | /hɪm/ | /ɪm/ | *tell him* /tɛl ɪm/ |
| *her* | /hɜː/ | /ə/ | *give her time* /gɪv ə taɪm/ |
| *them* | /ðem/ | /ðəm/, /əm/ | *tell them* /tɛl əm/ |

> [!note] Strong forms are used for **emphasis** or **contrast**: *I said I CAN do it* (not can't).

## 4.3 Connected Speech — Assimilation

**Assimilation** occurs when a sound changes to become more similar to a neighbouring sound.

| Type | Process | Example | Transcription |
|------|---------|---------|---------------|
| **Regressive** (influenced by following sound) | /n/ → /m/ before bilabials | *in bed* | /ɪm bɛd/ |
| **Regressive** | /n/ → /ŋ/ before velars | *in case* | /ɪŋ keɪs/ |
| **Regressive** | /d/ → /b/ before bilabials | *good boy* | /gʊb bɔɪ/ |
| **Yod coalescence** | /t/ + /j/ → /tʃ/ | *don't you* | /dəʊntʃuː/ |
| **Yod coalescence** | /d/ + /j/ → /dʒ/ | *did you* | /dɪdʒuː/ |
| **Yod coalescence** | /s/ + /j/ → /ʃ/ | *this year* | /ðɪʃ jɪə/ |
| **Yod coalescence** | /z/ + /j/ → /ʒ/ | *as you* | /æʒuː/ |

## 4.4 Connected Speech — Elision and Linking

**Elision** is the omission of sounds in natural speech:

| Context | What gets dropped | Examples |
|---------|------------------|---------|
| /t/ between consonants | /t/ often drops | *next door* /nɛks dɔː/, *facts* /fæks/ |
| /d/ between consonants | /d/ often drops | *old man* /əʊl mæn/, *used to* /juːs tə/ |
| /t/ or /d/ at end of cluster | often drops before consonant | *last week* /lɑːs wiːk/ |
| Unstressed syllable | full vowel → schwa or drops | *comfortable* /ˈkʌmftəbəl/ → /ˈkʌmftbl/ |

**Consonant-to-vowel linking**: when a word ending in a consonant is followed by a vowel-initial word, the consonant links smoothly:
- *pick it up* → /pɪk ɪt ʌp/ — the /k/ links directly to /ɪ/
- *turn it off* → /tɜːn ɪt ɒf/

## 4.5 Silent Letters

Many English letters are written but not pronounced — a result of historical spelling and borrowed words.

| Letter | When silent | Examples |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **b** | after *m*, before *t* at end | *comb, lamb, thumb, debt, doubt, subtle* |
| **c** | in *-ck* clusters, *sc-* before *e/i* | *back, science, scissors, muscle* |
| **g** | before *n* | *gnaw, sign, foreign, gnarl, design* |
| **gh** | after vowels in many words | *daughter, through, night, though, eight, bought* |
| **h** | word-initial (some words) | *hour, honest, heir, honour, vehicle* |
| **k** | before *n* | *knee, know, knife, knock, knight* |
| **l** | before *f, m, k, d, v* | *calf, calm, walk, could, would, should, half* |
| **p** | in *ps-*, *pt-*, *pn-* | *psychology, pneumonia, pterodactyl, receipt* |
| **s** | in some words | *island, aisle, debris, viscount* |
| **t** | in common words | *castle, listen, whistle, often, fasten, ballet* |
| **w** | before *r*, in some words | *write, wrong, wrist, answer, sword, two* |

## 4.6 Intonation

**Intonation** is the pattern of pitch rise and fall across an utterance. It conveys meaning beyond the words themselves.

| Tone Pattern | Use | Sounds | Example |
|-------------|-----|--------|---------|
| **Falling ↘** | Statements, commands, wh-questions | Definite, finished, certain | *I'm going home.* ↘ / *What's your name?* ↘ |
| **Rising ↗** | Yes/no questions, listing (all but last item), showing surprise | Open, unfinished, uncertain | *Are you coming?* ↗ / *I'll have tea ↗, coffee ↗, or juice ↘.* |
| **Fall-rise ↘↗** | Implies reservation, "but...", hedging | Cautious, incomplete | *It's quite good* ↘↗ *(but...)* |
| **Rise-fall ↗↘** | Strong agreement, sarcasm, obvious statements | Emphatic, sometimes sarcastic | *Of COURSE.* ↗↘ |

> 💡 **Tip:** Intonation can completely change meaning. *She passed her exam* ↘ = neutral fact. *She PASSED her exam?* ↗ = surprised disbelief.

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Stress-timed** | A rhythm where stressed syllables occur at roughly equal intervals; unstressed syllables compress (English, German) |
| **Syllable-timed** | A rhythm where syllables are roughly equal in length (French, Spanish) |
| **Primary stress** | The main emphasis in a word, marked ˈ before the syllable |
| **Secondary stress** | A lesser emphasis, marked ˌ before the syllable |
| **Weak form** | The reduced pronunciation of a function word in natural speech |
| **Strong form** | The full, unreduced pronunciation used for emphasis or contrast |
| **Assimilation** | When a sound changes to become more like a neighbouring sound |
| **Elision** | The omission of a sound (especially /t/ or /d/) in connected speech |
| **Linking** | The joining of a final consonant to a following vowel across a word boundary |
| **Yod coalescence** | The merging of /t d s z/ + /j/ into /tʃ dʒ ʃ ʒ/ across word boundaries |
| **Intonation** | The pattern of pitch movement across an utterance, conveying attitude and sentence type |
| **Falling tone ↘** | Pitch descends; conveys finality or certainty |
| **Rising tone ↗** | Pitch ascends; conveys openness, question, or uncertainty |
| **Fall-rise ↘↗** | Pitch falls then rises; conveys reservation or implication |
