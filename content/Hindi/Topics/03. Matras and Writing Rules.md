## 3.1 What Are Mātrās? (मात्राएँ क्या होती हैं?)

In Devanagari, every consonant carries an **inherent vowel /a/** built into it:
- क alone = **ka** (not just 'k')
- म alone = **ma** (not just 'm')
- ग alone = **ga** (not just 'g')

When you want a consonant to have a **different vowel sound** (ki, ku, ke, ko, etc.), you attach a **mātrā** — a vowel diacritic symbol — to the consonant.

> 💡 **Analogy:** Think of the consonant as a template, and the mātrā as a modification sticker you attach to it. The same consonant (k-sound) looks and sounds different depending on which mātrā you attach.

When **no mātrā** is attached, the consonant always carries the inherent /a/ sound — unless a halant (§3.3) is used to suppress it.

---

## 3.2 Complete Mātrā Table

The table below shows every vowel mātrā using **क** (ka) as the example consonant. Learn both the symbol position and the example carefully.

| Vowel | Independent Form | Mātrā Symbol | With क | Transliteration | Position of Mātrā |
|-------|-----------------|-------------|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| a | अ | (inherent — no symbol) | क | ka | None needed |
| ā | आ | ा | का | kā | **Right** of consonant |
| i | इ | ि | कि | ki | **Left** of consonant (written before, read after!) |
| ī | ई | ी | की | kī | **Right** of consonant |
| u | उ | ु | कु | ku | **Below** consonant |
| ū | ऊ | ू | कू | kū | **Below** consonant (larger form) |
| e | ए | े | के | ke | **Above** consonant |
| ai | ऐ | ै | कै | kai | **Above** consonant (double stroke) |
| o | ओ | ो | को | ko | **Right** of consonant (combines ा + े) |
| au | औ | ौ | कौ | kau | **Right** of consonant (combines ा + ै) |
| aṃ | अं | ं | कं | kaṃ | **Above** consonant (anusvara dot) |
| aḥ | अः | ः | कः | kaḥ | **Right** of consonant (two dots) |

> ⚠️ **Critical Note — The इ mātrā:** The short-i mātrā (ि) is written **visually to the LEFT** of the consonant, even though it is pronounced **after** it. This is the most counterintuitive feature of Devanagari for beginners: कि = ki, not ik. Your eye reads right to left here, but your mouth says it left to right.

## 3.3 The Halant / Virāma (हलन्त / विराम — ् )

The **halant** (्) is a diagonal hook written below a consonant to **cancel its inherent /a/ vowel**, leaving just the bare consonant sound.

**Uses:**
1. At the end of words that end in a consonant (suppresses the final 'a')
2. As part of conjunct consonants (§3.4)

| Without Halant | With Halant | Result |
|----------------|-------------|--------|
| क = ka | क् = k | bare k-sound, no vowel |
| त = ta | त् = t | bare t-sound, no vowel |
| म = ma | म् = m | bare m-sound, no vowel |
| न = na | न् = n | bare n-sound, no vowel |

**Example word decomposition:**

**नमस्कार** (namaskār — a greeting):
- न (na) + म (ma) + स् (s, halant) + क (ka) + ा (ā mātrā) + र (ra)
- na + ma + s + kā + r = **namaskār**

**राम** (rām — the name Ram):
- र (ra) + ा (ā mātrā) + म् (m, halant)
- rā + m = **rām**

## 3.4 Conjunct Consonants (संयुक्त व्यंजन — Saṃyukta Vyañjan)

When two or more consonants come together **without any intervening vowel**, they merge into a **conjunct consonant** form. This is very common in Hindi, especially in Sanskrit-origin words.

**Formation principle:** The first consonant loses its right vertical stroke (or takes a subscript/superscript form) and fuses with the consonant that follows.

### The Four Traditional Special Conjuncts

These four are treated almost as independent letters and must be memorised:

| Conjunct | Components | Transliteration | Example Word | Meaning |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|--------------|---------|
| क्ष | क + ष | kṣa | क्षमा (kṣamā) | forgiveness |
| त्र | त + र | tra | त्रिकोण (trikoṇ) | triangle |
| ज्ञ | ज + ञ | jña | ज्ञान (jñān) | knowledge |
| श्र | श + र | śra | श्रम (śram) | labour / effort |

### Common Conjunct Consonants

| Conjunct | Components | Example | Meaning |
|----------|-----------|---------|---------|
| क्क | क + क | पक्का (pakkā) | firm / certain / cooked |
| त्त | त + त | पत्ता (pattā) | leaf / card |

| ग्र | ग + र | ग्राम (grām) | village |

| न्द | न + द | चंद्र (caṃdra) | moon |

> 💡 **Tip:** When you see two consonants joined strangely, suspect a conjunct. Look for the missing vertical stroke on the first consonant or a subscript/superscript form. With practice, common conjuncts become immediately recognisable.

## 3.5 Special Forms of र (ra) in Conjuncts

The consonant **र** behaves specially in conjunct formations because of its unique shape:

### र as the First Consonant — Superscript Repha (◌र्)

When **र** comes *first* in a conjunct, it cannot lose its vertical stroke (it has a different shape), so instead it appears as a **small curved stroke above** the following consonant:

| Word | Breakdown | Transliteration | Meaning |
|------|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| कर्म | क + र् + म | karma | karma / deed |
| धर्म | ध + र् + म | dharma | duty / religion |
| शर्म | श + र् + म | śarm | shame / shyness |
| पर्स | प + र् + स | pars | purse |
| सर्दी | स + र् + दी | sardī | winter / cold |

### र as the Second Consonant — Subscript Hook (◌्र)

When **र** comes *second* in a conjunct, it appears as a **small hook below** the preceding consonant:

| Word | Breakdown | Transliteration | Meaning |
|------|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| प्रश्न | प्र + श् + न | praśna | question |
| ट्रेन | ट्र + े + न | ṭren | train |
| ड्राइवर | ड्र + ाइवर | ḍrāivar | driver |
| ग्राम | ग्र + ाम | grām | village |
| क्रिकेट | क्र + िकेट | krikeṭ | cricket |

## 3.6 The Śirorēkhā (शिरोरेखा) — The Horizontal Head Line

The **शिरोरेखा** is the distinctive horizontal bar running across the top of each Devanagari character, connecting all the letters of a word into a single visual unit.

**Writing rules:**
1. All letters hang **downward** from this line
2. When writing by hand, the horizontal bar is usually drawn **last**
3. Letters within a word share one continuous bar
4. A **small gap** separates words (the bar breaks between words)
5. The bar runs at a consistent height throughout a line of text

## 3.8 Common Writing Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Incorrect | Correct | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Confusing short and long i | की vs कि reversed | कि (ki), की (kī) | ि goes LEFT; ी goes RIGHT |
| Confusing short and long u | कु vs कू | कु (ku) is small hook; कू (kū) is large hook | कू has a longer/larger form |
| Forgetting the head-line | Letters without bar | All letters must have शिरोरेखा | Practise connecting all letters |
| Writing ि after the consonant | किta written as kait | Always write ि visually before the consonant | Counterintuitive position |
| Confusing ब (ba) and व (va) | ब has bump on right only | व has two humps at top | Look at the top of each letter |
| Confusing ध (dha) and घ (gha) | Both look similar | ध is dental aspirated d; घ is velar aspirated g | ध has a loop on the right |
| Confusing र (ra) and न (na) | Both curve on right | र has diagonal lower right; न is more closed | र curves down-right; न closes |
