## 2.1 Overview of Hindi Consonants

Hindi has **33 main consonants** (व्यंजन — vyaṃjan), organised systematically by **place of articulation** (where in the mouth the sound is produced) and **manner of articulation** (how the sound is produced).

**The 5 main consonant groups (वर्ग — varg):**

| Group | Name | Place of Articulation | Consonants |
|-------|------|-----------------------|------------|
| क-वर्ग | ka-varg | Velar — back of the throat | क ख ग घ ङ |
| च-वर्ग | ca-varg | Palatal — middle of the hard palate | च छ ज झ ञ |
| ट-वर्ग | ṭa-varg | Retroflex — tongue curled back | ट ठ ड ढ ण |
| त-वर्ग | ta-varg | Dental — tongue against upper teeth | त थ द ध न |
| प-वर्ग | pa-varg | Labial — both lips | प फ ब भ म |

Within every group of five, the consonants follow the **same pattern**:
1. **Unaspirated voiceless** — no breath puff, no voice (क, च, ट, त, प)
2. **Aspirated voiceless** — strong breath puff, no voice (ख, छ, ठ, थ, फ)
3. **Unaspirated voiced** — no breath puff, voice on (ग, ज, ड, द, ब)
4. **Aspirated voiced** — strong breath puff, voice on (घ, झ, ढ, ध, भ)
5. **Nasal** — voiced nasal resonant (ङ, ञ, ण, न, म)

## 2.2 क-वर्ग — Velar Consonants (कंठ्य — Kaṃṭhya)

*Back of the throat — the soft palate (velum)*

| Devanagari | Name | Translit. | Pronunciation | Example | Meaning |
|------------|------|-----------|---------------|---------|---------|
| क | ka | k | Like 'k' in *ski* — unaspirated, no breath puff | कमल (kamal) | lotus |
| ख | kha | kh | Strongly aspirated 'k' — strong puff of air | खाना (khānā) | food / to eat |
| ग | ga | g | Like 'g' in *go* — voiced, soft | गाय (gāy) | cow |
| घ | gha | gh | Aspirated 'g' — breathy voiced stop | घर (ghar) | house |
| ङ | ṅa | ṅ | 'ng' as in *sing* — rarely standalone in modern Hindi | पङ्क्ति (used in conjuncts) | row/line |

---

## 2.3 च-वर्ग — Palatal Consonants (तालव्य — Tālavya)

*Tongue against the hard palate*

| Devanagari | Name | Translit. | Pronunciation | Example | Meaning |
|------------|------|-----------|---------------|---------|---------|
| च | ca | c / ch | Like 'ch' in *chair* — unaspirated | चाय (cāy) | tea |
| छ | cha | ch | Strongly aspirated 'ch' — big puff | छाता (chātā) | umbrella |
| ज | ja | j | Like 'j' in *jump* — voiced | जल (jal) | water |
| झ | jha | jh | Aspirated 'j' — breathy voiced | झंडा (jhaṃḍā) | flag |
| ञ | ña | ñ | Palatal nasal 'ny' — rarely standalone | rarely alone | — |

---

## 2.4 ट-वर्ग — Retroflex Consonants (मूर्धन्य — Mūrdhanya)

*Tongue tip curled backwards to touch the roof of the mouth — NO English equivalent!*

| Devanagari | Name | Translit. | Pronunciation | Example | Meaning |
|------------|------|-----------|---------------|---------|---------|
| ट | ṭa | ṭ | Retroflex t — harder/darker than dental त | टमाटर (ṭamāṭar) | tomato |
| ठ | ṭha | ṭh | Aspirated retroflex t | ठंडा (ṭhaṃḍā) | cold |
| ड | ḍa | ḍ | Retroflex d | डाक (ḍāk) | post / mail |
| ढ | ḍha | ḍh | Aspirated retroflex d | ढक्कन (ḍhakkan) | lid / cover |
| ण | ṇa | ṇ | Retroflex nasal n | कण (kaṇ) | particle / grain |

> [!note] The retroflex consonants (ट ठ ड ढ ण) are one of the hardest aspects of Hindi for English speakers. The key: **curl your tongue tip backwards** so it touches the roof of the mouth slightly behind the ridge. Contrast these with the dental त, थ, द, ध, न where the tongue touches the back of the upper front *teeth*. Many Hindi minimal pairs exist: **टन** (ṭan = clang) vs **तन** (tan = body); **डर** (ḍar = fear) vs **दर** (dar = rate/door).

## 2.5 त-वर्ग — Dental Consonants (दंत्य — Dantya)

*Tongue tip touches the back of the upper front teeth — softer than English t/d*

| Devanagari | Name | Translit. | Pronunciation | Example | Meaning |
|------------|------|-----------|---------------|---------|---------|
| त | ta | t | Dental t — softer than English 't' | तालाब (tālāb) | pond |
| थ | tha | th | Aspirated dental t — NOT English 'th'! | थैला (thailā) | bag |
| द | da | d | Dental d — softer than English 'd' | दही (dahī) | yogurt |
| ध | dha | dh | Aspirated dental d | धन (dhan) | wealth / money |
| न | na | n | Dental n | नाम (nām) | name |

> ⚠️ **Critical Note:** **थ and ध are absolutely NOT the English 'th' in *think* or *this*!** They are aspirated dental consonants — your tongue goes against the teeth AND you release a strong puff of breath. Think: 't' + big exhale = थ. 'd' + big exhale = ध. This mistake is very common and important to avoid.

---

## 2.6 प-वर्ग — Labial Consonants (ओष्ठ्य — Oṣṭhya)

*Both lips together*

| Devanagari | Name | Translit. | Pronunciation | Example | Meaning |
|------------|------|-----------|---------------|---------|---------|
| प | pa | p | Like 'p' in *spin* — unaspirated (no puff) | पानी (pānī) | water |
| फ | pha | ph | Aspirated p — like 'ph', sometimes 'f' | फल (phal) | fruit |
| ब | ba | b | Like 'b' in *bat* | बाज़ार (bāzār) | market |
| भ | bha | bh | Aspirated b — breathy voiced | भाई (bhāī) | brother |
| म | ma | m | Like 'm' in *man* | माँ (māṃ) | mother |

## 2.7 Semi-Vowels, Sibilants and Aspirate (अवर्गीय व्यंजन)

These eight consonants fall outside the five main groups:

| Devanagari | Name | Translit. | Pronunciation | Example | Meaning |
|------------|------|-----------|---------------|---------|---------|
| य | ya | y | Like 'y' in *yes* | यहाँ (yahāṃ) | here |
| र | ra | r | A flap/tap 'r' — like a single tap, not rolled | राम (rām) | Ram (name) |
| ल | la | l | Clear 'l' sound | लाल (lāl) | red |
| व | va | v | Between English 'v' and 'w' | वन (van) | forest |
| श | śa | ś / sh | Palatal 'sh' — like 'sh' in *sheep* | शहर (śahar) | city |
| ष | ṣa | ṣ | Retroflex 'sh' — tongue curled back | rarely in modern Hindi | — |
| स | sa | s | Like 's' in *sun* | सब (sab) | all / everyone |
| ह | ha | h | Like 'h' in *hat* | हाथ (hāth) | hand |

> 💡 **Tip:** In modern spoken Hindi, **श** and **ष** are pronounced nearly identically (both like English 'sh'). **व** is pronounced like a 'v' at word beginnings and softer in other positions. **र** is a flap (like the 'r' in Spanish *pero*), not a rolled 'r'.

## 2.8 Aspirated vs Unaspirated Consonants — The Key Distinction

This contrast is one of the most important features of Hindi phonology. English has it too (compare 'k' in *ski* vs *key*) but Hindi makes it **phonemically distinctive** — it changes meaning:

| Feature | Unaspirated | Aspirated |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Definition | No extra breath after the stop | Strong puff of air released after the stop |
| English analogy | 'p' in *spin* (no puff) | 'p' in *pin* (clear puff) |
| Test | Hold hand in front of mouth — no movement | Aspirated: hand moves noticeably |
| Hindi examples | क ग ट ड त द प ब | ख घ ठ ढ थ ध फ भ |

**Minimal pairs — aspiration changes meaning:**

| Unaspirated | Translit. | Meaning | Aspirated | Translit. | Meaning |
|-------------|-----------|---------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| कान | kān | ear | खान | khān | mine (mineral) |
| पल | pal | moment | फल | phal | fruit |
| बाल | bāl | hair | भाल | bhāl | forehead (literary) |
| दन | dan | — | धन | dhan | wealth |
| गल | gal | cheek / to melt | घल | — | — |

## 2.9 Voiced vs Voiceless Consonants

**Voiced** consonants have the vocal cords vibrating; **voiceless** do not:

| Voiceless (अघोष) | Voiced (सघोष) |
|------------------|----------------|
| क, ख (no voice) | ग, घ (voice on) |
| च, छ (no voice) | ज, झ (voice on) |
| ट, ठ (no voice) | ड, ढ (voice on) |
| त, थ (no voice) | द, ध (voice on) |
| प, फ (no voice) | ब, भ (voice on) |

**Test:** Place your hand on your throat. Voiceless = no vibration. Voiced = vibration felt.

---

## 2.10 Retroflex vs Dental — The Critical Distinction

This contrast does not exist in English and is one of the hardest things for English speakers to hear and produce:

| Feature | Dental (त, थ, द, ध, न) | Retroflex (ट, ठ, ड, ढ, ण) |
|---------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Tongue position | Tip against back of upper *teeth* | Tip **curled back** to roof of mouth |
| Sound quality | Softer, thinner | Harder, darker, more resonant |
| English comparison | Like French/Spanish 't' | No English equivalent |

**Minimal pairs:**

| Dental | Translit. | Meaning | Retroflex | Translit. | Meaning |
|--------|-----------|---------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| तन | tan | body | टन | ṭan | clang / ton |
| दर | dar | rate / door | डर | ḍar | fear |
| नल | nal | tap/faucet | — | — | — |

## 2.11 Additional Consonants with Nukta (नुक्ता — ़)

A **nukta** (dot below: ़) is added to certain letters to represent sounds borrowed from **Arabic, Persian, and English** that do not exist in classical Sanskrit:

| Devanagari | Base Letter | Translit. | Pronunciation | Example | Meaning |
|------------|-------------|-----------|---------------|---------|---------|
| क़ | क | qa | Deep 'k' from Arabic — back of throat | क़िला (qilā) | fort |
| ख़ | ख | ḵh | Like 'kh' in German *Bach* | ख़त (khat) | letter (correspondence) |
| ग़ | ग | ġ | Like 'gh' in Arabic/French | ग़लत (ġalat) | wrong, incorrect |
| ज़ | ज | z | Like 'z' in *zoo* | ज़रूर (zarūr) | certainly |
| फ़ | फ | f | Like 'f' in *far* | फ़िल्म (film) | film |

> 💡 **Tip:** **ज़** (za) and **फ़** (fa) are by far the most common nukta letters and appear in many everyday words: ज़िंदगी (life), ज़रूरी (important), फ़र्क (difference), फ़ायदा (benefit). Learn to recognise the nukta dot immediately.
