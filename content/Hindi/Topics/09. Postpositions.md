## 9.1 What Are Postpositions?

Postpositions (परसर्ग — parasarg) are **the Hindi equivalent of English prepositions** — but they come **after** the noun or pronoun, not before it.

**English:** *in* the house — preposition BEFORE the noun
**Hindi:** घर ***में*** — postposition AFTER the noun

**The Oblique Case Rule:** Any noun or pronoun that is followed by a postposition must be in its **oblique case** (see [[04. Nouns, Gender and Cases|Chapter 4]]):
- Direct: **लड़का** (the boy)
- Before postposition: **लड़के** को (to the boy) — लड़के is the oblique form

> [!note] This is one of the most important rules in Hindi grammar. Every time you use a postposition, check that the preceding noun is in its oblique form. With practice, this becomes automatic.

## 9.2 Simple Postpositions (सरल परसर्ग)

| Postposition | Transliteration | English Equivalent | Example | Translation |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|---------|-------------|
| को | ko | to, for (indirect object) | राम को | to Ram |
| में | meṃ | in, into | घर में | in the house |
| पर | par | on, at | मेज़ पर | on the table |
| से | se | from, with, by, than | दिल्ली से | from Delhi |
| तक | tak | until, up to, as far as | कल तक | until tomorrow |
| ने | ne | (agent marker in ने construction) | राम ने | Ram (subject of past transitive) |
| का / की / के | kā / kī / ke | of, 's (possessive) | राम का घर | Ram's house |

> 💡 **Tip on का/की/के:** This possessive postposition agrees with the thing **possessed** (not the possessor): राम का घर (m.s.) = Ram's house; राम की किताब (f.) = Ram's book; राम के दोस्त (m.pl.) = Ram's friends.

## 9.3 Compound Postpositions (संयुक्त परसर्ग)

Compound postpositions are formed with **के + a noun/adverb**. The noun before के must be in the **oblique case**:

| Postposition | Transliteration | English Equivalent | Example | Translation |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|---------|-------------|
| के लिए | ke lie | for (purpose/benefit) | तुम्हारे लिए | for you |
| के साथ | ke sāth | with (accompaniment) | मेरे साथ | with me |
| के बिना | ke binā | without | पानी के बिना | without water |
| के ऊपर | ke ūpar | above, on top of | मेज़ के ऊपर | on top of the table |
| के नीचे | ke nīce | below, under | मेज़ के नीचे | under the table |
| के पास | ke pās | near, next to; have (possession) | स्कूल के पास | near the school |
| के अंदर | ke aṃdar | inside | कमरे के अंदर | inside the room |
| के बाहर | ke bāhar | outside | घर के बाहर | outside the house |
| के बाद | ke bād | after | खाने के बाद | after eating |
| के पहले / से पहले | ke pahle | before | परीक्षा से पहले | before the exam |
| के बारे में | ke bāre meṃ | about, concerning | हिंदी के बारे में | about Hindi |
| के द्वारा | ke dvārā | by means of, by (passive agent) | सरकार के द्वारा | by the government |
| के सामने | ke sāmne | in front of | घर के सामने | in front of the house |
| के पीछे | ke pīche | behind | स्कूल के पीछे | behind the school |
| के बीच | ke bīc | between, among | दो दोस्तों के बीच | between two friends |
| के बावजूद | ke bāvajūd | despite, in spite of | मुश्किलों के बावजूद | despite difficulties |
| की तरह | kī tarah | like, similar to | पक्षी की तरह | like a bird |
| की वजह से | kī vajah se | because of, due to | बारिश की वजह से | because of the rain |

## 9.4 The Postposition को — Multiple Uses

**को** is the most versatile Hindi postposition:

### 1. Indirect Object (to / for)
- मैंने राम **को** किताब दी → I gave Ram a book (to Ram)
- उसने मुझ **को** पैसे दिए → He gave me money

### 2. Destination (to a place — with verbs of motion)
- मैं दिल्ली **को** जाता हूँ → I go to Delhi *(less common; usually just जाता हूँ दिल्ली)*

### 3. Obligation — with चाहिए and पड़ना
- मुझ **को** / मुझे जाना चाहिए → I should go
- उस **को** / उसे काम करना पड़ता है → He/she has to work

### 4. Sensations and Feelings (special to Hindi!)
Hindi uses को to express physical sensations — the **experiencer** takes को:

| Hindi | Transliteration | English |
|-------|-----------------|---------|
| मुझे भूख लगी है | mujhe bhūkh lagī hai | I am hungry (lit: hunger is felt by me) |
| मुझे प्यास लगी है | mujhe pyās lagī hai | I am thirsty |
| मुझे ठंड लग रही है | mujhe ṭhaṃḍ lag rahī hai | I am feeling cold |
| मुझे नींद आ रही है | mujhe nīṃd ā rahī hai | I am feeling sleepy |
| मुझे बुखार है | mujhe bukhār hai | I have a fever |
| मुझे दर्द है | mujhe dard hai | I am in pain |
| मुझे खुशी हुई | mujhe khuśī huī | I felt happy |

> [!note] In Hindi, you do **not** say "मैं भूखा हूँ" for "I am hungry" — instead you say **मुझे भूख लगी है** (hunger has struck me). This को-construction for sensations is very important and very different from English. Learn these phrases as fixed expressions.

## 9.5 The Postposition से — Multiple Uses

**से** is also highly versatile:

| Use | Example | Translation |
|-----|---------|-------------|
| From (origin/source) | दिल्ली से आया | came from Delhi |
| With (instrument/means) | कलम से लिखो | write with a pen |
| By (agent — cause) | बुखार से बीमार | sick from fever |
| Than (comparison) | राम श्याम से लंबा | Ram is taller than Shyam |
| Since (time) | कल से | since yesterday |
| Away from | घर से दूर | far from home |

## 9.6 में vs पर — In vs On

These two are frequently confused:

| Postposition | Use | Examples |
|--------------|-----|---------|
| **में** | Enclosed/contained inside something | घर में (in the house), कमरे में (in the room), बैग में (in the bag), भारत में (in India) |
| **पर** | On a surface; at a location; on a vehicle | मेज़ पर (on the table), बस पर (on the bus), स्टेशन पर (at the station), छत पर (on the roof) |

**Tricky cases:**
- बस **पर** (on the bus) — NOT बस में (even though you're inside!)
- but: ट्रेन **में** (in the train) — both forms exist for trains

## 9.7 Oblique Case Before Compound Postpositions — Summary

| Direct Form | Oblique (before ke/kī) | Example |
|-------------|------------------------|---------|
| लड़का (boy, m.) | लड़के | लड़के के लिए (for the boy) |
| कमरा (room, m.) | कमरे | कमरे के अंदर (inside the room) |
| घर (house, m.) | घर | घर के बाहर (outside the house) — no change for consonant-final |
| लड़की (girl, f.) | लड़की | लड़की के साथ (with the girl) |
| माँ (mother, f.) | माँ | माँ के बारे में (about mother) |
| मैं → मेरे | मेरे | मेरे लिए (for me) |
| वह → उस | उस | उसके पास (near him/her) |
