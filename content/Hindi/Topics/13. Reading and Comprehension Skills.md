## 13.1 Paper 2 Structure — Reading (पठन परीक्षा)

Paper 2 tests your ability to **read and understand Hindi texts**. You have **1 hour** for **50 marks**.

| Section | Task Types | Marks | Text Type |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Section A | Multiple choice, True/False, Matching | ~20 | One or two shorter texts |
| Section B | Short answer questions (written in Hindi) | ~20 | One medium-length text |
| Section C | Structured/extended response, notes, summary | ~10 | One longer text |

**Text types you may encounter:**
- समाचार लेख (samācār lekh) — newspaper article
- पत्र (patra) — letter (formal or informal)
- सूचना / विज्ञापन (sūcnā / vijñāpan) — notice / advertisement
- ब्लॉग पोस्ट (blog post) — blog / diary entry
- छोटी कहानी (choṭī kahānī) — short story extract
- विवरण (vivaran) — description / information text

## 13.2 Reading Strategies (पठन रणनीतियाँ)

### Before Reading
1. **Read the questions first** — know what you are looking for before diving into the text
2. **Note the text type** — a letter will have different conventions from a newspaper article
3. **Look at any titles, headings, or subheadings** — they give crucial context
4. **Scan numbers, names, and dates** — these are often directly testable

### During Reading
5. **Underline key words** as you read (if permitted to write on the paper)
6. **Work through the text systematically** — don't skip around randomly
7. **Match question keywords to text keywords** — look for synonyms and paraphrases
8. **Don't panic about unknown words** — use context to infer meaning (§13.6)

### After Reading
9. **Check your answers** refer to specific parts of the text, not general knowledge
10. **For short answer questions**, write in complete or near-complete Hindi phrases
11. **Re-read the question** after writing your answer to confirm you answered what was asked

## 13.3 Types of Questions (प्रश्नों के प्रकार)

### 1. सही/गलत (Sahī / Galat — True/False)

**Strategy:** Find the sentence in the text that directly relates to the statement. Don't use general knowledge — base your answer entirely on what the text says.

- Beware of statements that are partially true — they count as false
- Look for absolute words like "हमेशा" (always), "कभी नहीं" (never) — these are often false

### 2. बहुविकल्पीय (Bahuvikalpīya — Multiple Choice)

**Strategy:** Eliminate obviously wrong answers first. Then compare the remaining options against the exact text.

- Watch for distractors — options that sound right but use words not in the text
- The correct answer is often a paraphrase (different words, same meaning)

### 3. संक्षिप्त उत्तर (Saṃkṣipt Uttar — Short Answer)

**Strategy:** Answer in Hindi using words from or closely based on the text. You usually need:
- A key fact (name, number, reason, description)
- Sometimes a full sentence, sometimes a word or phrase

### 4. रिक्त-स्थान पूर्ति (Rikt-sthān Pūrti — Gap Fill)

**Strategy:** Read both before and after the gap. Check: grammar (what part of speech fits?), meaning (what makes sense?), and the text (is the answer stated directly?).

### 5. नोट्स लेना (Notes Taking)

**Strategy:** Extract specific pieces of information under given headings. Use concise Hindi phrases — you do not need complete sentences unless instructed.

### 6. सारांश (Sārāṃś — Summary)

**Strategy:** Identify the main points only. Do not copy chunks of text — paraphrase in your own words where possible. Stay within the word count.

## 13.4 Scanning for Specific Information

Scanning = reading quickly to find a specific piece of information (name, number, date, place).

**Practise scanning for:**
- **Numbers and statistics:** अस्सी प्रतिशत (80%), पाँच हज़ार (5,000)
- **Names of people/places:** proper nouns, often written with capital-equivalent conventions
- **Dates and times:** तारीख, समय, साल, महीना
- **Prices and measurements:** रुपये, किलोग्राम, किलोमीटर

> 💡 **Tip:** When scanning, let your eye sweep across the page looking for numerals, capital letters (for transliterated proper nouns), or specific Devanagari characters you associate with the target word.

## 13.5 Inference Questions — Reading Between the Lines

Some questions ask you to **infer** meaning not directly stated:

**Types of inference questions:**
- "इससे क्या पता चलता है...?" (What does this suggest...?)
- "लेखक के अनुसार..." (According to the author...)
- "लेखक किस बारे में चिंतित है?" (What is the author worried about?)

**How to answer inference questions:**
1. Find the relevant part of the text
2. Identify the **implication** — what must be true *given* what is said?
3. Express this implication in your answer

**Avoid:** Importing information not in the text / making guesses not supported by the text.

## 13.6 Vocabulary in Context — Guessing Unknown Words

When you encounter an unknown word:

1. **Look at the surrounding sentence** — what role does this word play?
2. **Look at the word's form** — is it a noun (ends in typical noun pattern)? A verb (ends in -nā or conjugated)?
3. **Look for cognates** — Hindi has many English loanwords (कंप्यूटर, टेलीविज़न, मोबाइल)
4. **Use the overall context** — what is this passage about?
5. **Check if the meaning changes your answer** — sometimes you can answer correctly even with an unknown word

## 13.7 Common Text Conventions

### Newspaper Articles (समाचार लेख)
- Start with headline (शीर्षक) and location/date
- Inverted pyramid structure — most important information first
- Formal language, passive constructions, reported speech

### Letters (पत्र)
- **Formal (औपचारिक):** Sender address, date, designation, विषय (subject), महोदय (salutation), signature
- **Informal (अनौपचारिक):** प्रिय [नाम] (Dear [Name]), casual language, personal closing

### Advertisements / Notices (विज्ञापन / सूचना)
- Bold headings, bullet points
- Key details: date, time, place, price, contact
- Action phrases: अभी संपर्क करें (contact now), सीमित सीटें (limited seats)

## 13.9 Common Errors to Avoid

| Error | How to Avoid |
|-------|-------------|
| Answering from general knowledge instead of the text | Always quote or reference the specific text |
| Copying entire sentences from the text | Paraphrase where possible; extract only what answers the question |
| Giving English answers in a Hindi exam | Always answer in Hindi |
| Missing negatives (नहीं, मत, न) | Underline negatives; they completely reverse meaning |
| Confusing कल (yesterday/tomorrow) | Check tense to determine which meaning applies |
| Ignoring the last part of a sentence | Hindi verbs come at the end — never skip the verb! |
| Writing illegibly in Devanagari | Practise neat script; marks can be lost if answers cannot be read |
