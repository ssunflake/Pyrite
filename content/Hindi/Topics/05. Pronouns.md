## 5.1 Overview of Hindi Pronouns

Hindi pronouns are more complex than English pronouns in two key ways:
1. **Three levels of "you"** — reflecting social register and respect (§5.6)
2. **Oblique forms** — all pronouns change form before postpositions

---

## 5.2 Personal Pronouns — Direct and Oblique Forms

| Person | Direct Form | Transliteration | English | Oblique Form | Translit. |
|--------|------------|-----------------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| 1st singular | मैं | maiṃ | I | मुझ | mujh |
| 1st plural | हम | ham | we | हम | ham |
| 2nd intimate | तू | tū | you (very intimate) | तुझ | tujh |
| 2nd familiar | तुम | tum | you (familiar) | तुम | tum |
| 2nd formal | आप | āp | you (polite/formal) | आप | āp |
| 3rd near singular | यह | yah | he / she / this (near) | इस | is |
| 3rd near plural | ये | ye | they / these (near) | इन | in |
| 3rd far singular | वह | vah | he / she / that (far) | उस | us |
| 3rd far plural | वे | ve | they / those (far) | उन | un |

> [!note] Hindi has **no separate gendered he/she** distinction. **यह** and **वह** mean both "he" and "she" — the gender is understood from context or the verb agreement. The near/far distinction (यह/वह) indicates physical or conceptual proximity to the speaker.

## 5.3 Pronouns with Common Postpositions

This is the most important pronoun table for practical use. Memorise it fully.

| Pronoun | को (ko) to/for | में (meṃ) in | से (se) from/by | के लिए (ke lie) for | के साथ (ke sāth) with |
|---------|----------------|-------------|-----------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| मैं | मुझे / मुझको | मुझमें | मुझसे | मेरे लिए | मेरे साथ |
| हम | हमें / हमको | हममें | हमसे | हमारे लिए | हमारे साथ |
| तू | तुझे / तुझको | तुझमें | तुझसे | तेरे लिए | तेरे साथ |
| तुम | तुम्हें / तुमको | तुममें | तुमसे | तुम्हारे लिए | तुम्हारे साथ |
| आप | आपको | आपमें | आपसे | आपके लिए | आपके साथ |
| यह (near) | इसे / इसको | इसमें | इससे | इसके लिए | इसके साथ |
| ये (near pl) | इन्हें / इनको | इनमें | इनसे | इनके लिए | इनके साथ |
| वह (far) | उसे / उसको | उसमें | उससे | उसके लिए | उसके साथ |
| वे (far pl) | उन्हें / उनको | उनमें | उनसे | उनके लिए | उनके साथ |

**Example sentences:**
- **मुझे** भूख लगी है → I am hungry (lit: hunger has struck me)
- **उसे** बुखार है → He/she has a fever
- यह किताब **मेरे लिए** है → This book is for me
- **हमसे** मिलो → Meet us / Come and meet us
- **आपके साथ** जाऊँगा → I will go with you (formal)

## 5.4 Possessive Pronouns / Adjectives (स्वामित्व सर्वनाम)

Possessives in Hindi **agree with the possessed object** (not the owner) in gender and number:

| Owner | Masc Singular | Masc Plural / Before Postposition | Feminine |
|-------|--------------|----------------------------------|---------|
| मैं (I) | मेरा | मेरे | मेरी |
| हम (we) | हमारा | हमारे | हमारी |
| तू (you, intimate) | तेरा | तेरे | तेरी |
| तुम (you, familiar) | तुम्हारा | तुम्हारे | तुम्हारी |
| आप (you, formal) | आपका | आपके | आपकी |
| यह / वह (he/she near) | इसका / उसका | इसके / उसके | इसकी / उसकी |
| ये / वे (they) | इनका / उनका | इनके / उनके | इनकी / उनकी |

**Examples of possessive agreement:**
- मेरा **घर** (my house — घर is masc singular) → मेरा
- मेरी **किताब** (my book — किताब is fem) → मेरी
- मेरे **दोस्त** (my friends — plural) → मेरे
- उसका **बेटा** (his/her son) → उसका
- उसकी **बेटी** (his/her daughter) → उसकी

> 💡 **Tip:** The possessive changes to agree with **the thing possessed**, not the person doing the possessing. So a female speaker says मेरा घर (my house) — not मेरी घर — because घर is masculine.

## 5.5 Demonstrative Pronouns (संकेतवाचक सर्वनाम)

| Hindi | Transliteration | Singular/Plural | Distance | English |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|----------|---------|
| यह | yah | Singular | Near | this / he / she |
| ये | ye | Plural | Near | these / they |
| वह | vah | Singular | Far | that / he / she |
| वे | ve | Plural | Far | those / they |

> [!note] In spoken Hindi, **यह** is often pronounced **ये** (ye) in singular too, and **वह** is often pronounced **वो** (vo). You will hear this constantly. The written forms remain यह and वह in standard Hindi.

**As demonstrative adjectives (before a noun):**
- यह किताब (this book) vs वह किताब (that book)
- ये लड़के (these boys) vs वे लड़के (those boys)

---

## 5.6 Question Pronouns (प्रश्नवाचक सर्वनाम)

| Hindi | Transliteration | Oblique | English | Example |
|-------|-----------------|---------|---------|---------|
| कौन | kaun | किस/किन (kis/kin) | who (person) | कौन आया? (Who came?) |
| क्या | kyā | — | what (thing) | यह क्या है? (What is this?) |
| कौन-सा/सी | kaun-sā/sī | किस | which | कौन-सी किताब? (Which book?) |

**Oblique forms with postpositions:**
- किसे (kise) = to whom — किसे फ़ोन आया? (To whom did the call come?)
- किसका (kiskā) = whose — यह किसका घर है? (Whose house is this?)
- किससे (kisse) = from whom — आप किससे मिले? (Whom did you meet?)

## 5.7 The Reflexive Pronoun — अपना (Apnā)

**अपना / अपनी / अपने** means "one's own" and refers back to the subject of the sentence. It is used when the possessor IS the subject:

| Form | Masculine | Feminine | Plural/Obl |
|------|-----------|----------|------------|
| अपना | अपना | अपनी | अपने |

**Examples:**
- राम **अपना** घर देखता है → Ram looks at his own house (अपना = Ram's)
- सीता **अपनी** किताब पढ़ती है → Sita reads her own book
- हम **अपने** दोस्तों से मिले → We met our own friends

**Contrast:**
- राम **उसका** घर देखता है → Ram looks at *his* (someone else's) house
- राम **अपना** घर देखता है → Ram looks at *his own* house

> [!note] Forgetting to use अपना when referring back to the subject is a very common error. If the possessor is the subject of the sentence, always use अपना/अपनी/अपने.

## 5.8 The Honorific System (आदर — Ādar)

Hindi has **three levels of "you"**, reflecting social relationship and respect:

| Pronoun | Register | Used for | Verb ending |
|---------|---------|----------|-------------|
| **तू** (tū) | Intimate / Rude | Very close intimates; deity in prayer; can be insulting to strangers | Singular |
| **तुम** (tum) | Familiar | Friends, younger relatives, children | Plural (grammatically) |
| **आप** (āp) | Formal / Respectful | Elders, strangers, teachers, formal situations | Plural (grammatically) |

> 💡 **Exam Tip:** For IGCSE writing tasks, use **आप** in formal letters and **तुम** in informal letters to friends. Using तू with a stranger is considered rude in standard Hindi. आप is always the safest choice when uncertain.

## 5.9 Key Definitions

| Term | Hindi | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-------|-----------------|------------|
| Pronoun | सर्वनाम | sarvanām | A word replacing a noun |
| Personal pronoun | पुरुषवाचक सर्वनाम | puruṣavācak sarvanām | I, we, you, he, she, they |
| Possessive | स्वामित्ववाचक | svāmitva vācak | my, your, his, her, our, their |
| Demonstrative | संकेतवाचक | saṃketavācak | this, that, these, those |
| Reflexive | निजवाचक | nijvācak | अपना — one's own |
| Interrogative | प्रश्नवाचक | praśnavācak | who, what, which |
| Direct form | प्रथमा | prathamā | Form before no postposition |
| Oblique form | तिर्यक | tiryak | Form before postpositions |
| Near | निकट | nikaṭ | यह, ये — near the speaker |
| Far | दूर | dūr | वह, वे — away from the speaker |
