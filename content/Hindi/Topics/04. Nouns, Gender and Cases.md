## 4.1 Grammatical Gender (लिंग — Liṃg)

Hindi has **two grammatical genders**:
- **Masculine (पुल्लिंग — Pulliṃg):** abbreviated **m.**
- **Feminine (स्त्रीलिंग — Strīliṃg):** abbreviated **f.**

**Why gender is critical:**
- Adjectives change form to **agree** with the noun's gender and number
- Verbs in past tense agree with the gender of the subject (or object, in ने constructions)
- Postpositions often require oblique forms that differ by gender

> [!note] Unlike French or Spanish where most genders follow patterns, Hindi gender is often unpredictable and must be learnt with each new noun. **Always learn gender together with vocabulary.**

---

## 4.2 Patterns for Determining Gender

### Reliable Masculine Patterns (पुल्लिंग के नियम)

| Pattern | Examples | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Nouns ending in **-ā** | लड़का (boy), कमरा (room), घोड़ा (horse), रास्ता (road) | Very reliable — nearly all -ā nouns are masculine |
| Male animate beings | बाप (father), भाई (brother), शेर (lion) | Natural gender overrides form |
| Names of mountains | हिमालय, विंध्याचल | |
| Days and months | सोमवार, जनवरी | |
| Oceans and seas | हिंद महासागर | |

### Reliable Feminine Patterns (स्त्रीलिंग के नियम)

| Pattern | Examples | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Nouns ending in **-ī** | लड़की (girl), नानी (grandmother), घड़ी (watch), रोटी (bread) | Very reliable |
| Nouns ending in **-iyā** | चिड़िया (bird), गुड़िया (doll) | |
| Female animate beings | माँ (mother), बहन (sister), बिल्ली (cat) | |
| Names of rivers | गंगा, यमुना, नर्मदा, सरस्वती | All rivers are feminine |
| Names of languages | हिंदी, अंग्रेज़ी, संस्कृत, उर्दू | All languages are feminine |
| Abstract nouns in **-tā** | सुंदरता (beauty), अच्छाई (goodness) | |

### Must-Memorise Exceptions

| Hindi | Gender | Transliteration | English | Why Tricky |
|-------|--------|-----------------|---------|-----------|
| घर | m | ghar | house | Consonant-final — no gender clue |
| पानी | m | pānī | water | Ends in -ī but masculine! |
| आदमी | m | ādmī | man | Ends in -ī but masculine! |
| दूध | m | dūdh | milk | Consonant-final |
| पहाड़ | m | pahāṛ | mountain | Consonant-final |
| मेज़ | f | mez | table | Consonant-final, feminine |
| किताब | f | kitāb | book | Consonant-final, feminine |
| रात | f | rāt | night | Consonant-final, feminine |
| बात | f | bāt | talk / matter | Consonant-final, feminine |
| आँख | f | āṃkh | eye | Consonant-final, feminine |
| नाक | f | nāk | nose | Consonant-final, feminine |
| कान | m | kān | ear | Masculine — note: ear is masculine! |

> 💡 **Tip:** The fastest way to learn genders is to use gender-marked words in sentences. Say अच्छा घर (good house — masc) and अच्छी किताब (good book — fem). The adjective immediately tells you the gender.

## 4.3 Changing Masculine to Feminine

Many paired nouns follow regular gender-change patterns:

| Rule | Masculine | Feminine | English Pair |
|------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **-ā → -ī** | लड़का | लड़की | boy → girl |
| **-ā → -ī** | बेटा | बेटी | son → daughter |
| **-ā → -ī** | घोड़ा | घोड़ी | horse → mare |
| **-ā → -ī** | नाना | नानी | maternal grandfather → maternal grandmother |
| **-ā → -ī** | दादा | दादी | paternal grandfather → paternal grandmother |
| **-ak → -ikā** | शिक्षक | शिक्षिका | male teacher → female teacher |
| **stem + -inī** | मोर | मोरनी | peacock → peahen |
| **stem + -inī** | शेर | शेरनी | lion → lioness |
| **-a → -ā / -ī change** | छात्र | छात्रा | male student → female student |
| **-tā → -trī** | अभिनेता | अभिनेत्री | actor → actress |

## 4.4 Noun Cases and the Oblique Case (कारक — Kārak)

Hindi uses **postpositions** (words placed after the noun) to show grammatical function. Before any postposition, the noun must take its **oblique case** form.

**The Three Main Cases:**

| Case | Hindi Name | Usage | Example |
|------|-----------|-------|---------|
| Direct (कर्ता) | karta kārak | Subject of the verb | **लड़का** जाता है — The boy goes |
| Oblique | vibhakti | Before any postposition | **लड़के** को — to the boy |
| Vocative (संबोधन) | saṃbodhana | Directly addressing someone | **लड़के!** — Hey, boy! |

---

## 4.5 Oblique Case Forms

### Masculine -ā Nouns (most common change)

| Form | Singular | Plural |
|------|---------|--------|
| Direct | लड़का | लड़के |
| Oblique | लड़के | लड़कों |
| Vocative | लड़के! | लड़को! |

**Worked examples:**
- लड़के **को** किताब दो → Give the book **to the boy** (लड़के = oblique singular)
- सभी लड़कों **को** बुलाओ → Call all the boys (लड़कों = oblique plural)
- कमरे **में** जाओ → Go into the room (कमरे = oblique of कमरा)
- दरवाज़े **के पास** → near the door (दरवाज़े = oblique of दरवाज़ा)

### Other Masculine Nouns (consonant-final — no change in singular)

| Form | Singular | Plural |
|------|---------|--------|
| Direct | घर | घर |
| Oblique | घर | घरों |

### Feminine Nouns

Feminine nouns do **not** change in the singular oblique. They only change in the plural:

| Type | Direct Singular | Oblique Singular | Direct Plural | Oblique Plural |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| -ī ending | लड़की | लड़की | लड़कियाँ | लड़कियों |
| Consonant-final | रात | रात | रातें | रातों |
| Consonant-final | बात | बात | बातें | बातों |

---

## 4.6 Plural Formation (बहुवचन — Bahuvacana)

### Masculine Nouns — Plural Rules

| Noun Type | Singular | Plural | Rule |
|-----------|---------|--------|------|
| Ending in **-ā** | लड़का (boy) | लड़के (boys) | -ā → -e |
| Ending in **-ā** | कमरा (room) | कमरे (rooms) | -ā → -e |
| Consonant-final | घर (house) | घर (houses) | No change |
| Ending in **-ī** | आदमी (man) | आदमी (men) | No change |
| Ending in **-ū** | आलू (potato) | आलू (potatoes) | No change |

### Feminine Nouns — Plural Rules

| Noun Type | Singular | Plural | Rule |
|-----------|---------|--------|------|
| Ending in **-ī** | लड़की (girl) | लड़कियाँ (girls) | -ī → -iyāṃ |
| Ending in **-ī** | घड़ी (watch) | घड़ियाँ (watches) | -ī → -iyāṃ |
| Ending in **-ī** | नदी (river) | नदियाँ (rivers) | -ī → -iyāṃ |
| Consonant-final | रात (night) | रातें (nights) | add -eṃ |
| Consonant-final | औरत (woman) | औरतें (women) | add -eṃ |
| Consonant-final | किताब (book) | किताबें (books) | add -eṃ |

## 4.7 Model Sentences Using Nouns

| Hindi | Transliteration | English | Grammar Note |
|-------|-----------------|---------|--------------|
| लड़का स्कूल जाता है। | laṛkā skūl jātā hai. | The boy goes to school. | Direct case (subject) |
| लड़की किताब पढ़ती है। | laṛkī kitāb paṛhtī hai. | The girl reads a book. | लड़की = feminine subject |
| कमरे में एक मेज़ है। | kamre meṃ ek mez hai. | There is a table in the room. | कमरे = oblique of कमरा |
| माँ बाज़ार गई। | māṃ bāzār gaī. | Mother went to the market. | Feminine past agreement |
| सभी लड़कियाँ खुश हैं। | sabhī laṛkiyāṃ khuś haiṃ. | All the girls are happy. | Plural feminine noun |

## 4.8 Key Definitions

| Term | Hindi | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-------|-----------------|------------|
| Noun | संज्ञा | saṃjñā | A name word — person, place, thing, idea |
| Gender | लिंग | liṃg | Grammatical classification: masculine or feminine |
| Masculine | पुल्लिंग | pulliṃg | Masculine grammatical gender |
| Feminine | स्त्रीलिंग | strīliṃg | Feminine grammatical gender |
| Singular | एकवचन | ekavacana | Referring to one thing |
| Plural | बहुवचन | bahuvacana | Referring to more than one |
| Direct case | कर्ता कारक | kartā kārak | Nominative — the subject form |
| Oblique case | कर्म कारक | karm kārak | Form used before postpositions |
| Vocative | संबोधन | saṃbodhana | Form used when addressing someone |
| Postposition | परसर्ग | parasarg | Word placed after noun (Hindi equivalent of prepositions) |
| Case | कारक | kārak | Grammatical form showing noun's role in sentence |
