## 📝 Exam Papers

| Paper | Type | Duration | Marks | % of Total |
|-------|------|----------|-------|------------|
| Paper 1 | Listening (श्रवण) | 45 minutes | 40 | 30% |
| Paper 2 | Reading (पठन) | 1 hour | 50 | 40% |
| Paper 3 | Writing (लेखन) | 1 hour | 40 | 30% |

### Paper 1 — Listening (श्रवण परीक्षा)
- Short conversations and announcements → multiple choice, gap-fill
- Longer dialogues and monologues → short answer, note completion
- Each recording is played **twice**
- All questions and rubric in Hindi

### Paper 2 — Reading (पठन परीक्षा)
- **Section A:** Multiple choice, true/false, matching tasks
- **Section B:** Short answer questions (answers written in Hindi)
- **Section C:** Longer structured response; summary writing
- Text types: newspaper articles, letters, notices, advertisements, short stories

### Paper 3 — Writing (लेखन परीक्षा)
- **Task A (~100 words):** Note, message, postcard, short informational paragraph
- **Task B (~200 words):** Formal/informal letter, essay/article, story continuation, description

## 🗺️ Suggested Study Roadmap

1. **Weeks 1–2:** Chapters 1–3 — Master the Devanagari script before anything else
2. **Weeks 3–4:** Chapters 4–7 — Nouns, pronouns, all three verb tenses
3. **Week 5:** Chapters 8–9 — Adjectives, adverbs, postpositions
4. **Weeks 6–7:** Chapters 10–12 — Themed vocabulary with example sentences
5. **Week 8:** Chapters 13–14 — Exam technique for Papers 2 and 3
6. **Weeks 9–10:** Revision and timed past paper practice

---

> 💡 **Exam Tip — The Single Most Important Piece of Advice:** Learn the **Devanagari script** *before* you study any vocabulary or grammar. Without fluent reading ability, you cannot properly engage with Hindi text. The transliterations throughout these notes serve as a temporary crutch — aim to stop needing them as quickly as possible. Spend your first two weeks practising every vowel, consonant, and mātrā until reading feels natural and automatic. Everything else in this course builds on that foundation.
