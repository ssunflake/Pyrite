## Pythagoras' Theorem

In a **right-angled triangle**, with hypotenuse c (the longest side, opposite the right angle):

$$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$

> [!example] Find hypotenuse: legs 5 cm and 12 cm.
> c² = 5² + 12² = 25 + 144 = 169
> c = **13 cm**

> [!example] Find a leg: hypotenuse 10 cm, one leg 6 cm.
> a² = 10² − 6² = 100 − 36 = 64
> a = **8 cm**

> [!warning] Always identify the hypotenuse first — it's always opposite the 90° angle.

### Pythagorean Triples
Integer-sided right triangles. Memorise:
- (3, 4, 5) and multiples: (6, 8, 10), (9, 12, 15), (15, 20, 25)
- (5, 12, 13)
- (8, 15, 17)
- (7, 24, 25)

## Pythagoras in 2D

### Distance Between Two Points
d = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²]

This is just Pythagoras — the horizontal and vertical distances are the two legs.

### Diagonal of a Rectangle
d = √(l² + w²)

### Finding Height / Slant
Use Pythagoras when any right-angled triangle can be identified.

> [!example] An isosceles triangle has equal sides 10 cm and base 12 cm. Find height.
> Drop perpendicular to base → right triangle with hypotenuse 10, base 6
> h² = 10² − 6² = 64 → h = **8 cm**

---

## Pythagoras in 3D

### Diagonal of a Cuboid
d = √(l² + w² + h²)

> [!example] Cuboid 3 × 4 × 5 cm. Find space diagonal.
> d = √(9 + 16 + 25) = √50 = **5√2 cm**

### Method for 3D Problems
1. Identify the right-angled triangle(s) in the 3D shape
2. Apply Pythagoras step by step — often needs two applications

> [!example] A pyramid has a square base of side 8 cm and height 6 cm. Find slant edge (corner to apex).
> Step 1: diagonal of base = √(8² + 8²) = √128 = 8√2
> Half-diagonal = 4√2
> Step 2: slant edge = √((4√2)² + 6²) = √(32 + 36) = √68 = **2√17 cm**

## Similar Shapes

Two shapes are **similar** if they have the same shape but different sizes:
- All corresponding angles are equal
- All corresponding sides are in the same ratio (the **scale factor**)

### Identifying Similar Triangles
Two triangles are similar if any of these hold:
- **AA** — two pairs of equal angles (most common)
- **SSS** — all three pairs of sides in the same ratio
- **SAS** — two sides in ratio and included angle equal

> [!example] Triangles ABC and PQR: ∠A = ∠P, ∠B = ∠Q → AA → **similar**

### Finding Missing Lengths in Similar Triangles
Set up a ratio equation using corresponding sides.

> [!example] In similar triangles, one triangle has sides 3, 4, 5. Corresponding side in larger triangle = 12 (matches the "4" side). Find the other sides.
> Scale factor = 12/4 = 3
> Other sides = 3 × 3 = 9 and 5 × 3 = **15**

## Scale Factor, Area Factor, Volume Factor

If two similar shapes have linear scale factor k:

| Quantity | Factor |
|----------|--------|
| Lengths, perimeters | k |
| Areas | k² |
| Volumes, masses (same density) | k³ |

> [!example] Two similar cylinders: radii 3 cm and 5 cm.
> Linear SF = 5/3
> Area ratio = (5/3)² = 25/9
> Volume ratio = (5/3)³ = 125/27
> If smaller volume = 54 cm³, larger = 54 × 125/27 = **250 cm³**

> [!tip] Working backwards (given area ratio):
> Area ratio = k² → k = √(area ratio)
> Volume ratio = k³ → k = ∛(volume ratio)

## Similar Triangles in Problems

Commonly tested: **ladder, shadow, cross-section** problems.

> [!example] A tree casts a 9 m shadow. A 2 m post nearby casts a 3 m shadow at the same time. Find tree height.
> Similar triangles (same angle of elevation of sun)
> h/2 = 9/3 → h = **6 m**

### Triangles Divided by a Parallel Line
If PQ is parallel to BC inside triangle ABC:
- Triangle APQ ~ Triangle ABC (AA)
- AP/AB = AQ/AC = PQ/BC

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Hypotenuse | Longest side of a right triangle; opposite the right angle |
| Pythagorean triple | Set of three integers satisfying a² + b² = c² |
| Similar | Same shape, proportional sides, equal angles |
| Congruent | Same shape AND same size |
| Scale factor (k) | Ratio of corresponding lengths in similar shapes |
| Area factor | k² — ratio of corresponding areas in similar shapes |
| Volume factor | k³ — ratio of corresponding volumes in similar shapes |
| Space diagonal | The diagonal of a cuboid connecting opposite vertices |
