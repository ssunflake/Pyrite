## Core Financial Formulas

```
Profit / Loss  =  Selling price − Cost price
% profit/loss  =  (profit or loss / cost price) × 100

Simple interest:
  I  =  PRT / 100        (P = principal, R = rate%, T = years)
  Total = P + I

Compound interest / growth:
  A  =  P × (1 + r/100)ⁿ    (n = number of periods)

Depreciation / decay:
  V  =  P × (1 − r/100)ⁿ
```

---

## VAT

```
Price inc. VAT  =  original × (1 + VAT rate/100)
Original price  =  price inc. VAT ÷ (1 + VAT rate/100)
```

---

## Exchange Rates

```
Foreign currency  =  home currency × exchange rate
Home currency     =  foreign currency ÷ exchange rate
```

---

## Hire Purchase

```
HP total  =  deposit + (n instalments × instalment amount)
Extra cost = HP total − cash price
```

---

## Earnings

```
Weekly pay    =  hours × hourly rate
Overtime pay  =  overtime hours × (hourly rate × multiplier)
Commission    =  sales value × commission rate
Tax owed      =  (gross income − allowance) × tax rate
Net pay       =  gross pay − tax − other deductions
```

---

## Comparison: Simple vs Compound Interest

```
Same P, r, n — compound always gives MORE interest than simple.
Simple:   linear growth  (straight line graph)
Compound: exponential growth (curved graph)
```
