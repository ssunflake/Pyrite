## Earnings

### Wages and Salaries
- **Salary**: fixed annual amount, paid monthly or weekly
- **Wage**: paid by hours worked
- **Overtime**: often paid at 1.5× (time-and-a-half) or 2× (double time) the normal rate

> [!example] Normal rate £12/hr, 35 hrs/week. Overtime (5 hrs) at 1.5×.
> Normal pay = 35 × 12 = £420
> Overtime = 5 × 12 × 1.5 = £90
> Total = **£510**

### Commission
A percentage of sales earned as pay.

> [!example] 8% commission on £3200 in sales: 0.08 × 3200 = **£256**

---

## Income Tax

Earnings above a **tax-free allowance** are taxed.

> [!example] Annual salary £28 000. Tax-free allowance £12 570. Tax rate 20%.
> Taxable income = 28 000 − 12 570 = £15 430
> Tax = 0.20 × 15 430 = **£3 086**

## Profit, Loss and Percentage

$$\text{Profit/Loss} = \text{Selling price} - \text{Cost price}$$

$$\text{Percentage profit/loss} = \frac{\text{Profit or loss}}{\text{Cost price}} \times 100\%$$

> [!example] Bought for £80, sold for £92.
> Profit = £12; % profit = (12/80) × 100 = **15%**

> [!example] Bought for £50, sold for £35.
> Loss = £15; % loss = (15/50) × 100 = **30%**

---

## Simple Interest

Interest is calculated on the **original principal** only.

$$I = \frac{PRT}{100}$$

where P = principal, R = rate (%), T = time (years)

> [!example] £2000 at 5% per year for 3 years:
> I = (2000 × 5 × 3)/100 = **£300**
> Total = £2000 + £300 = £2300

---

## Compound Interest

Interest is calculated on the **principal + accumulated interest**.

$$A = P\left(1 + \frac{r}{100}\right)^n$$

where A = final amount, P = principal, r = rate (%), n = number of periods

> [!example] £1500 at 4% compound interest for 5 years:
> A = 1500 × (1.04)⁵ = 1500 × 1.2167 ≈ **£1825**

> [!example] Find compound interest earned:
> CI = A − P = 1825 − 1500 = **£325**

### Depreciation
An asset loses value each year (e.g. a car).
Use the same formula with (1 − r/100):

$$V = P\left(1 - \frac{r}{100}\right)^n$$

> [!example] Car worth £12 000 depreciates at 15% per year for 3 years:
> V = 12 000 × (0.85)³ = 12 000 × 0.6141 ≈ **£7370**

## Exchange Rates

Used to convert between currencies.

> [!example] £1 = $1.28
> Convert £350 to dollars: 350 × 1.28 = **$448**
> Convert $320 to pounds: 320 ÷ 1.28 = **£250**

> [!tip] Always check: multiplying gives more of the cheaper currency, dividing goes back to the stronger currency.

---

## Hire Purchase

Buy now, pay later — usually with a **deposit** plus regular instalments.

Total HP cost = Deposit + (Number of instalments × Instalment amount)
Extra cost = HP total − Cash price

> [!example] TV costs £600 cash. HP: £60 deposit + 24 monthly payments of £26.
> HP total = 60 + (24 × 26) = 60 + 624 = **£684**
> Extra cost = 684 − 600 = **£84**

---

## Loans and Repayments

> [!example] Loan of £5000 at 8% compound interest per year. Repaid in 2 years.
> Amount owed = 5000 × (1.08)² = **£5832**
> Monthly payment ≈ 5832/24 ≈ **£243**

---

## Budgeting and Bills

Reading utility bills, understanding standing charges, unit costs.

> [!example] Electricity: 300 units at 28p/unit + £15 standing charge.
> Cost = 300 × 0.28 + 15 = 84 + 15 = **£99**

---

## Value Added Tax (VAT)

A tax added to goods/services (rate varies by country).

> [!example] Item costs £80 before VAT. VAT = 20%.
> VAT amount = 0.20 × 80 = £16
> Price including VAT = £80 + £16 = **£96**
> Or: 80 × 1.20 = **£96**

Reverse VAT (find price before VAT):
> Price with VAT = £120. Rate = 20%.
> Original = 120/1.20 = **£100**

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Principal | The original amount of money invested or borrowed |
| Simple interest | Interest on the original principal only |
| Compound interest | Interest calculated on principal + previous interest |
| Depreciation | Decrease in value over time |
| Exchange rate | The value of one currency in terms of another |
| Hire purchase | Buying by paying a deposit then instalments |
| VAT | Value Added Tax — percentage added to sale price |
| Net pay | Pay after deductions (tax, etc.) |
| Gross pay | Total pay before deductions |
