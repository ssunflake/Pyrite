## Ratio

A **ratio** compares two or more quantities of the same type.

Written as a : b (read "a to b").

### Simplifying Ratios
Divide all parts by their HCF.

> [!example] Simplify 24 : 36
> HCF(24, 36) = 12 → **2 : 3**

> [!example] Simplify 1.5 : 2.5
> Multiply by 2 to clear decimals: 3 : 5 → **3 : 5**

> [!example] Simplify 1/2 : 3/4
> Multiply by 4: 2 : 3 → **2 : 3**

### Equivalent Ratios
Multiply or divide all parts by the same number.
3 : 5 = 6 : 10 = 15 : 25

### Ratio and Fractions
If a : b = 3 : 5, then:
- Fraction that is part a = 3/(3+5) = **3/8**
- Fraction that is part b = 5/8

## Dividing in a Given Ratio

> [!example] Share £240 in the ratio 3 : 5.
> Total parts = 8
> One part = 240/8 = £30
> 3 parts = £90; 5 parts = **£150**

> [!example] Share 360 g in ratio 1 : 2 : 3.
> Total parts = 6; one part = 60 g
> 60 g : 120 g : **180 g**

## Rate

A **rate** compares two quantities with **different units**.

| Rate | Units |
|------|-------|
| Speed | km/h, m/s |
| Price | £ per kg, $ per litre |
| Flow | litres per minute |
| Salary | £ per hour |

> [!example] 5 kg of apples cost £3.50. Find cost per kg.
> Rate = £3.50 / 5 = **£0.70 per kg**

---

## Direct Proportion

Two quantities are in **direct proportion** if one increases, the other increases **by the same factor**.

Symbol: y ∝ x → y = kx (k = constant of proportionality)

**Graph**: a straight line through the **origin**

> [!example] y is directly proportional to x. When x = 4, y = 10. Find y when x = 6.
> k = 10/4 = 2.5 → y = 2.5x
> y = 2.5 × 6 = **15**

### Other Direct Proportion Types

| Relationship | Equation |
|-------------|---------|
| y ∝ x | y = kx |
| y ∝ x² | y = kx² |
| y ∝ x³ | y = kx³ |
| y ∝ √x | y = k√x |

---

## Inverse Proportion

Two quantities are in **inverse proportion** if one increases, the other **decreases** by the same factor.

Symbol: y ∝ 1/x → y = k/x

**Graph**: a hyperbola (reciprocal curve)

> [!example] y is inversely proportional to x. When x = 3, y = 8. Find y when x = 6.
> k = 3 × 8 = 24 → y = 24/x
> y = 24/6 = **4**

### Other Inverse Proportion Types

| Relationship | Equation |
|-------------|---------|
| y ∝ 1/x | y = k/x |
| y ∝ 1/x² | y = k/x² |
| y ∝ 1/√x | y = k/√x |

---

## Finding k — General Method

1. Write the proportionality equation (y = kx, y = kx², etc.)
2. Substitute known values of x and y
3. Solve for k
4. Write the complete equation
5. Use it to find unknowns

> [!example] y ∝ x². When x = 3, y = 36. Find y when x = 5.
> y = kx² → 36 = k(9) → k = 4
> Equation: y = 4x²
> y = 4(25) = **100**

---

## Unitary Method

Find the value of **one unit**, then scale.

> [!example] 8 workers build a wall in 6 days. How long for 12 workers?
> This is inverse proportion (more workers → fewer days)
> 1 worker takes 6 × 8 = 48 days
> 12 workers take 48 ÷ 12 = **4 days**

> [!example] 5 identical items cost £35. Cost of 9 items?
> Direct proportion: 1 item = £7 → 9 × 7 = **£63**

---

## Map/Scale Ratio Problems

> [!example] Map scale 1 : 50 000. Two towns are 4.6 cm apart on the map.
> Real distance = 4.6 × 50 000 = 230 000 cm = **2.3 km**

---

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Ratio | Comparison of two or more quantities of the same type |
| Rate | Comparison of two quantities with different units |
| Direct proportion | y = kx; as x increases, y increases proportionally |
| Inverse proportion | y = k/x; as x increases, y decreases proportionally |
| Constant of proportionality (k) | The fixed multiplier in a proportional relationship |
| Unitary method | Finding the unit value first, then scaling |
