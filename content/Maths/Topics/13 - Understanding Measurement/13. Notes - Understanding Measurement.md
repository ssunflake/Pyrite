## Rounding

### Decimal Places (d.p.)
Count digits after the decimal point. Look at the next digit:
- If ≥ 5 → round **up**
- If < 5 → round **down** (leave as is)

> [!example] Round 3.4762 to 2 d.p.
> Look at 3rd decimal: 6 ≥ 5 → round up → **3.48**

### Significant Figures (s.f.)
Count from the **first non-zero digit**.

> [!example] Round 0.004762 to 2 s.f.
> First s.f. = 4 (the leading zeros don't count)
> 2nd s.f. = 7; next digit = 6 ≥ 5 → round up → **0.0048**

> [!example] Round 34 800 to 3 s.f.
> First 3 s.f. = 3, 4, 8; next digit = 0 < 5 → **34 800** (already 3 s.f. — but write as 3.48 × 10⁴ to make it clear)

> [!warning] Trailing zeros can be ambiguous. Use standard form or state "to 3 s.f." explicitly.

### Rounding to the Nearest…
Same principle — identify the place value, look at the next digit.
> 3470 to nearest 100: look at tens digit (7 ≥ 5) → **3500**

## Estimation

Round each value to 1 s.f. (or a convenient round number), then calculate.

> [!example] Estimate: (38.7 × 5.2) / 9.8
> ≈ (40 × 5) / 10 = 200/10 = **20**

> [!tip] Estimation is used to check calculator answers for reasonableness.

---

## Upper and Lower Bounds

When a value is rounded, the **true value** lies within a range.

If x is given as 4.7 (to 1 d.p.):
- Lower bound = 4.65
- Upper bound = 4.75

**Rule:** bounds are half a unit either side of the last stated digit.

> [!example] A length is measured as 12.3 cm (to 1 d.p.)
> Lower bound = 12.25 cm; Upper bound = 12.35 cm
> We write: **12.25 ≤ length < 12.35**

> [!tip] The upper bound uses a strict inequality < because if the value were exactly 12.35, it would round to 12.4 (to 1 d.p.).

### Bounds for Calculations

| Operation | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| Max of (a + b) | max a + max b |
| Min of (a + b) | min a + min b |
| Max of (a × b) | max a × max b |
| Min of (a × b) | min a × min b |
| Max of (a / b) | max a / min b |
| Min of (a / b) | min a / max b |
| Max of (a − b) | max a − min b |
| Min of (a − b) | min a − max b |

> [!example] a = 4.2 (1 d.p.), b = 3.1 (1 d.p.). Find max value of a/b.
> Max a = 4.25, Min b = 3.05
> Max(a/b) = 4.25/3.05 ≈ **1.393**

## Compound Measures

### Speed
$$\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}$$

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Speed | D / T |
| Distance | S × T |
| Time | D / S |

> [!example] Car travels 150 km in 2.5 hours. Speed = 150/2.5 = **60 km/h**

> [!tip] Triangle: cover what you want — D on top, S × T on bottom.

#### Units
- km/h — for cars, trains
- m/s — for science / physics
- Conversion: 1 m/s = 3.6 km/h; 1 km/h = 1/3.6 m/s

### Density
$$\text{Density} = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Volume}}$$

| Find | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Density | M / V |
| Mass | D × V |
| Volume | M / D |

> [!example] Object: mass 80 g, volume 10 cm³. Density = 80/10 = **8 g/cm³**

### Pressure
$$\text{Pressure} = \frac{\text{Force}}{\text{Area}}$$

Units: N/m² (pascals, Pa) or N/cm²

> [!example] Force 60 N on area 0.5 m². Pressure = 60/0.5 = **120 Pa**

## Time Calculations

### Reading and Converting Time
- 24-hour clock: 14:30 = 2:30 pm
- Convert hours to minutes: × 60
- Convert minutes to hours: ÷ 60

> [!example] Journey starts 09:45, ends 13:20. Duration = 13:20 − 09:45 = **3 hours 35 minutes**

> [!warning] Don't treat time like decimals!
> 3 hours 35 minutes ≠ 3.35 hours
> 3 h 35 min = 3 + 35/60 = 3.583… hours

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Decimal places | Digits after the decimal point |
| Significant figures | Digits from the first non-zero digit |
| Lower bound | Smallest value that rounds to the given value |
| Upper bound | Largest value that rounds to the given value (exclusive) |
| Compound measure | A measure involving two units (e.g. speed = distance/time) |
| Speed | Rate of change of distance; distance per unit time |
| Density | Mass per unit volume |
| Pressure | Force per unit area |
