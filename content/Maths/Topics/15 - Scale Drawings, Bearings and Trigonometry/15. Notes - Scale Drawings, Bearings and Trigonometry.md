## Scale Drawings

A scale drawing represents a real object using a **scale factor**.

**Scale** is written as a ratio, e.g. 1 : 50 000 means 1 cm on the drawing = 50 000 cm (500 m) in real life.

> [!example] Scale 1 : 200. A wall is 6 m long. Length on drawing?
> 6 m = 600 cm ‚Üí 600/200 = **3 cm**

> [!example] On a map of scale 1 : 25 000, a path is 4.5 cm long. Real length?
> 4.5 √ó 25 000 = 112 500 cm = **1.125 km**

## Bearings

A **bearing** is a direction measured:
- **Clockwise** from **North**
- Always given as **3 digits** (e.g. 045¬į, not 45¬į)

| Direction | Bearing |
|-----------|---------|
| North | 000¬į |
| East | 090¬į |
| South | 180¬į |
| West | 270¬į |

> [!example] Point B is North-East of A. Bearing = **045¬į**

### Back Bearings
If bearing from A to B is őł, then bearing from B to A = őł + 180¬į (or ‚ąí 180¬į if > 360¬į).

> [!example] Bearing A‚ÜíB = 110¬į. Bearing B‚ÜíA = 110¬į + 180¬į = **290¬į**

## Trigonometry in Right-Angled Triangles

For an angle őł in a right triangle, the three sides are:
- **Hypotenuse (H)** ‚ÄĒ opposite the right angle; longest side
- **Opposite (O)** ‚ÄĒ side opposite to őł
- **Adjacent (A)** ‚ÄĒ side next to őł (not the hypotenuse)

### SOHCAHTOA

| Ratio | Formula | Remember |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Sine | sin őł = O/H | **S**OH |
| Cosine | cos őł = A/H | **C**AH |
| Tangent | tan őł = O/A | **T**OA |

> [!tip] Mnemonic: **S**ome **O**ld **H**orses **C**an **A**lways **H**ear **T**heir **O**wners **A**pproach

### Finding a Side
> [!example] Angle őł = 35¬į, hypotenuse = 10 cm. Find opposite side.
> sin 35¬į = O/10 ‚Üí O = 10 sin 35¬į ‚Čą **5.74 cm**

### Finding an Angle
> [!example] Opposite = 7 cm, adjacent = 9 cm. Find őł.
> tan őł = 7/9 ‚Üí őł = tan‚ĀĽ¬Ļ(7/9) ‚Čą **37.9¬į**

## Angles of Elevation and Depression

- **Angle of elevation**: angle **above** the horizontal (looking up)
- **Angle of depression**: angle **below** the horizontal (looking down)

Both are measured from the **horizontal**.

> [!example] A person 20 m from a building looks up at the top at an elevation of 40¬į. Find height.
> tan 40¬į = h/20 ‚Üí h = 20 tan 40¬į ‚Čą **16.8 m**

## Sine Rule

Used for **any triangle** when you know:
- Two angles and one side (AAS or ASA), OR
- Two sides and a non-included angle (SSA ‚ÄĒ gives two possible solutions sometimes)

$$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$$

(or inverted: sin A/a = sin B/b = sin C/c ‚ÄĒ use this form when finding an angle)

> [!example] Triangle ABC: A = 40¬į, B = 75¬į, a = 8 cm. Find b.
> C = 180 ‚ąí 40 ‚ąí 75 = 65¬į
> b/sin 75¬į = 8/sin 40¬į
> b = 8 sin 75¬į / sin 40¬į ‚Čą **12.0 cm**

> [!warning] Ambiguous case (SSA): when finding an angle, there may be two solutions.
> If sin A = k, then A could be the acute angle OR 180¬į ‚ąí A (obtuse). Check if both fit the triangle.

## Cosine Rule

Used for **any triangle** when you know:
- Two sides and the **included** angle (SAS), OR
- All three sides (SSS ‚ÄĒ to find an angle)

**Finding a side:**
$$a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$$

**Finding an angle:**
$$\cos A = \frac{b^2 + c^2 - a^2}{2bc}$$

> [!example] b = 7, c = 10, A = 50¬į. Find a.
> a¬≤ = 49 + 100 ‚ąí 2(7)(10)cos 50¬į
> a¬≤ = 149 ‚ąí 140(0.6428) ‚Čą 149 ‚ąí 90.0 = 59.0
> a ‚Čą **7.68 cm**

> [!example] a = 5, b = 7, c = 8. Find angle A.
> cos A = (49 + 64 ‚ąí 25)/(2 √ó 7 √ó 8) = 88/112 ‚Čą 0.786
> A = cos‚ĀĽ¬Ļ(0.786) ‚Čą **38.2¬į**

## Area of Any Triangle

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$$

where C is the angle **between** sides a and b.

> [!example] Two sides 8 and 11 cm, included angle 65¬į.
> Area = ¬Ĺ √ó 8 √ó 11 √ó sin 65¬į ‚Čą ¬Ĺ √ó 88 √ó 0.906 ‚Čą **39.9 cm¬≤**

## Choosing the Right Rule

| Known | Use |
|-------|-----|
| Right-angled triangle | SOHCAHTOA |
| Two sides + included angle (SAS) | Cosine rule |
| Three sides (SSS) ‚ÄĒ find angle | Cosine rule |
| Two angles + one side (AAS/ASA) | Sine rule |
| Two sides + non-included angle (SSA) | Sine rule (check ambiguous case) |
