## Perimeter

Perimeter = total length of boundary of a 2D shape.

| Shape | Perimeter |
|-------|-----------|
| Rectangle | 2(l + w) |
| Square | 4s |
| Triangle | a + b + c |
| Circle (circumference) | 2πr = πd |
| Semicircle | πr + 2r |
| Any polygon | Sum of all sides |

> [!example] A semicircle has diameter 10 cm. Find perimeter.
> r = 5 cm
> Perimeter = π(5) + 2(5) = 5π + 10 ≈ **25.7 cm**

## Area of 2D Shapes

| Shape | Formula | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Rectangle | A = lw | |
| Square | A = s² | |
| Triangle | A = ½bh | h = perpendicular height |
| Parallelogram | A = bh | h = perpendicular height |
| Trapezium | A = ½(a + b)h | a, b = parallel sides |
| Circle | A = πr² | |
| Sector | A = (θ/360) × πr² | θ in degrees |
| Kite | A = ½d₁d₂ | d₁, d₂ = diagonals |
| Rhombus | A = ½d₁d₂ | same as kite |

> [!example] Find area of trapezium with parallel sides 8 cm and 5 cm, height 4 cm.
> A = ½(8 + 5) × 4 = ½ × 13 × 4 = **26 cm²**

### Arc Length and Sector Area

For a sector with radius r and angle θ°:

**Arc length** = (θ/360) × 2πr

**Sector area** = (θ/360) × πr²

> [!example] Sector: r = 6 cm, θ = 120°
> Arc length = (120/360) × 2π(6) = ⅓ × 12π = **4π ≈ 12.6 cm**
> Sector area = (120/360) × π(36) = ⅓ × 36π = **12π ≈ 37.7 cm²**

### Segment Area
Area of segment = Area of sector − Area of triangle

> [!tip] For the triangle in a sector, use:
> Area of triangle = ½r²sin(θ)

## Surface Area of 3D Solids

| Solid | Surface Area |
|-------|-------------|
| Cuboid | 2(lw + lh + wh) |
| Cube | 6s² |
| Cylinder | 2πr² + 2πrh (curved + two circles) |
| Curved surface of cylinder | 2πrh |
| Sphere | 4πr² |
| Hemisphere | 3πr² (curved = 2πr² + flat circle = πr²) |
| Cone (total) | πrl + πr² (curved + base) |
| Curved surface of cone | πrl (l = slant height) |
| Pyramid (square base) | base area + 4 × triangular face area |

> [!warning] Slant Height vs Perpendicular Height
> For a cone: slant height l = √(r² + h²) using Pythagoras.
> Always check which height is given.

> [!example] Total surface area of a cone: r = 3 cm, l = 5 cm
> TSA = πrl + πr² = π(3)(5) + π(3²) = 15π + 9π = **24π ≈ 75.4 cm²**

## Volume of 3D Solids

| Solid | Volume |
|-------|--------|
| Cuboid | V = lwh |
| Cube | V = s³ |
| Prism (any cross-section) | V = area of cross-section × length |
| Cylinder | V = πr²h |
| Pyramid | V = ⅓ × base area × h |
| Cone | V = ⅓πr²h |
| Sphere | V = (4/3)πr³ |
| Hemisphere | V = (2/3)πr³ |

> [!important] Prism Rule
> Any solid with a **uniform cross-section** is a prism.
> Volume = cross-sectional area × length
> This covers: cylinder, triangular prism, trapezoidal prism, hexagonal prism, etc.

> [!example] Triangular prism: triangle base 6 cm, height 4 cm, length 10 cm
> Cross-section area = ½ × 6 × 4 = 12 cm²
> Volume = 12 × 10 = **120 cm³**

> [!example] Sphere: r = 3 cm
> V = (4/3)π(3)³ = (4/3)π(27) = 36π ≈ **113.1 cm³**

## Compound Shapes

Decompose into simpler shapes. Add or subtract areas/volumes as needed.

> [!example] L-shaped region: 10×8 minus 4×3 cut-out
> Area = 10×8 − 4×3 = 80 − 12 = **68 cm²**

## Units and Conversions

| Length | Area | Volume |
|--------|------|--------|
| 1 cm = 10 mm | 1 cm² = 100 mm² | 1 cm³ = 1000 mm³ |
| 1 m = 100 cm | 1 m² = 10 000 cm² | 1 m³ = 1 000 000 cm³ |
| 1 km = 1000 m | 1 km² = 1 000 000 m² | 1 litre = 1000 cm³ |

> [!tip] Area units: multiply length conversion factor²
> e.g. 1 m = 100 cm → 1 m² = 100² cm² = 10 000 cm²

---

## Similar Shapes — Scale Factor

If two shapes are similar with linear scale factor k:

| Measurement | Scale factor |
|-------------|-------------|
| Length / Perimeter | k |
| Area | k² |
| Volume | k³ |

> [!example] Two similar cylinders: height ratio 2:3 (k = 2/3)
> Volume ratio = (2/3)³ = 8/27
> If larger cylinder volume = 270 cm³, smaller = 270 × 8/27 = **80 cm³**

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Perimeter | Total boundary length of a 2D shape |
| Area | Amount of 2D space enclosed |
| Surface area | Total area of all faces of a 3D solid |
| Volume | Amount of 3D space enclosed |
| Prism | Solid with uniform (constant) cross-section |
| Pyramid | Solid with a polygon base and triangular faces meeting at an apex |
| Slant height | Distance from apex to midpoint of base edge (cones/pyramids) |
| Sector | Region bounded by two radii and an arc |
| Segment | Region bounded by a chord and an arc |
| Arc | Part of a circle's circumference |
