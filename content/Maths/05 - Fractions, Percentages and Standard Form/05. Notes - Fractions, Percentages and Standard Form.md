## Fractions

### Equivalent Fractions
Multiply or divide numerator and denominator by the same number.
3/4 = 6/8 = 9/12 = 75/100

### Simplifying (Lowest Terms)
Divide top and bottom by their HCF.
> 18/24: HCF(18, 24) = 6 → **3/4**

### Comparing Fractions
Convert to a common denominator, then compare numerators.

### Adding and Subtracting
1. Find the LCM of the denominators (common denominator)
2. Rewrite both fractions with the common denominator
3. Add/subtract numerators; keep denominator

> [!example] 2/3 + 3/4
> LCM(3,4) = 12
> = 8/12 + 9/12 = **17/12 = 1⁵/₁₂**

### Multiplying
Multiply numerator × numerator and denominator × denominator. Cancel diagonally if possible first.

> [!example] 3/5 × 10/9
> Cancel: 3 and 9 → divide by 3; 10 and 5 → divide by 5
> = (1/1) × (2/3) = **2/3**

### Dividing
Flip (reciprocal) the second fraction, then multiply.

> [!example] 4/7 ÷ 2/3
> = 4/7 × 3/2 = 12/14 = **6/7**

### Mixed Numbers ↔ Improper Fractions
- Mixed to improper: multiply whole number by denominator, add numerator
- 2¾ = (2×4 + 3)/4 = **11/4**
- Improper to mixed: divide; quotient is whole part, remainder is numerator

## Fractions ↔ Decimals ↔ Percentages

| Fraction | Decimal | Percentage |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| 1/2 | 0.5 | 50% |
| 1/4 | 0.25 | 25% |
| 3/4 | 0.75 | 75% |
| 1/3 | 0.333… | 33.3…% |
| 2/3 | 0.666… | 66.7% |
| 1/5 | 0.2 | 20% |
| 1/8 | 0.125 | 12.5% |
| 1/10 | 0.1 | 10% |

**Fraction → Decimal**: divide numerator by denominator
**Decimal → Percentage**: multiply by 100
**Percentage → Decimal**: divide by 100

### Recurring Decimals
A recurring decimal can always be written as a fraction.
> [!example] Convert 0.̄3̄6̄ (0.363636…) to a fraction
> Let x = 0.363636…
> 100x = 36.363636…
> 100x − x = 36
> 99x = 36
> x = 36/99 = **4/11**

## Percentages

### Finding a Percentage of an Amount
% of amount = (percentage / 100) × amount

> [!example] 35% of 240 = (35/100) × 240 = **84**

### Percentage Increase / Decrease
New value = original × multiplier

| Change | Multiplier |
|--------|-----------|
| 20% increase | × 1.20 |
| 15% decrease | × 0.85 |
| 7.5% increase | × 1.075 |
| n% increase | × (1 + n/100) |
| n% decrease | × (1 − n/100) |

> [!example] Price of £120 increased by 15%:
> 120 × 1.15 = **£138**

### Percentage Change Formula
% change = [(new − original) / original] × 100

> [!example] Price changes from $80 to $92:
> % change = [(92−80)/80] × 100 = (12/80) × 100 = **15% increase**

### Reverse Percentage (Finding the Original)
If a value after a % change is known, divide by the multiplier.

> [!example] After a 20% increase, price is £96. Find original.
> Original = 96 ÷ 1.20 = **£80**

> [!warning] Common Mistake
> Do NOT subtract the percentage from the new value — always divide by the multiplier!
> 96 − 20% of 96 ≠ original price (that would give £76.80, which is wrong)

---

## Compound Percentages (repeated change)
Apply the multiplier multiple times.

> [!example] £1000 invested at 5% compound interest for 3 years:
> 1000 × 1.05³ = 1000 × 1.157625 = **£1157.63**

## Standard Form (Scientific Notation)

A number in standard form is written as:

**A × 10ⁿ** where **1 ≤ A < 10** and n is an integer.

> [!example] Write 4,500,000 in standard form:
> Move decimal 6 places left → A = 4.5, n = 6
> = **4.5 × 10⁶**

> [!example] Write 0.00072 in standard form:
> Move decimal 4 places right → A = 7.2, n = −4
> = **7.2 × 10⁻⁴**

### Converting Back
- Positive n: move decimal right (large number)
- Negative n: move decimal left (small number)

### Calculating with Standard Form
> [!example] (3 × 10⁴) × (2 × 10³)
> = (3 × 2) × (10⁴ × 10³) = **6 × 10⁷**

> [!example] (6 × 10⁵) ÷ (4 × 10²)
> = (6/4) × 10⁵⁻² = 1.5 × 10³

> [!example] (2.5 × 10³) + (3 × 10²)
> = 2500 + 300 = 2800 = **2.8 × 10³**
> (convert to same power before adding)

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Numerator | Top number of a fraction |
| Denominator | Bottom number of a fraction |
| Improper fraction | Numerator > denominator (e.g. 7/3) |
| Mixed number | Whole number + fraction (e.g. 2⅓) |
| Reciprocal | 1 divided by the number; flip the fraction |
| Percentage | A fraction out of 100 |
| Multiplier | The factor used in percentage calculations |
| Reverse percentage | Finding the original value before a % change |
| Recurring decimal | A decimal with repeating digit(s) |
| Standard form | A × 10ⁿ where 1 ≤ A < 10 |
| Significant figures | The meaningful digits in a number |
