## Line Symmetry (Reflection Symmetry)

A shape has **line symmetry** if a line (the **line of symmetry** or **axis of symmetry**) divides it into two mirror-image halves.

> [!tip] Fold the shape along the line — if both halves match perfectly, it is a line of symmetry.

### Number of Lines of Symmetry

| Shape | Lines of symmetry |
|-------|-----------------|
| Equilateral triangle | 3 |
| Isosceles triangle | 1 |
| Scalene triangle | 0 |
| Square | 4 |
| Rectangle | 2 |
| Rhombus | 2 |
| Parallelogram | 0 |
| Kite | 1 |
| Trapezium | 0 |
| Isosceles trapezium | 1 |
| Regular pentagon | 5 |
| Regular hexagon | 6 |
| Regular n-gon | n |
| Circle | Infinitely many |

## Rotational Symmetry

A shape has **rotational symmetry** of order n if it maps onto itself **n times** in a full 360° rotation.

- Every shape has rotational symmetry of at least order 1 (itself at 360°)
- We only say a shape has rotational symmetry if order ≥ 2

### Angle of Rotation
Minimum angle = 360° / order

### Order of Rotational Symmetry

| Shape | Order |
|-------|-------|
| Equilateral triangle | 3 |
| Square | 4 |
| Rectangle | 2 |
| Rhombus | 2 |
| Parallelogram | 2 |
| Kite | 1 |
| Regular n-gon | n |
| Circle | Infinite |

> [!example] A regular hexagon: order 6, minimum rotation angle = 60°

---

## Symmetry in Regular Polygons

A regular n-gon has:
- **n lines of symmetry**
- **Rotational symmetry of order n**

---

## Planes of Symmetry (3D)

A **plane of symmetry** divides a 3D solid into two mirror-image halves.

| Solid | Planes of symmetry |
|-------|--------------------|
| Cube | 9 |
| Cuboid | 3 |
| Cylinder | Infinite (any plane through axis + 1 horizontal) |
| Sphere | Infinite |
| Square-based pyramid | 4 |
| Regular tetrahedron | 6 |
| Cone | Infinite (any plane through apex and centre) |

---

## Symmetry Properties of Circles

- A circle has **infinite** lines of symmetry (every diameter)
- Any chord bisected perpendicularly passes through the centre
- Equal chords are equidistant from the centre
- The perpendicular from the centre to a chord **bisects** the chord

| Square | 4 | 4 | Equal, bisect at 90° |

| Parallelogram | 0 | 2 | Unequal, bisect each other |
| Kite | 1 | 1 | One bisects the other at 90° |
| Isosceles trap. | 1 | 1 | Equal length |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Line of symmetry | A line dividing a shape into two congruent mirror halves |
| Rotational symmetry | Property of mapping onto itself under rotation |
| Order of rotational symmetry | Number of times a shape coincides with itself in 360° |
| Centre of rotation | The fixed point about which rotation occurs |
| Plane of symmetry | A flat surface dividing a 3D solid into two mirror halves |
