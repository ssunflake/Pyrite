## The Probability Scale

Probability is always between 0 and 1 (inclusive):

```
0 ────────────────────────────── 1
Impossible  Unlikely  Evens  Likely  Certain
```

- P = 0 → **impossible**
- P = 0.5 = ½ → **equally likely**
- P = 1 → **certain**

---

## Calculating Probability (Equally Likely Outcomes)

$$P(event) = \frac{\text{number of favourable outcomes}}{\text{total number of possible outcomes}}$$

> [!example] A bag has 3 red, 5 blue, and 2 green balls. P(blue) = 5/10 = **1/2**

> [!example] A fair die is rolled. P(prime number) = P({2,3,5}) = **3/6 = 1/2**

---

## Complementary Events

P(event does NOT happen) = 1 − P(event happens)

$$P(A') = 1 - P(A)$$

> [!example] P(rain) = 0.35 → P(no rain) = 1 − 0.35 = **0.65**

---

## Combined Events

### AND Rule (Multiplication) — Independent Events
Two events are **independent** if the outcome of one doesn't affect the other.

$$P(A \text{ and } B) = P(A) \times P(B)$$

> [!example] Flip a coin and roll a die. P(heads AND 6) = ½ × 1/6 = **1/12**

> [!warning] This rule ONLY applies when events are independent. For dependent events, use conditional probability (→ Ch 24).

### OR Rule (Addition) — Mutually Exclusive Events
Two events are **mutually exclusive** if they cannot happen at the same time.

$$P(A \text{ or } B) = P(A) + P(B)$$

> [!example] Roll a die. P(1 or 6) = 1/6 + 1/6 = **2/6 = 1/3**

### OR Rule — Not Mutually Exclusive (General Addition)
$$P(A \text{ or } B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \text{ and } B)$$

> [!example] From a pack of 52 cards: P(King or Heart)
> P(King) = 4/52, P(Heart) = 13/52, P(King of Hearts) = 1/52
> = 4/52 + 13/52 − 1/52 = **16/52 = 4/13**

## Listing Sample Spaces

A **sample space** is the set of all possible outcomes.

### Two-Way Tables / Sample Space Diagrams

> [!example] Roll two dice. Show sums in a table.
>
> | + | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | **1** | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |
> | **2** | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 |
> | **3** | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 | 9 |
> | **4** | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 | 9 | 10 |
> | **5** | 6 | 7 | 8 | 9 | 10 | 11 |
> | **6** | 7 | 8 | 9 | 10 | 11 | 12 |
>
> Total outcomes: 36
> P(sum = 7) = 6/36 = **1/6**

### Listing Systematically
For small sample spaces, list all outcomes:
> Toss 3 coins: {HHH, HHT, HTH, HTT, THH, THT, TTH, TTT} — 8 outcomes

## Experimental Probability (Relative Frequency)

When outcomes are **not equally likely**, or an experiment is conducted:

$$P(event) \approx \frac{\text{frequency of event}}{\text{total number of trials}}$$

As the number of trials increases, experimental probability approaches **theoretical probability** (Law of Large Numbers).

> [!example] A biased die is rolled 200 times. It lands on 6 exactly 50 times.
> P(6) ≈ 50/200 = **1/4** (not 1/6 — the die is biased)

---

## Expected Frequency

If the probability of an event is p and the experiment is done n times:

**Expected frequency = n × p**

> [!example] P(win) = 0.3, play 200 games: expected wins = 200 × 0.3 = **60**

---

## Venn Diagrams (Basic)

Used to show relationships between events.

- A ∩ B = A **and** B (intersection)
- A ∪ B = A **or** B (union)
- A' = **not** A (complement)

> [!example] 30 students: 18 study French (F), 14 study Spanish (S), 5 study both.
> P(F ∩ S) = 5/30 = 1/6
> P(F ∪ S) = (18+14−5)/30 = 27/30 = **9/10**
> P(neither) = 3/30 = **1/10**

---

## Mutually Exclusive vs Independent

| | Mutually Exclusive | Independent |
|-|--------------------|-------------|
| Can both happen? | No | Yes |
| If A happens, B can? | Never | Still possible (unaffected) |
| P(A and B) = | 0 | P(A) × P(B) |
| P(A or B) = | P(A) + P(B) | P(A)+P(B)−P(A)×P(B) |

---

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Outcome | A possible result of an experiment |
| Event | A set of one or more outcomes |
| Sample space | The set of all possible outcomes |
| Equally likely outcomes | All outcomes have the same probability |
| Mutually exclusive | Two events that cannot occur simultaneously |
| Independent events | Outcome of one does not affect the other |
| Complementary event | The event NOT occurring; P(A') = 1 − P(A) |
| Experimental probability | Probability estimated from actual trials |
| Theoretical probability | Probability calculated from known outcomes |
| Expected frequency | n × p — predicted number of occurrences |
