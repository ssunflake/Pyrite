## Scale Drawings

A scale drawing represents a real object using a **scale factor**.

**Scale** is written as a ratio, e.g. 1 : 50 000 means 1 cm on the drawing = 50 000 cm (500 m) in real life.

> [!example] Scale 1 : 200. A wall is 6 m long. Length on drawing?
> 6 m = 600 cm → 600/200 = **3 cm**

> [!example] On a map of scale 1 : 25 000, a path is 4.5 cm long. Real length?
> 4.5 × 25 000 = 112 500 cm = **1.125 km**

## Bearings

A **bearing** is a direction measured:
- **Clockwise** from **North**
- Always given as **3 digits** (e.g. 045°, not 45°)

| Direction | Bearing |
|-----------|---------|
| North | 000° |
| East | 090° |
| South | 180° |
| West | 270° |

> [!example] Point B is North-East of A. Bearing = **045°**

### Back Bearings
If bearing from A to B is θ, then bearing from B to A = θ + 180° (or − 180° if > 360°).

> [!example] Bearing A→B = 110°. Bearing B→A = 110° + 180° = **290°**

## Trigonometry in Right-Angled Triangles

For an angle θ in a right triangle, the three sides are:
- **Hypotenuse (H)** — opposite the right angle; longest side
- **Opposite (O)** — side opposite to θ
- **Adjacent (A)** — side next to θ (not the hypotenuse)

### SOHCAHTOA

| Ratio | Formula | Remember |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Sine | sin θ = O/H | **S**OH |
| Cosine | cos θ = A/H | **C**AH |
| Tangent | tan θ = O/A | **T**OA |

> [!tip] Mnemonic: **S**ome **O**ld **H**orses **C**an **A**lways **H**ear **T**heir **O**wners **A**pproach

### Finding a Side
> [!example] Angle θ = 35°, hypotenuse = 10 cm. Find opposite side.
> sin 35° = O/10 → O = 10 sin 35° ≈ **5.74 cm**

### Finding an Angle
> [!example] Opposite = 7 cm, adjacent = 9 cm. Find θ.
> tan θ = 7/9 → θ = tan⁻¹(7/9) ≈ **37.9°**

| 45° | √2/2 | √2/2 | 1 |
| 60° | √3/2 | 1/2 | √3 |
| 90° | 1 | 0 | undefined |

## Angles of Elevation and Depression

- **Angle of elevation**: angle **above** the horizontal (looking up)
- **Angle of depression**: angle **below** the horizontal (looking down)

Both are measured from the **horizontal**.

> [!example] A person 20 m from a building looks up at the top at an elevation of 40°. Find height.
> tan 40° = h/20 → h = 20 tan 40° ≈ **16.8 m**

---

## Sine Rule

Used for **any triangle** when you know:
- Two angles and one side (AAS or ASA), OR
- Two sides and a non-included angle (SSA — gives two possible solutions sometimes)

$$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$$

(or inverted: sin A/a = sin B/b = sin C/c — use this form when finding an angle)

> [!example] Triangle ABC: A = 40°, B = 75°, a = 8 cm. Find b.
> C = 180 − 40 − 75 = 65°
> b/sin 75° = 8/sin 40°
> b = 8 sin 75° / sin 40° ≈ **12.0 cm**

> [!warning] Ambiguous case (SSA): when finding an angle, there may be two solutions.
> If sin A = k, then A could be the acute angle OR 180° − A (obtuse). Check if both fit the triangle.

---

## Cosine Rule

Used for **any triangle** when you know:
- Two sides and the **included** angle (SAS), OR
- All three sides (SSS — to find an angle)

**Finding a side:**
$$a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$$

**Finding an angle:**
$$\cos A = \frac{b^2 + c^2 - a^2}{2bc}$$

> [!example] b = 7, c = 10, A = 50°. Find a.
> a² = 49 + 100 − 2(7)(10)cos 50°
> a² = 149 − 140(0.6428) ≈ 149 − 90.0 = 59.0
> a ≈ **7.68 cm**

> [!example] a = 5, b = 7, c = 8. Find angle A.
> cos A = (49 + 64 − 25)/(2 × 7 × 8) = 88/112 ≈ 0.786
> A = cos⁻¹(0.786) ≈ **38.2°**

## Area of Any Triangle

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$$

where C is the angle **between** sides a and b.

> [!example] Two sides 8 and 11 cm, included angle 65°.
> Area = ½ × 8 × 11 × sin 65° ≈ ½ × 88 × 0.906 ≈ **39.9 cm²**

---

## Choosing the Right Rule

| Known | Use |
|-------|-----|
| Right-angled triangle | SOHCAHTOA |
| Two sides + included angle (SAS) | Cosine rule |
| Three sides (SSS) — find angle | Cosine rule |
| Two angles + one side (AAS/ASA) | Sine rule |
| Two sides + non-included angle (SSA) | Sine rule (check ambiguous case) |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Scale drawing | A proportional drawing with a stated ratio |
| Bearing | Direction measured clockwise from North, 3 digits |
| Angle of elevation | Angle above horizontal looking up |
| Angle of depression | Angle below horizontal looking down |
| SOHCAHTOA | Mnemonics for sin, cos, tan in right triangles |
| Sine rule | a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C |
| Cosine rule | a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos A |
| Ambiguous case | Two possible triangles when using SSA with sine rule |
